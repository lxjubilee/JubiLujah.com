## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to the meaning & the gospel** — theology must survive intact (the cross, the resurrection, the name of Jesus, repentance, the King's return). Never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels fit the melody. In Vietnamese also weigh **tone-vs-melody** (see §4): a line a native cannot sing comfortably — or that a sung melody renders ambiguous — has failed even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Vietnamese** — it must sound like a Vietnamese believer wrote it, never like translated English; no calques, no English word order; uses the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture; swap an image only if untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook** — Vietnamese rhyme (vần, often vowel-rhyme) only with real, sensible words; ALWAYS keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** Every line must make complete, natural sense to a Vietnamese native *with the rhyme ignored*. Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement. Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar, or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT.** Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line: delete the final rhyme word — does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?

> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** Adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Vietnamese culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable, and **never import folk-religion / ancestor-veneration / Buddhist concepts** (thờ cúng tổ tiên, nghiệp/karma, ông Trời as fate, deities) to "fill" a Christian slot. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

1. **No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler.** Vietnamese padding to avoid: empty rhythm/rhyme tails dropped only to fit a vần — stray final particles **mà / thôi / nha / nhé / đây / kìa / ơi** at a reverent line-end; meaningless reduplicatives jammed in for sound. **Test:** cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no real meaning, cut it and recast.
2. **Only REAL Vietnamese words & correct grammar.** Vietnamese is uninflected, but watch: wrong **classifier** (con/cái/người/đấng); misplaced **tense/aspect markers** (đã/đang/sẽ) — don't drop the one that carries the meaning; broken **serial-verb / topic-comment** order; do not fuse syllables into a non-word to save a beat. Negation **không/chẳng** stays before the verb.
3. **Correct set phrases — no English calques.** Don't calque "fall in love with God" as a romantic idiom; "give your heart to Jesus" → established **dâng đời sống cho Chúa / tin nhận Chúa Giê-xu**, not a literal *cho trái tim*. "Lift high" → **tôn cao / tôn vinh**, not literal *nâng cao lên*. Don't calque the English "the reason that…" structure (use **vì / bởi vì / cho nên**). Don't calque "make a difference," "reach out," "let go" word-for-word.
4. **No English-calque or tech/telephone images.** "On the line to heaven," "signal/connection to God," "download grace," "recharge my soul" misfire; render the relationship plainly (**thưa cùng Ngài / kêu cầu Ngài**).
5. **Loanwords/register that misread (see §2E below).** Watch loans/register flips: **fan** (supporter) reads as English slang in worship — use **người thờ phượng / con dân Chúa**; **"yêu"** alone (romantic love) for love of God can read romantic — prefer **kính yêu / yêu mến Chúa**; northern/southern dialect words that read wrong region (e.g., overly colloquial Saigon slang in a solemn line). Never drop street-slang (**vãi, đỉnh, xịn**) into a reverent line.
6. **(Vietnamese is uninflected — gender/number agreement N/A.)** Instead: keep **pronoun/kinship address consistent** — don't switch between **Ngài / Chúa / Cha** registers for God carelessly, and keep the worshipper's self-pronoun consistent (**con** is the reverent self-reference to God).
7. **No grammatically stranded tails** — every **của (of)** and **mà (rel.)** must resolve to a head; don't leave a dangling modifier.
8. **Imagery internally consistent** — no contradicting/antecedent-less image (don't pair "cold floor" with a sudden "blaze"; don't introduce an object with no antecedent).
9. **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word distorts doctrine; **never let "fate/luck/karma" (số phận / may mắn / nghiệp / duyên)** stand as the source of blessing — grace comes from **Chúa**. Keep luck/chance subordinate to God's sovereignty. Don't let **ông Trời** (folk "Heaven/fate") replace the personal God.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** delete the final rhyme; if the rest still says something true and natural and the dropped word is one you'd actually use, keep it — otherwise rewrite meaning-first, rhyme or no rhyme.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Vietnam is majority folk-religion/Buddhist with strong ancestor veneration; **Christianity is a significant minority — Roman Catholic (large, ~7%, deeply rooted since the 17th c.) and Protestant/Evangelical (Tin Lành, fast-growing, incl. ECVN and house churches, strong among ethnic-minority highland communities)**. The Vietnamese version must **hold for the whole Vietnamese Body of Christ** while staying unmistakably Christian.

- **Shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary all traditions own:** *Chúa, Đức Chúa Trời / Thiên Chúa, Chúa Giê-xu / Giê-su, Đấng Christ / Kitô, Đức Thánh Linh / Chúa Thánh Thần, ân điển / ân sủng, sự cứu rỗi, thập tự giá / thánh giá, sự sống lại / phục sinh, A-men, Ha-lê-lu-gia.* Anchor Scripture echoes to the **Kinh Thánh** — the **Bản Truyền Thống (1926, Phan Khôi)** is the most broadly recognized Protestant text; the **Bản Hiệu Đính (Revised, 2010)** is the modern standard; Catholics use the **Bản dịch CGKPV**. John 19:30 → **"Mọi việc đã được trọn"**; Rev 22:20 → **"Lạy Đức Chúa Jêsus, xin hãy đến!"**
- **No tradition-specific imports the source lacks** — for an evangelical default, no Marian/saint devotion, no one-church liturgical formulas, no glossolalia naming. Christ-centered, Bible-plain — the common ground. (Where a Catholic edition is wanted, switch the divine-name set deliberately and document it; do not mix Protestant/Catholic terms within one album.)
- **Reverent register toward God.** Vietnamese reverence is **grammatical via pronouns/kinship**: address God as **Ngài** (reverent "He/You"), **Chúa**, **Cha** (Father); the worshipper refers to self as **con** (child) before God — never as an equal/peer pronoun. Capitalize divine pronouns (**Ngài, Chúa, Cha, Đấng**). No vulgar/street-slang anywhere.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (**cô dâu / chàng rể**, or **Tân Lang / Tân Phụ** in church usage) is biblical — keep it pure, never romantic-pop longing. Don't call God a romantic **người yêu**.
- **Gender/inclusive language & family:** defer to the Vietnamese church's plain, Scripture-anchored norms; family/filial honor is a strength to lean on, not a debate to import.

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP  (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame — lead with HONOR–SHAME + FEAR–POWER.** Vietnamese culture is built on **face (thể diện), filial piety (hiếu thảo), family belonging**, with a folk substratum of fear of fate/spirits/ancestors. Foreground: **the Father who welcomes the prodigal home and restores honor/belonging (về nhà với Cha, đoàn tụ); adoption into God's family (làm con cái Đức Chúa Trời); Christ's authority and victory over every dark power and over fear of fate/death (đắc thắng, ánh sáng chiếu vào bóng tối); freedom from striving for merit/luck.** Legal/courtroom (guilt-innocence) is secondary — keep it but don't make it the emotional entry.
- **Taboo/danger images → safe substitutes:**
  - *Folk/ancestral/Buddhist concepts* — never use **nghiệp (karma), duyên/số (fate), ông Trời (folk Heaven-as-fate), thắp hương/thờ cúng (incense/ancestor worship), Niết Bàn (nirvana)** approvingly for Christian truth. "Blessing" = **phước / phước lành**; "eternal life" = **sự sống đời đời**.
  - *"Buddy/homeboy Jesus"* — Vietnamese reverence forbids matey God-talk; keep **Ngài/Chúa**, never a peer-pronoun "bạn Giê-xu."
  - *Dancing before the Lord* — ecstatic public dance reads odd in restrained Viet worship; prefer **vui mừng / reo mừng / nhảy mừng (leap for joy)**; if you keep "dance," frame it as **joy before God** and SAY you did.
  - *Fire* — distinguish redemptive **lửa Thánh Linh** from judgment fire; bare *lửa* can read literal.
  - *Ancestor/family imagery* — leverage filial love toward **the Father**, but never blur into ancestor veneration.
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom:** **khao khát** (deep thirst/yearning) and **nhớ nhung / mong mỏi** (aching longing); **nỗi niềm** (deep inner feeling). Use **khao khát** for sacred longing for God. The felt-need to resolve is **bình an** (deep peace) and **được về nhà** (coming home).
- **Worship temperature: warm-reverent.** Vietnamese worship is heartfelt and tender, joyful but not brash; sincerity and warmth over spectacle.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** **political/national** terms — avoid Party/State slogans, "cách mạng," "giải phóng," "đồng chí," red/flag imagery, and anything that reads as a political anthem; **war/conquering** imagery is sensitive given recent history — frame "victory" spiritually (đắc thắng thuộc linh), never militarily/nationalistically. "Kingdom/King" (**Vua, Nước Trời**) is fine for Christ's majesty. Be neutral on North/South dialect to stay nationally readable.
- **Reverence/honorific forms required when addressing God:** pronoun **Ngài** (reverent You/He), titles **Chúa / Đức Chúa Trời / Cha**, worshipper self = **con**; reverent verbs **ngự (dwells/reigns), phán (speaks), ban (gives/grants), đến/ngự đến (comes)**. Keep one consistent set per song.
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** Vietnamese **lục bát** (6–8 syllable couplet) and **song thất lục bát** rhythms, and **vần (vowel-rhyme, often internal/linking)** — reach for flowing vowel-rhyme and balanced phrasing rather than hard English AABB; tonal **bằng/trắc** (level vs sharp tone) balance gives Vietnamese verse its music.

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY ( Vietnamese-specific )

- **TONE-vs-MELODY caveat (CRITICAL — Vietnamese has 6 tones, marked by diacritics: ngang/level (a), huyền/falling (à), sắc/rising (á), hỏi/dipping (ả), ngã/glottal-rising (ã), nặng/heavy (ạ).** Tone distinguishes words (**ma/má/mà/mả/mã/mạ** are six different words). A sung melody **flattens or overrides lexical tone**, creating real ambiguity. Mitigation: (1) **DIACRITICS ARE MANDATORY in the written text** — Suno/TTS pronounce the tone from the mark; an unmarked vowel mis-sings or means a different word (write **Chúa**, never *Chua*; **sống lại**, never *song lai*); (2) **choose words whose meaning stays clear from context even when the tune flattens the tone**; (3) avoid placing tone-minimal-pairs where mishearing flips meaning, especially near sacred words (make sure a flattened **Chúa / thập tự / cứu** can't be misheard as something profane/comic); (4) Vietnamese song tradition accepts melody-over-tone, but composers traditionally match **bằng (level: ngang, huyền) vs trắc (sharp: the other four)** tones to melodic contour — favor **bằng** tones on long sustained notes for natural, intelligible singing.
- **Match syllable count.** Vietnamese is monosyllabic and usually fits English tightly; compress by dropping optional words, not by inventing them. Natural reductions singers use: drop optional **thì/là/mà**, classifier elision in song, **của → 's** by juxtaposition. No filler particles to pad a bar (§0A.1).
- **Stress/rhythm:** Vietnamese has light stress; rhythm rides **tone contour and vowel length**. Put **open, bằng-tone syllables on sustained/belted notes** (e.g., **-a, -ơi, -ua, -ương**: *Chúa, Cha ơi, vinh quang, Ha-lê-lu-gia*); avoid landing a long held note on a heavy **nặng (ạ)** or a closed clipped syllable.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot;** the chorus arrives on the same bar.
- **Rhyme is OPTIONAL and last; DON'T FORCE end-rhyme.** Vietnamese uses **vần** (vowel-rhyme, frequently linking line-end to mid-line of the next, as in lục bát) — forcing English-style hard end-rhyme produces filler. Prefer recasting the OTHER line; reach for flowing vowel-rhyme where it fits naturally.
- **Diacritics/script mandatory** — both **tone marks** (à á ả ã ạ) and **vowel diacritics** (ă â ê ô ơ ư đ) must be complete in the sung body AND in titles. Missing marks change the word and break the vocal.
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing the line aloud as a flattened AI vocal would and check that blurred/toneless syllables don't form a vulgar or comic word (Vietnamese has many tone-pair traps where dropping a tone yields a crude word) — recast if so.

## 5. IDIOMS, IMAGERY & CULTURAL FIT

- **Translate the sense, not the words** → native Vietnamese idioms. "I don't need permission" → **không cần ai cho phép**; "the bell on Sunday morning" → **tiếng chuông Chúa nhật** (church bells are real for Viet Catholics/Protestants).
- **Keep concrete sensory anchors** (bathroom floor at 3 AM, cage door, wedding feast), rendered vividly in Vietnamese. **Localize culture-bound American daily-life anchors** (carpool line, diner, prom, credit-card debt) to emotionally equivalent Vietnamese scenes (**kẹt xe buổi sáng / mâm cơm gia đình / gánh nợ**) — but **do NOT localize away the global/heaven imagery** (every-tribe, throne, Lamb, wedding feast stay).
- Lean hard on **family belonging and coming home to the Father** — the honor-shame prodigal image lands powerfully (**về nhà với Cha, Cha chạy ra ôm con**).

## 9. WORKED EXAMPLE (chorus)

**EN (Jubilujah, T1 chorus):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus is the reason we are dancing now! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / The King is coming back — somehow, somehow!

**Vietnamese (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, worldview-frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Giê-xu là lý do hôm nay con nhảy mừng! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Vua muôn vua sẽ trở lại — chắc chắn, chắc rồi!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim and on the same beat; **Giê-xu** consistent; "the reason we are dancing now" → **lý do hôm nay con nhảy mừng** ("the reason today I/we leap for joy") — keeps the joy and uses **nhảy mừng (leap for joy)**, a reverent-acceptable celebration verb rather than club-style "dancing"; worshipper self = **con** (humble); "the King is coming back" → **Vua muôn vua sẽ trở lại** (King of kings, future marker **sẽ**); diacritics complete; line-ends on open bằng-leaning vowels (**mừng / rồi**); no folk-religion term; warm-reverent throughout.

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0A):**
- ❌ Forced/wrong-frame: *"…Giê-xu cho ta **số** may, **duyên** lành nhé!"* — uses **số/duyên (fate)** + folk luck + cutesy particle **nhé**: wrong theology (blessing reduced to fate/luck), wrong register, filler particle.
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"…Giê-xu ban cho con **ân điển**, **phước** tuôn tràn!"* — **ban (reverent "grants") ân điển (grace, not luck) + phước (blessing)**, reverent, real words, true doctrine; rhyme dropped in favor of meaning. **A true clear line beats a forced one.**
