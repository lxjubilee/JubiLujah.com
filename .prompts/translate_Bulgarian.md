## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to meaning & the gospel** — the cross, the resurrection, the name of Исус, repentance, the King's return must survive intact. Never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels must fit the original melody. A line a native can't sing comfortably has failed, even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Bulgarian** — it must sound like a Bulgarian believer wrote it, not a translated English song. No calques, no English word order; use the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture (bathroom floor, cage door, wedding feast). Swap an image only when untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo in Bulgaria (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook preserved** — match the scheme *only with real, sensible words*; ALWAYS keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** Every single line must make complete, natural sense to a native Bulgarian *on its own, with the rhyme ignored.* **Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement.** Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar, or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT**. Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line (delete the final rhyme word; does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?).

> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Bulgarian culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. NARRATOR GENDER — FIX THIS FIRST (Slavic past tense is gendered)

**Before translating a single line, lock the narrator's gender for the song.** Bulgarian past-tense (aorist/imperfect) verbs and predicate adjectives inflect for gender:
- Masculine singular past: **бях, пях, паднах, видях, вървях** ("I was / sang / fell / saw / walked").
- Feminine singular past: **бях** (same form for "was" in 1sg, but) **пях/пях** — note participle forms differ: *паднал съм* (m.) vs *паднала съм* (f.); *изгубен* (m.) vs *изгубена* (f.) for "lost"; *сам* (m.) vs *сама* (f.) for "alone."
- The **perfect tense uses the -л participle, which IS gendered**: *аз съм пял* (m.) / *аз съм пяла* (f.); *видял съм* (m.) / *видяла съм* (f.). Watch these closely.
- Plural / congregational "we": *били сме / пели сме* (m. or mixed) vs all-female *пели сме* (participle plural is *-ли*, gender-neutral in plural — safe for a congregation).

Decide per persona/song; match the persona's voice. If the EN source is gender-neutral and unknown, **default to the recorded vocal's gender** (VOCAL GENDER footer) and keep it consistent. **A mismatch (male persona singing *паднала съм*) is an instant, glaring error to every Bulgarian ear.**

## 0A. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

1. **No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** Never end a line with a word there *only* to rhyme. Bulgarian offenders to watch: padding particles **бре, де, я, ма, нали, па** dropped to fill a beat; archaic filler **ей, се** jammed in; abstract nouns parked at line-end with no role (**мощ, твърд, бран** standing alone). Cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no meaning, cut it and recast.
2. **Only REAL Bulgarian words & correct forms.** **BULGARIAN HAS NO INFINITIVE** (see §0A grammar pitfall below) — never calque an English/Russian-style infinitive. Use the **definite article as a suffix** correctly (*Бог* → *Богът*, *Господ* → *Господът/Господа*, *кръст* → *кръстът*) — wrong or missing article is a classic error. Watch verb aspect (perfective/imperfective) and the renarrated/evidential mood (don't drop into *-л* renarration by accident: *Исус дошъл* implies hearsay — use *Исус дойде / ще дойде*).
3. **Correct prepositions & set phrases — no calques.** *вярвам **в** Бога* ("believe in God"), NOT *вярвам **на** Бога* (that means "I trust/obey him"); *моля се **на** Бога* (dat. sense "pray to"); *благодаря **за*** (acc.). English calque *"правя разлика"* ("make a difference") → *"променям нещо."* Avoid Russianisms (Bulgarian and Russian are close — do NOT slip Russian forms: not *спасение* misread, not *воскресение* — Bulgarian is **възкресение**).
4. **No English-calque or tech/telephone-metaphor images.** "On the line to heaven" must not become a phone-line; "reach out" → *протягам ръка / обръщам се*, not a calque. De-hyphenate English stacked compounds into a natural clause.
5. **Loanwords/register that misread in Bulgarian.** **ринг** = a *boxing ring* first → use *кръг / под / дансинг (танцов под)*. **публика** in a throne-room line reads as a concert audience → *онези, които слушат*. Russian-flavored church words can read as foreign/Orthodox-Slavonic where a plain Bulgarian word is wanted. Never drop slang (*мерак, готин, яко, кеф*) or a flippant word into a reverent line.
6. **Line-end adjectives & participles agree** in gender/number (Bulgarian inflects these, though nouns lost cases): *свята Отец*✗ → *свят Отец*; fix the rhyme partner, not the grammar.
7. **No grammatically stranded tails** — with the definite-article-as-suffix system, a stranded modifier with no head reads broken; every phrase must resolve.
8. **Imagery internally consistent** — no appended image contradicting the scene, no object with no antecedent.
9. **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word distorts doctrine; **късмет / случайност** ("luck / chance") stays strictly subordinate to God's sovereignty — never let "luck/fate" (*съдба, орис, късмет*) rhyme its way into God's place.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** delete the final rhyme word; if the rest doesn't still say something true and natural, the line is forced — rewrite it meaning-first, even unrhymed.

## 0A-GRAMMAR PITFALL — BULGARIAN HAS NO INFINITIVE (flag, mandatory)

Unlike English (and most of Europe), **Bulgarian lost the infinitive.** There is no *"to sing," "to come," "to be saved"* as a single verb form. Instead use the construction **да + present-tense conjugated verb**:
- "I want **to sing**" → *Искам **да пея**.*
- "He came **to save**" → *Той дойде **да спаси**.*
- "Teach me **to pray**" → *Научи ме **да се моля**.*
- "Ready **to follow**" → *готов **да следвам**.*
**Never** calque an English infinitive or borrow a Russian-style one (*петь, спасти*✗). Every "to + verb" in the EN source must become a **да-clause** with the verb agreeing in person/number. This is the single most common foreign-author error in Bulgarian lyrics — check every English infinitive.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Bulgaria is overwhelmingly **Eastern Orthodox** by cultural-national identity (the **Българска православна църква** is woven into national history and the Cyrillic/Saints Cyril-and-Methodius heritage), with a **Muslim minority** and a **smaller but vibrant Protestant/evangelical community** — **Pentecostals** (the largest Protestant group, incl. strong Roma-church movements), Baptists, Congregationalists, and Methodists. The BG version must **hold for the whole Bulgarian Body of Christ** — readable and warm to an Orthodox-cultural listener and authentic to evangelicals.

- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** all traditions own: *Бог, Господ, Исус, Христос, Свети Дух, Отец, Спасител, благодат, слава, царство, кръст, възкресение, Агнец, Алилуя, Амин.* Anchor Scripture echoes to a recognized Bulgarian Bible (see §2C — **Синодалната Библия** for the Orthodox-familiar classic register, **Верен / Ревизирана** for the evangelical/contemporary).
- **No tradition-specific imports the source lacks:** **avoid icon and saint devotion**, no veneration of the Theotokos as devotion, no one-church liturgical formulas, no glossolalia naming. Orthodoxy is the cultural water Bulgarians swim in, so it is tempting to reach for icon/saint imagery — **don't**; keep it Christ-centered and Bible-plain, the common ground.
- **Reverent register toward God.** Bulgarian Christian print capitalizes divine pronouns (*Ти, Теб, Той, Него, Неговия*) and addresses God with the singular reverent **Ти**. No vulgar/street slang anywhere.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Невястата / Младоженеца*) is biblical — keep it pure.
- **Keep the offense of the cross, remove the offense of bad form** (1 Cor 1:23). Don't dilute sin, blood, repentance, judgment, or Christ's exclusivity — say them in clean, dignified Bulgarian.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family handling:** defer to receptor (Bulgarian church) norms, which are traditional; do not import English-language inclusive-language debates.

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame: a BLEND of honor–shame and guilt–innocence.** Bulgarian culture carries strong honor–shame currents — **срам** (shame), **чест** (honor), **достойнство** (dignity), family name and не-да-се-изложиш ("don't disgrace yourself"). **Foreground for honor-shame:** the outcast restored to belonging, the Father **running to embrace** the prodigal, shame lifted and a name restored, adoption into God's family. **Also foreground for guilt-innocence:** forgiveness, a clean conscience (*съвест*), debt cancelled. Lead with **restored honor / belonging**, support with pardon.
- **Worship temperature: REVERENT.** Bulgarian sacred feeling is solemn, weighty, awe-tinged — shaped by Orthodox gravity and a history of suffering. Pentecostal services can be warm and expressive, but the broadly safe register is **reverent and heartfelt**, not arena-hype. Tone down "screaming/jumping"; keep deep, sincere joy.
- **Taboo/danger images → safe substitutes:**
  - *Buddy/"homeboy" Jesus* → never; keep Исус reverent (*Господ Исус*).
  - *Exuberant dancing before the Lord* → keep the joy but render as *радваме се / ликуваме / славим*; literal nightclub "dancing" reads worldly. Folk-circle *хоро* is cultural but secular-festive — don't import it into worship.
  - *Wine flowing like a river* → biblical and fine, but keep it clearly the **new wine of the Kingdom**, not tavern imagery (rakia/wine culture is strong).
  - *Fire* → default to **purifying/refining fire**; disambiguate when judgment is meant.
  - *Icons / saints / candles* → do NOT import as devotional images even though they're culturally everywhere (§1).
  - *King/Kingdom* → Bulgaria is a republic but reveres its medieval **царе** (tsars) historically; *Цар / Царство* is biblical and resonant — lean on majesty and justice.
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom:** **копнеж** (deep yearning/longing) and **тъга** (a soulful sorrow); the folk register of **жалба/жал** carries lament. Channel the song's longing through *копнеж*, not loud display.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** **съдба / орис / късмет** ("fate/destiny/luck") — pre-Christian fatalism is culturally present; keep God's providence clearly above it (§0A.9). Avoid blood-and-soil nationalism; *родина* (homeland) carries weight — use sparingly. Ethnic sensitivity around Roma and Turkish/Muslim minorities — keep "every tribe" warmly inclusive, never triumphalist-over-others.
- **Reverence forms required when addressing God:** singular reverent **Ти**, capitalized **Ти/Теб/Тебе/Твоя/Той/Него/Неговия** for God; vocatives *Боже! Господи! Господи Исусе! Отче!*
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** the solemn, slightly archaic **Church-register of the Synodal Bible** for sacred lines (functions for Bulgarians like the KJV/Slavonic gravity), and the folk-song parallelism and refrain repetition of Bulgarian song tradition. Allude to recognizable hymn/Scripture cadences — don't quote wholesale.

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (Bulgarian-specific)

- **Match syllable count.** Compress with the contractions Bulgarian singers actually use: enclitic clitics chained smoothly (**да те, ще се, не ще, що е**); the reduced **ми/ти/си/го/я/ги/ни** clitics ride lightly; **ела** (come!) for *дойди* when shorter; drop the auxiliary in colloquial perfect where natural. Never pad with filler particles (*бре, де, я*).
- **Bulgarian stress is FREE and MOBILE (lexical) — and unmarked in spelling.** It is NOT fixed; it varies by word and can shift with the definite article/plural (*вода́ → во́ди*, *глава́ → глави́*). You MUST know the real stress of each word and place stressed syllables on strong beats — guessing produces a foreign accent. Read every line aloud in rhythm. (Vowel reduction of unstressed *о→у, а→ъ, е→и* in speech is natural in singing too.)
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes** — land big held notes on **а, о, е, у, и** (*слава, радост, Агнец, Алилуя*); Bulgarian has no long/short distinction, so any vowel sustains, but avoid landing on the **ъ** (schwa) or a thick consonant cluster. The hook "Jubiluyah" ends open (-ah) — exploit it.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot** (lands 0:00–0:08); the chorus must arrive by the same bar.
- **Rhyme is OPTIONAL and last.** Bulgarian rhymes readily; resist grammar-rhyming. Preserve AABB/ABAB *only with words that genuinely belong*; else near-rhyme/assonance/blank verse. **Rhyme stance for Bulgarian: do NOT force end-rhyme** — recast the OTHER line of a couplet before cramming a bad word.
- **Cyrillic spelling is MANDATORY and exact** — Suno/TTS pronounce from text. Use proper Cyrillic (е not Latin e, а not Latin a, etc.); a Latin-look-alike letter mis-sings. Keep the **ь/ъ** distinction (*ъ* = schwa vowel, *ь* only softens before *о*). The coined Latin hook stays Latin (§3).
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing each line aloud slurred, as an AI vocal would; check blurred syllables across word boundaries don't form a vulgar/comic word (Bulgarian taboo words like *мамка, гъз* can surface accidentally through clitic chains). Reject and recast if so.

*Why it works:* hook verbatim in Latin, same beat; *Исус* (consistent name); "the reason we are dancing now" rendered as **причината да ликуваме сега** — note the **да-clause** (Bulgarian has no infinitive, §0A-GRAMMAR) and **ликуваме** ("we exult/rejoice"), which fits the **reverent worship temperature** (§1A) better than literal nightclub "dancing" (*танцуваме*) while keeping the joy; "the King is coming back" → *Царят се завръща* (majestic, reverent, definite article *Царят* correct); *сега / тук* is a real, sensible near-rhyme; mobile stress checked; zero offense across Orthodox-cultural and evangelical ears; no icon/saint import.

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0A):** suppose you need a rhyme for *небе* ("heaven").
- ❌ Forced: *"…съдбата ни води към светло **добре!**"* — "fate leads us to a bright okay!" *добре* (okay/well) is filler **and** "fate leads us" elevates *съдба* over God (§0A.9).
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"…Исус ни води нагоре, към **небе.**"* — "Jesus leads us upward, toward heaven": real words, sound doctrine, clean rhyme.
- ✅ Also fine (rhyme dropped): *"…Исус ни води, победата е Негова."* — no rhyme with *небе*, but clear and true. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**
