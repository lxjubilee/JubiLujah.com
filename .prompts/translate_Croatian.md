**Purpose:** Translate finished English (EN) Jubilee/Inspire song lyrics into **singable, natural, faith-appropriate, culturally resonant Croatian (hrvatski, HR)** — not a literal gloss. ## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to meaning & the gospel** — the cross, the resurrection, the name of Isus, repentance, the King's return must survive intact. Never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels must fit the original melody. A line a native can't sing comfortably has failed, even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Croatian** — it must sound like a Croatian believer wrote it, not a translated English song. No calques, no English word order; use the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture (bathroom floor, cage door, wedding feast). Swap an image only when untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo in Croatia (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook preserved** — match the scheme *only with real, sensible words*; ALWAYS keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** Every single line must make complete, natural sense to a native Croatian *on its own, with the rhyme ignored.* **Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement.** Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar, or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT**. Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line (delete the final rhyme word; does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?).

> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Croatian culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. NARRATOR GENDER — FIX THIS FIRST (Slavic past tense is gendered)

**Before translating a single line, lock the narrator's gender for the song.** Croatian past tense (perfekt) uses the gendered **-l participle**, and predicate adjectives agree:
- Masculine singular: **bio sam, pjevao sam, pao sam, vidio sam, hodao sam** ("I was / sang / fell / saw / walked").
- Feminine singular: **bila sam, pjevala sam, pala sam, vidjela sam, hodala sam.**
- Predicate adjectives agree: *bio sam izgubljen* (m.) vs *bila sam izgubljena* (f.); *sam* (m.) vs *sama* (f.) for "alone."
- Plural / congregational "we": **bili smo / pjevali smo** (m. or mixed) vs all-female **bile smo / pjevale smo** — for a congregation use the masculine/mixed *bili smo*.

Decide per persona/song; match the persona's voice. If the EN source is gender-neutral and unknown, **default to the recorded vocal's gender** (VOCAL GENDER footer) and keep it consistent. **A mismatch (male persona singing *pala sam*) is an instant, glaring error to every Croatian ear.**

## 0A. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

1. **No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** Never end a line with a word there *only* to rhyme. Croatian offenders to watch: padding particles **ej, joj, pa, ma, ta, baš, eto** dropped to fill a beat; archaic/poetic filler **gle** ("behold") jammed in; abstract nouns parked at line-end with no role (**moć, slava, kob** standing alone). Cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no meaning, cut it and recast.
2. **Only REAL Croatian words & correct forms.** Croatian has **7 cases** — wrong case to fit a rhyme is the classic error. Watch the locative (*u nebu / na zemlji*), instrumental (*s tobom*, not *s tebe*), genitive plural (*ljudi, dana, sinova*). Respect verb aspect (perfective/imperfective: *spasiti* vs *spašavati*) and the correct future (*spasit ću / doći ću*, not a calqued auxiliary). Don't slip into **Serbian/ekavian** forms — Croatian is **ijekavian**: *vrijeme* (not *vreme*), *vjera* (not *vera*), *svijet* (not *svet*), *lijep* (not *lep*).
3. **Correct prepositions & set phrases — no calques.** *vjerovati **u** Boga* (acc., "believe in God"); *moliti se **Bogu*** (dat., "pray to God"); *hvala **na*** (loc.) / *zahvaljujem **za*** (acc.). English calque *"napraviti razliku"* ("make a difference") → *"nešto promijeniti."* Avoid Anglicism *"na kraju dana"* as a filler idiom → *"naposljetku / na koncu."*
4. **No English-calque or tech/telephone-metaphor images.** "On the line to heaven" must not become a phone-line; "reach out" → *pružiti ruku / okrenuti se*, not a calque. De-hyphenate English stacked compounds into a natural clause.
5. **Loanwords/register that misread in Croatian.** **ring** = a *boxing ring* first → use *krug / pod / podij (plesni podij)*. **publika** in a throne-room line reads as a concert audience → *oni koji slušaju*. Anglicisms (*spika, frend, kul*) and Serbisms read as wrong-register. Never drop slang (*kul, faking, ziher*) or a flippant word into a reverent line.
6. **Line-end adjectives & participles agree** in gender/number/case (Croatian inflects heavily): *sveta Otac*✗ → *sveti Otac*; fix the rhyme partner, not the grammar.
7. **No grammatically stranded tails** — every case-marked phrase must attach to a head; a dangling genitive (*…tog svijeta* with no noun) is rejected.
8. **Imagery internally consistent** — no appended image contradicting the scene, no object with no antecedent.
9. **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word distorts doctrine; **sreća / slučaj / sudbina / kob** ("luck / chance / fate") stays strictly subordinate to God's sovereignty — never let "fate/luck" rhyme its way into God's place.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** delete the final rhyme word; if the rest doesn't still say something true and natural, the line is forced — rewrite it meaning-first, even unrhymed.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Croatia is **overwhelmingly Roman Catholic** (Katolička crkva) — Catholicism is woven into national and cultural identity — with **small Protestant/evangelical communities** (Evanđeoska/Baptist, Pentecostal "pentekostalci," Reformed in some regions, Lutheran), a small **Serbian Orthodox** minority, and a growing secular segment. The HR version must **hold for the whole Croatian Body of Christ** — warm and natural to a Catholic-cultural listener and authentic to evangelicals.

- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** all traditions own: *Bog, Gospodin, Isus, Krist, Duh Sveti, Otac, Spasitelj, milost, slava, kraljevstvo, križ, uskrsnuće, Janje, Aleluja, Amen.* Anchor Scripture echoes to a recognized Croatian Bible (see §2C — historic **Kašić** / the **Šarić** Catholic translation / a **suvremeni / Hrvatski biblijski nakladnik** contemporary version).
- **No tradition-specific imports the source lacks:** **avoid Marian devotion and imports** (no *Gospa*, no rosary/*krunica*, no Marian apparition imagery — though culturally pervasive, it is not in the gospel-song source and divides the Body), no saint veneration, no one-church liturgical formulas, no glossolalia naming. Keep it Christ-centered and Bible-plain — the common ground.
- **Reverent register toward God.** Croatian Christian print capitalizes divine pronouns (*Ti, Tebe, On, Njega, Njegov*) and addresses God with the singular reverent **Ti**. No vulgar/street slang anywhere.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Zaručnica / Zaručnik*) is biblical — keep it pure.
- **Keep the offense of the cross, remove the offense of bad form** (1 Cor 1:23). Don't dilute sin, blood, repentance, judgment, or Christ's exclusivity — say them in clean, dignified Croatian.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family handling:** defer to receptor (Croatian church) norms, which are traditional; do not import English-language inclusive-language debates.

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame: guilt–innocence, within a strong Catholic culture.** **Foreground:** sin confessed and forgiven (*ispovijed/oproštenje* is culturally familiar — but render it as the gospel reality of forgiveness, not the sacramental ritual), a clean conscience (*savjest*), grace freely given, debt cancelled, freedom from the bondage of sin. Secondary honor/belonging notes (family, *dom*, homeland) resonate but lead with **grace and pardon**.
- **Worship temperature: REVERENT.** Croatian sacred feeling is dignified and heartfelt — solemn Catholic gravity, with warmth in charismatic/Pentecostal circles and in beloved popular hymns. The broadly safe register is **reverent, sincere, melodic** — not arena-hype. Tone down "screaming/jumping"; keep deep, warm joy.
- **Taboo/danger images → safe substitutes:**
  - *Buddy/"homeboy" Jesus* → never; keep Isus reverent (*Gospodin Isus*).
  - *Exuberant dancing before the Lord* → keep the joy but render as *radujemo se / kličemo / slavimo*; literal nightclub "dancing" reads worldly. Folk *kolo* (circle dance) is cultural but festive-secular — don't import it into worship.
  - *Wine flowing like a river* → biblical and fine; in a strong wine culture keep it clearly the **new wine of the Kingdom**, not tavern imagery.
  - *Fire* → default to **purifying/refining fire**; disambiguate when judgment is meant.
  - *Marian / rosary / saint imagery* → do NOT import even though it's culturally everywhere (§1).
  - *King/Kingdom* → Croatia is a republic proud of its medieval kings; *Kralj / Kraljevstvo* is biblical and resonant — lean on majesty and justice.
  - *Warfare/army/conquering* → handle with care; the **Homeland War (Domovinski rat)** is living memory. Avoid militaristic triumphalism and any "blood-and-soil" homeland rhetoric; prefer light overcoming darkness, steadfast faith.
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom:** **čežnja** (deep yearning/longing) and the Mediterranean-Slavic mood of **sjeta** (wistful melancholy); Dalmatian song carries a tender longing (the spirit of *fjaka*'s opposite — soulful ache). Channel the song's longing through *čežnja*.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** **sudbina / kob / sreća** ("fate/luck") — keep God's providence above it (§0A.9). **Domovina** (homeland) and war/ethnic themes are sensitive — keep "every tribe" warmly inclusive, never nationalist. Be careful not to slip Serbisms — language is part of national identity.
- **Reverence forms required when addressing God:** singular reverent **Ti**, capitalized **Ti/Tebe/Tvoj/On/Njega/Njegov** for God; vocatives *Bože! Gospodine! Gospodine Isuse! Oče!*
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** the dignified register of beloved Croatian hymnody (*Crkva u Hrvata* hymn tradition, e.g., the cadence of *"Zdravo Djevo"* is Marian — avoid that one — but Christ-centered classics and Advent/Easter hymns are fair to allude to), and Dalmatian **klapa** parallel-voice warmth (allude in the Style block, §10). Allude to recognizable Scripture/hymn cadences — don't quote wholesale.

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (Croatian-specific)

- **Match syllable count.** Compress with the contractions/elisions Croatian singers actually use: future fusion **doći ću → doć' ću**, **bit ću** (biti + ću); clitic chains ride lightly (**da te, da se, ću ti, mi je, su me**); colloquial **nemam/nemoj** vs full forms; drop a weak vowel in fast lines where natural (*neka → nek'*). Never pad with filler particles (*pa, ma, eto*).
- **Croatian stress NEVER falls on the last syllable** of a multisyllabic word (it sits on a non-final syllable), and Croatian has **pitch accent** (rising vs falling tone) plus vowel **length** — these are phonemic but unmarked in everyday spelling. Practically: place the word's natural stressed syllable on a strong beat and **avoid stressing a final syllable**; monosyllables and clitics lean on neighbors. Don't force an English end-stress. Read every line aloud in rhythm.
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes** — land big held notes on **a, o, e, u, i** (*slava, radost, Janje, Aleluja*); avoid landing a long note on a thick consonant cluster. The hook "Jubiluyah" ends open (-ah) — exploit it.
- **The Croatian-specific letters č, ć, š, ž, đ matter for sound AND meaning** — *č* (hard) vs *ć* (soft) distinguish words; *đ*/dž, *š*, *ž*. Write them correctly: TTS/Suno pronounce from text (*"crkva"* needs no diacritic but *"križ" / "Spasitelj" / "uskrsnuće" / "Janjetova"* do). Syllabic **r** (*krv, prst, smrt, trg*) is singable but eats melodic space — don't stack on fast melismas.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot** (lands 0:00–0:08); the chorus must arrive by the same bar.
- **Rhyme is OPTIONAL and last.** Croatian rhymes readily; resist grammar-rhyming. Preserve AABB/ABAB *only with words that genuinely belong*; else near-rhyme/assonance/blank verse. **Rhyme stance for Croatian: do NOT force end-rhyme** — recast the OTHER line of a couplet before cramming a bad word.
- **Diacritics MANDATORY** (č ć š ž đ) in all sung text and titles — and keep **ijekavian** spellings (*vrijeme, vjera, svijet*), not ekavian.
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing each line aloud slurred, as an AI vocal would; check blurred syllables across word boundaries don't form a vulgar/comic word (Croatian taboo words like *kurac, jeba-, govno* can surface accidentally through clitic chains). Reject and recast if so.

## 9. WORKED EXAMPLE (chorus)

**EN (Jubilujah, T1 chorus):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus is the reason we are dancing now! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / The King is coming back — somehow, somehow!

**HR (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Isus je razlog što slavimo sad! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Kralj se vraća — vratit će se, znaj!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim, same beat; *Isus* (consistent name); "the reason we are dancing now" rendered as **razlog što slavimo sad** ("the reason we celebrate now") — the **reverent Croatian worship temperature** (§1A) makes literal nightclub "dancing" (*plešemo*) read worldly, so *slavimo* keeps the joy and the meaning while staying relatable; "the King is coming back" → *Kralj se vraća* (majestic, reverent, *Kralj* stressed on the downbeat, never final-syllable stress); *sad / znaj* is a real, sensible near-rhyme; ijekavian and diacritics correct; zero offense across Catholic-cultural and evangelical ears; no Marian import.

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0A):** suppose you need a rhyme for *nebo* ("heaven").
- ❌ Forced: *"…sudbina nas vodi, sjaji **kao slavo!**"* — invented/wrong-case "slavo," **and** "fate leads us" elevates *sudbina* over God (§0A.9).
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"…Isus nas vodi gore, prema **nebu.**"* — "Jesus leads us up, toward heaven": real words, correct locative (*nebu*), sound doctrine, clean near-rhyme.
- ✅ Also fine (rhyme dropped): *"…Isus nas vodi, pobjeda je Njegova."* — no rhyme with *nebo*, but clear and true. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**

## 10. ADDICTIVENESS / RE-LISTENABILITY PASS (after the album is produced in Croatian)

Run AFTER the Croatian tracks are generated. Three listen passes:
1. **Feeling pass** — does it land on the §1A frame (guilt-innocence: forgiveness, clean conscience, grace) with the reverent-yet-warm Croatian temperature and the *čežnja* ache?
2. **Craft pass** — hook clarity, prosody (no final-syllable stress, natural stress on strong beats), diction, no padded bars, č/ć/cluster words off fast runs, ijekavian intact.
3. **Replay-triggers pass** — hook, groove, ear-candy, payoff.

Then amplify, tuning the **English `Style` block (§6) and re-gen prompts only — never doctrine or the locked translation**:
