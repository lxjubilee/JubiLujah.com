# Jubilee Music — Polish (PL) Lyrics Translation Engine
<!-- Endonym: Polski (pl) — Latin script (Polish alphabet with diacritics) -->

**Purpose:** Translate finished English (EN) Jubilee/Inspire song lyrics into **singable, natural, faith-appropriate, culturally resonant Polish (PL / Polski)** — not a literal gloss. A good PL translation **sings as well as the original, is instantly understandable and *relatable* to a native Polish believer, and does not offend any faith community in the culture** (a strongly Catholic majority culture, with a small but real evangelical / Pentecostal / Baptist minority). Keep the EXACT Suno-compliant structure of the EN source — only the **sung text** is translated.

**Companion to:** `music_albums (Part 2) - content.md` (canonical Suno lyrics format) and `Part 3 - Music SOP v2.md`.

## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to meaning & the gospel** — theology must survive intact (the cross, the resurrection, the name of Jesus, repentance, judgment, the King's return). Never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels must fit the original melody. Polish stress is regular (penultimate) but the language is **consonant-heavy**, so a line dense with clusters can choke the melody even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Polish** — it must sound like a native believer wrote it; no calques, no English word order; uses the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture (bathroom floor, cage door, wedding feast). Swap an image only if untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook** — match the rhyme scheme ONLY with real, sensible words; always keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** every line must make complete, natural sense to a native Polish speaker *with the rhyme ignored*. Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement. Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar (aspect, case endings), or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT** — use blank verse, assonance, or near-rhyme. Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line.

> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Polish culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. NARRATOR GENDER — FIX THIS FIRST (Polish past-tense verbs are gendered)

**Before translating a single line, decide the singer's grammatical gender** and lock it for the whole song. Polish marks gender in the **past tense and predicate adjectives** of a first-person singer:
- Male singer: *śpiewałem, modliłem się, byłem zgubiony, byłem zmęczony, płakałem, znalazłem, jestem gotów/gotowy, jestem wybaczony.*
- Female singer: *śpiewałam, modliłam się, byłam zgubiona, byłam zmęczona, płakałam, znalazłam, jestem gotowa, jestem wybaczona.*

The EN source is genderless in past tense, so the error is invisible until written. **Read the VOCAL GENDER footer; if absent, ask.** A mismatch (*"znalazłam"* sung by a male vocalist) is an instant defect to every native ear. For a congregation/"we" voice use **my** + plural (masculine-personal *my śpiewaliśmy, byliśmy zgubieni* / feminine *śpiewałyśmy, byłyśmy zgubione*) — prefer the plural where the EN is communal, but note Polish "we" is ALSO gendered (the virile/non-virile distinction). God as subject is masculine (*On przyszedł, On zbawił, On zmartwychwstał*).

## 0B. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

1. **No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** No word that exists only to rhyme. Watch tacked-on particles **już, że, oto, wszak, hen** used as syllable-padding; archaic filler (*ci, sam, wonczas*) dropped in for an "old" sound; abstract dangling nouns appended with no grammatical home; *na wieki* used as a meaningless tail.
2. **Only REAL words & correct verb forms — especially ASPECT.** Perfective vs imperfective is the key trap: *zbawiać* (ongoing) vs *zbawić* (accomplished) — "On mnie zbawił" (done) NOT "On mnie zbawiał" (was saving) for the finished work of the cross. No invented verbs. Correct case after prepositions (*do Ciebie, z Tobą, w Tobie, o Tobie*).
3. **Correct prepositions & set phrases — no calques.** Caught calque traps: *"na końcu dnia"* (English "at the end of the day" filler) → drop or *"ostatecznie"*; *"robić sens"* (calque of "make sense") → *"mieć sens"*; *"wziąć udział"* is correct, but *"brać miejsce"* (calque of "take place") → *"odbywać się / dziać się"*; *"aplikować"* in the English sense → *"ubiegać się / zgłaszać"*. Verify every preposition is the one a native uses (*czekać NA*, *tęsknić ZA*).
4. **No English-calque or tech/telephone-metaphor images.** Don't render "on the line to heaven" as *"na linii"* (phone line); don't calque "reach out." De-stack English hyphen-compounds into a natural clause.
5. **Loanwords/register that misread in Polish** (§5): *los/fortuna/szczęście* (fate/luck — note *szczęście* means BOTH "happiness" AND "luck"!) must stay subordinate to God's sovereignty; *party* flat → *święto / radosne świętowanie*; English anglicisms (*sorry, ok*) out of a reverent line.
6. **Line-end adjectives agree** in gender, number, and case with their noun (e.g. *miłość… wieczna*, feminine — not *wieczny*).
7. **No grammatically stranded tails** — a genitive/instrumental left attached to nothing.
8. **Imagery internally consistent** — no contradicting or antecedent-less image.
9. **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word distorts doctrine; *los/szczęście/przypadek* (fate/luck/chance) stay subordinate to God, never the cause of salvation.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** delete the final rhyming word; if the rest doesn't still say something true and natural — and that word isn't one you'd actually use — the line is forced. Rewrite it meaning-first.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Poland is one of Europe's most **strongly Roman Catholic** cultures — Catholicism is woven into national identity, family life, and the calendar. There is a **small but real evangelical / Protestant minority**: Pentecostals (the Kościół Zielonoświątkowy is the largest Protestant body), Baptists, and other evangelicals, plus a historic Lutheran/Reformed presence. Jubilee/Inspire songs are CCM/evangelical in origin, but the PL version must **hold for the whole Polish Body of Christ** — and land naturally in a Catholic-shaped culture without importing Catholic-specific devotions.

**Cross-tradition safety rules (PL-specific):**
- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** that Catholics and evangelicals both own: *Bóg, Pan, Jezus, Chrystus, Duch Święty, Ojciec, Zbawiciel, łaska, chwała, Królestwo, krzyż, Zmartwychwstanie, Baranek, Alleluja, Amen.*
- **Anchor Scripture echoes to the standard Bibles:** **Biblia Tysiąclecia** (the Catholic standard, by far the most widely recognized in Poland) and **Biblia Warszawska** (the standard Protestant Bible). When a line quotes/echoes Scripture: John 19:30 → **"Wykonało się!"** (both traditions); Rev 22:20 → **"Przyjdź, Panie Jezu!"** Alluding to a known cadence builds instant trust across the Body.
- **Do NOT import tradition-specific practices** the source lacks — **and in Poland the biggest flag is MARIAN DEVOTION.** Marian piety (Matka Boża, Maryja, Częstochowa, the rosary/różaniec, Marian hymns) is enormous in Polish Catholic culture, but Jubilee songs are Christ-centered and the lyrics must NOT add it — and evangelicals would reject it. **No Marian references, no saint/relic veneration, no rosary, no one-church liturgical formulas, no glossolalia naming.** Keep it Christ-centered, Bible-plain — that is the common ground. (Be aware Marian devotion exists and is beloved; the rule is simply: do not import it into these lyrics.)
- **Reverence register toward God — use respectful forms and capitalize divine pronouns.** Polish Christian print capitalizes *Ty, Ciebie, Tobie, Tobą, Twój; On, Jego, Jemu, Nim* when referring to God; do the same. The intimate singular **Ty** is the correct, universal address to God in Polish prayer/worship (as in *Ojcze nasz… święć się imię Twoje*) — never the formal *Pan/Państwo* address to God. Use the vocative (*Boże, Panie, Ojcze, Jezu*). No crude/vulgar/street slang.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Oblubienica / Oblubieniec*) is biblical and fine; keep it pure.
- **Keep the offense of the cross, remove the offense of bad form** (1 Cor 1:23). Don't dilute sin, blood, repentance, judgment, or Christ's exclusivity — say them in clean, dignified Polish.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family handling:** defer to THIS community's norms (traditional; do not import source-culture debates).

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame — primarily GUILT–INNOCENCE, inside a strong Catholic moral-conscience culture.** Sin, confession, conscience, forgiveness, and grace are central, deeply felt categories (the Catholic practice of confession makes *grzech / spowiedź / przebaczenie / sumienie* weighty, familiar words). **Foreground:** the legal/moral debt cancelled, guilt lifted, conscience cleansed, the prodigal forgiven and welcomed home (*Ojciec* running to embrace — also lands well). The cross as the price paid for sin is the natural emotional entry. (Honor-shame is secondary but present — restored dignity, belonging — use where the song calls for it.)
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom: TĘSKNOTA** (longing, yearning, the ache of missing someone/something) — the verb *tęsknić (za)* — and **żal** (sorrow/regret/grief). Reach for *tęsknota za Tobą / za domem* ("longing for You / for home") where the EN aches or expresses homesickness for God (echoes Ps 42).
- **Worship temperature: REVERENT.** Polish worship culture leans dignified, sincere, and reverent rather than exuberant/raucous (even charismatic Polish worship retains a certain seriousness). Default to **warm reverence**: heartfelt and sincere, joyful but not flippant. Avoid breezy American "hype." Earnest devotion lands; chumminess does not.
- **Taboo / danger images → safe native substitutes:**
  - "buddy / homeboy Jesus," backslapping casualness → keep Christ exalted; intimate but reverent (*Ty* yes, slang no).
  - "dancing before the Lord" — joy is welcome (*radujmy się, weselmy się, śpiewajmy, świętujmy*); literal "tańczyć" in worship can read frivolous in a reverent Catholic-shaped setting — prefer **radujmy się / weselmy się / śpiewajmy** unless the song genuinely calls for dance.
  - "wine flowing like a river" — keep biblical-feast imagery, handle soberly; prefer *rzeka życia / źródło* for abundance.
  - fire → keep **redemptive/refining** (*ogień, który oczyszcza; Duch jak ogień*), not casual destruction.
  - homeland / nation / "blood and soil" — handle with care (*Ojczyzna* is emotionally huge in Poland and tied to faith historically); keep belonging spiritual (*Królestwo, dom Ojca, niebieska ojczyzna*), not nationalist.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** military/"conquering/army" imagery — Poland's history makes martial-triumphalist framing risky; keep spiritual-warfare imagery as **Christ over darkness / light over the night**, not nationalist conquest. King/kingdom (*Król, Królestwo*) reads majestic and biblical — fine. *Los/szczęście/fortuna* (fate/luck) must never frame salvation (and remember *szczęście* = happiness AND luck).
- **Reverence/honorific forms required when addressing God:** singular intimate **Ty** + capitalized divine pronouns + the vocative (*Boże, Panie, Ojcze, Jezu, Duchu Święty*). Never the formal *Pan/Państwo* to God.
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** Polish verse rhymes richly (its case endings give many natural rhyme families) and the ear appreciates clean rhyme + regular **syllabic meter** (Polish poetry is classically syllable-counting, e.g. the sylabotonic tradition); honor rhyme where honest words allow, never force it (Law #1). **Parallelism/anaphora** (Psalm-style) lands well.
- **Native hymnody to allude to (not quote):** the cadence of the Polish *Ojcze nasz*; the great Polish hymn tradition — e.g. the beloved Advent hymns and *kolędy* (carols) carry deep resonance, and Christ-centered classics; echo their dignity, don't lift lines (and avoid the Marian hymns specifically, per §1).

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (PL-specific)

- **Script: Latin alphabet with Polish diacritics — ą, ć, ę, ł, ń, ó, ś, ź, ż.** All sung text and titles carry full diacritics. Suno/TTS pronounce from the text: *ł* = English "w" (*Bóg* fine, but *miłość* = "mee-WOSHCH"); the **nasal vowels ą/ę** add a nasal/"-ǫ/-ę" coloring that changes singing (*są, idę, Cię*); *ś/ź/ć/ń* are soft consonants distinct from *sz/ż/cz/n*. Writing *l* for *ł* or dropping a nasal mis-sings the word.
- **CONSONANT CLUSTERS — the Polish singability challenge.** Polish stacks consonants (*wszechmocny, źdźbło, przestępstwo, chrzest, Chrystus, krzyż, wdzięczność*) that are hard to sustain or belt. **Pick smoother synonyms or recast** so big/held notes don't land on a cluster: prefer *moc* over *wszechmoc* where it fits; reach for vowel-final words on sustained notes. Read every line aloud at tempo; if a cluster trips the tongue, it will trip the singer and the AI vocal.
- **Stress is REGULAR — penultimate (second-to-last) syllable** of almost every word. This is a gift: put the penult on the strong beat and Polish sits naturally. Watch a few exceptions (some borrowings/forms stress antepenult: *muzyka, matematyka, fizyka*). One stress per word; don't force an unnatural accent.
- **Native elisions/contractions singers actually use** to fit syllables: enclitic pronoun forms **mię/mnie, cię/ciebie, go/jego, mi/mnie** (choose the short form to save a syllable); the conditional/particle clitics **-m, -ś, -śmy** can attach (*gdziem był, tyś jest* — archaic-poetic, use sparingly); merge with prepositions (*we mnie, ze mną, ku Tobie, przede mną*). Drop the copula where natural (*"On Panem"* / *"On jest Panem"* — keep *jest* if the meter needs the syllable). Prefer one-/two-syllable synonyms on tight bars (*pan→Król* won't help; *droga→ścieżka*, *światłość→światło*).
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes** — land big held notes on **a, o, e, u, i, y** (*chwała, radość→ends in cluster, avoid; prefer chwałA, AllelujA, Cię́ → nasal but singable, Tobą*). The hook "Jubiluyah" ends open (-ah) — exploit it.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot;** chorus arrives on the same bar as the EN.
- **Rhyme stance for Polish: honor clean rhyme where honest words allow — never force it.** Polish rhymes richly via case endings and the ear appreciates it; aim for AABB/ABAB **with real words**. The instant a rhyme demands awkward syntax, wrong aspect/case, an invented word, or distorted doctrine — abandon it (Law #1): near-rhyme/assonance, or recast the OTHER line. Reach for **anaphora/parallelism**.
- **Diacritics mandatory** (ą ć ę ł ń ó ś ź ż) in sung text AND titles — never strip them.
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing the line aloud slurred (as an AI vocal would) and check that blurred junctions and tight clusters don't form a vulgar/comic/sacred-taboo word; recast if so.

## 5. IDIOMS, IMAGERY & CULTURAL FIT

- **Translate the sense, not the words → native idioms.** "permission slip" → *nie potrzebuję pozwolenia / nie muszę pytać*; "did the flip" → *wszystko się odwróciło / radość wróciła*; "Sunday morning bell" → *niedzielny dzwon* (church bells are universal and resonate deeply in PL).
- **Keep concrete sensory anchors** (bathroom floor at 3 AM, cage door, wedding feast) — render vividly (*zimna podłoga o trzeciej w nocy*, *drzwi klatki*).
- **Localize culture-bound daily-life anchors** (carpool, diner, prom, credit-card debt) to equivalent native scenes (*stół w kuchni*, *droga do domu*, *długi, co przygniatają*) — do NOT localize away the global/heaven imagery (every-tribe, throne, Lamb, wedding feast).
- **Loanword/register misreads:** *los/szczęście/fortuna* (fate/luck — *szczęście* also = happiness) never cause salvation; *wino* soberly (§1A); *party* → *święto*.

- [ ] **Drop-the-rhyme test on EVERY line**; no nonsense fillers (*już/że/oto* padding, archaic *wonczas*); real words; **correct verb ASPECT** (*zbawił* not *zbawiał* for the finished work); correct case after prepositions; agreement; no stranded tails.
- [ ] **Register/loanword check** (*los/szczęście/fortuna*, *wino*, *party*); euphony pass (no accidental obscene/comic word from slurred clusters).
- [ ] **Image consistency**; **theology over rhyme** (fate/luck subordinate to God).
- [ ] **NATIVE FAITH-INSIDER SIGN-OFF** (hard release gate).
- [ ] **Addictiveness re-rating done (§10)** with before/after scores and report.
- [ ] **Naturalness** — reads as original Polish songwriting, not a translation.

## 9. WORKED EXAMPLE (chorus)

**EN (Jubilujah, T1 chorus):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus is the reason we are dancing now! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / The King is coming back — somehow, somehow!

**PL (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, communal "we" to dodge gender, frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jezus jest radością — weselmy się wraz! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Wróci nasz Król — usłyszy nas!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim, same beat; **Jezus** (consistent name); "the reason we are dancing now" → *Jezus jest radością — weselmy się wraz* — **weselmy się** ("let us rejoice/be glad") replaces literal "tańczymy," which can read frivolous in reverent Polish worship (§1A flip), and the **"we/imperative" form sidesteps narrator gender**; "the King is coming back" → *Wróci nasz Król* (majestic biblical **Król**); rhyme *wraz / nas* is real and natural; reverent-warm temperature; diacritics intact; open vowels on held notes (*wrAz, nAs*); no consonant-cluster trap; zero offense across traditions (no Marian import).

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0B):**
- ❌ Forced / wrong aspect / luck-theology: *"Jezus nas zbawiał, bo taki już los i nasz fart!"* — *zbawiał* (imperfective, "was saving," wrong for the finished work), and *los… fart* credits **fate/luck** with salvation (forbidden, §0B-9), *fart* (slang for "luck") jarringly irreverent.
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"Jezus nas zbawił — w Nim mamy nowy start!"* — *zbawił* (perfective, accomplished), grace from Christ alone, real words, sound doctrine, natural rhyme *start* (a common Polish loan, acceptable in upbeat body lyrics) with a sensible partner.
- ✅ Also fine (rhyme dropped): *"Jezus nas zbawił — i Jego Królestwo już tu."* — no end-rhyme, but clear, true, natural. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**

## 10. ADDICTIVENESS / RE-LISTENABILITY PASS (after the album is produced in Polish)

Run AFTER the Polish album is produced. Tunes the **English `Style` block (§6)** and re-gen prompts ONLY — never doctrine, never the locked translation.

**Three listen passes:**
1. **Feeling pass** — does it move you on first listen? Mark where attention drifts.
2. **Craft pass** — hook clarity, vocal delivery, mix, arrangement; note weak bars (and any cluster that fights the vocal).
3. **Replay-triggers pass** — what makes you want to hear it again (loop point, ear-candy, payoff)?

**Then amplify:**
- **Hook & delivery** — make the hook land harder and earlier; sharpen lead vocal phrasing.
- **Groove & pulse** — target a head-nod pocket (~100–128 BPM where the style allows); tighten drums/bass.
- **Texture & ear-candy** — add culturally resonant instrumentation from §1A where it fits (rich choral pads, organ/piano-forward worship texture, dignified strings) plus tasteful production candy.
- **Dynamic arc & payoff** — build the reverent-warm arc to a clear emotional lift in the final chorus.
- **Loop point & album flow** — make the end invite a restart; sequence for momentum.
- **Emotional pull & relatability** — lean on §1A's frame (guilt-innocence) and the ache word **tęsknota** to deepen the emotional hook.

**Re-rate each track + album BEFORE/AFTER** across: hook strength, groove, replay value, dynamic payoff, emotional pull, loop-ability. Deliver a summary of changes, **why each boosts re-listening**, and the measurable before→after effect.
