# Jubilee Music — Hungarian (HU) Lyrics Translation Engine
<!-- endonym: Magyar -->

**Purpose:** Translate finished English (EN) Jubilee/Inspire song lyrics into **singable, natural, faith-appropriate, culturally resonant Hungarian (magyar, HU)** — not a literal gloss. A good HU translation **sings as well as the original, is instantly understandable and *relatable* to a native Hungarian believer, and does not offend any faith community** in a country with a Catholic majority and a large, theologically robust Reformed (Református) tradition, alongside Lutheran and Pentecostal communities. Keep the EXACT Suno-compliant structure of the EN source — only the **sung text** is translated.

**Companion to:** `music_albums (Part 2) - content.md` (canonical Suno lyrics format) and `Part 3 - Music SOP v2.md`. The HU file keeps the EXACT same Suno-compliant structure as the EN source — only the **sung text** is translated.

> **Note: Hungarian is NOT Slavic — it is Uralic (Finno-Ugric) and strongly agglutinative.** There is no grammatical gender at all (no he/she; *ő* covers both), so unlike the Slavic engines there is **no narrator-gender fix** — the challenge here is vowel harmony, agglutinated word length, and fixed first-syllable stress.

## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to meaning & the gospel** — the cross, the resurrection, the name of Jézus, repentance, the King's return must survive intact. Never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels must fit the original melody. A line a native can't sing comfortably has failed, even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Hungarian** — it must sound like a Hungarian believer wrote it, not a translated English song. No calques, no English word order; use the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture (bathroom floor, cage door, wedding feast). Swap an image only when untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo in Hungary (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook preserved** — match the scheme *only with real, sensible words*; ALWAYS keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** Every single line must make complete, natural sense to a native Hungarian *on its own, with the rhyme ignored.* **Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement.** Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar, or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT**. Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line (delete the final rhyme word; does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?).

> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Hungarian culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

1. **No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** Never end a line with a word there *only* to rhyme. Hungarian offenders to watch: padding particles **hát, no, ám, bizony, ám, hisz, csak úgy** dropped to fill a beat; archaic filler **ím / íme** ("behold") jammed in; abstract nouns parked at line-end with no role (**fény, mély, lét** standing alone). Cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no meaning, cut it and recast.
2. **Only REAL Hungarian words & correct forms.** Hungarian is agglutinative — do NOT invent a non-existent suffix stack to fit. Respect **vowel harmony** in every suffix (back vs front: *-ban/-ben, -hoz/-hez/-höz, -nak/-nek, -val/-vel*) — a harmony error (*Istenben* ✓ but *háznek* ✗ → *háznak*) is a glaring mistake. Use the **definite vs indefinite conjugation** correctly (*látom* "I see it" vs *látok* "I see"). Don't drop the linking vowel where required.
3. **Correct postpositions/case suffixes & set phrases — no calques.** Hungarian uses **case suffixes**, not English-style prepositions: "believe in God" → *hiszek **Istenben*** (inessive -ben), not a calqued *hiszek -ba/-on*; "pray to God" → *imádkozom Istenhez* / *Istenhez szólok*; "thank you for" → *köszönöm a...-t/-ért*. English calque *"különbséget tenni"* can mean "discriminate" → for "make a difference" use *"számít / változtatni valamin."* Avoid *"a nap végén"* as the English "at the end of the day" idiom → *"végső soron / végül is."*
4. **No English-calque or tech/telephone-metaphor images.** "On the line to heaven" must not become a phone-line; "reach out" → *kinyújtom a kezem / odafordulok*, not a calque. De-hyphenate English stacked compounds; Hungarian compounds are written solid (*mennyország*), not hyphen-stacked English-style.
5. **Loanwords/register that misread in Hungarian.** **ring** = a *boxing/wrestling ring* first → use *kör / padló / táncparkett*. **publikum / közönség** in a throne-room line reads as a concert audience → *azok, akik hallgatják*. Overused Anglicisms (*oké, kúl, csekkol*) and Germanisms read as wrong-register. Never drop slang (*tök jó, gáz, király* as slang "cool") or a flippant word into a reverent line — note *király* literally means "king," so it's fine as the noun but NOT as teen-slang "awesome."
6. **Agreement & suffix correctness at line-end** — Hungarian has **no grammatical gender and no adjective-noun number agreement** (adjective before noun stays uninflected: *szent Atya*, *nagy Isten*), so the trap is **wrong case/possessive suffix or broken vowel harmony**, not gender. A rhyme-chosen suffix that breaks harmony or attaches the wrong case is the error — fix the rhyme partner, not the grammar.
7. **No grammatically stranded tails** — a possessive or case-marked word must resolve (*…a világnak* dative with no governing verb is stranded). Every suffix needs a grammatical home.
8. **Imagery internally consistent** — no appended image contradicting the scene, no object with no antecedent.
9. **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word distorts doctrine; **szerencse / véletlen / sors** ("luck / chance / fate") stays strictly subordinate to God's sovereignty — never let "luck/fate" rhyme its way into God's place.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** delete the final rhyme word; if the rest doesn't still say something true and natural, the line is forced — rewrite it meaning-first, even unrhymed.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Hungary's Christian landscape is plural: a **Roman Catholic majority** (Katolikus Egyház), a **large and historically central Reformed/Calvinist church** (Magyar Református Egyház — the "Hungarian religion," strong in the east and Transylvania), a significant **Lutheran/Evangélikus** community, and growing **Pentecostal/charismatic** churches (Hit Gyülekezete and others) plus Baptists. The HU version must **hold for the whole Hungarian Body of Christ** — natural to Catholic, deeply at home with Reformed believers, and authentic to Pentecostals.

- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** all traditions own: *Isten, Úr, Jézus, Krisztus, Szentlélek, Atya, Megváltó, kegyelem, dicsőség, ország/királyság, kereszt, feltámadás, Bárány, Halleluja, Ámen.* Anchor Scripture echoes to a recognized Hungarian Bible (see §2C — the historic **Károli** translation for classic/heightened register, and the **új fordítás (Magyar Bibliatársulat / MBT)** for contemporary clarity; the **Szent István Társulat** Catholic Bible is also recognized).
- **No tradition-specific imports the source lacks:** no Marian devotion, no saint/icon veneration (Reformed and Lutheran Hungarians do not venerate saints/Mary — importing it divides the Body), no one-church liturgical formulas, no glossolalia naming. Keep it Christ-centered and Bible-plain — the common ground across Catholic, Reformed, Lutheran and Pentecostal.
- **Reverent register toward God.** Hungarian Christian print capitalizes divine pronouns and divine references (*Ő, Őt, Néki, Téged, Hozzád*). Hungarian has no T/V choice problem with God — address God with **te** (intimate-reverent), as the churches do, capitalized for reverence. No vulgar/street slang anywhere.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Menyasszony / Vőlegény*) is biblical — keep it pure.
- **Keep the offense of the cross, remove the offense of bad form** (1 Cor 1:23). Don't dilute sin, blood, repentance, judgment, or Christ's exclusivity — say them in clean, dignified Hungarian.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family handling:** Hungarian is naturally genderless (no he/she), so English inclusive-language debates simply don't arise — defer to receptor (Hungarian church) family/traditional norms; do not import source-culture debates.

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame: guilt–innocence.** **Foreground:** sin honestly named and forgiven, a clean conscience (*lelkiismeret*), grace freely given (a deeply Reformed emphasis — *sola gratia*), debt cancelled, freedom from the bondage of sin, the covenant faithfulness of God. Reformed Hungary especially resonates with **grace, providence, and God's sovereignty**; lead with pardon and grace.
- **Worship temperature: REVERENT (Reformed) to WARM (Pentecostal).** Calibrate to the persona/album: Reformed-flavored material stays dignified, sober, hymn-like; Pentecostal/charismatic-flavored material can be warmer and more expressive. The broadly safe default is **reverent and sincere**, with warmth allowed — not arena-hype that reads as foreign. Hungarian sacred feeling carries a noble melancholy.
- **Taboo/danger images → safe substitutes:**
  - *Buddy/"homeboy" Jesus* → never; keep Jézus reverent (*Úr Jézus*).
  - *Exuberant dancing before the Lord* → keep the joy but render as *örvendünk / ujjongunk / magasztaljuk*; literal nightclub "dancing" (*táncolunk*) reads worldly to many — biblical *ujjongás* (jubilation) is safer across traditions. (See §9.)
  - *Wine flowing like a river* → biblical and fine; in a strong wine culture (Tokaji, Eger) keep it clearly the **new wine of the Kingdom**, not tavern imagery.
  - *Fire* → default to **purifying/refining fire**; disambiguate when judgment is meant.
  - *Saints/Mary/icons* → do NOT import even where culturally Catholic (§1).
  - *King/Kingdom* → Hungary is a republic proud of **Szent István (St Stephen)** and the Holy Crown historically; *Király / Királyság* is biblical and resonant — lean on majesty and justice, avoid nationalist crown symbolism.
  - *Warfare/army/conquering* → handle with care; Hungarian history of defeat and loss (Mohács, Trianon, 1956) makes triumphalist militarism ring hollow — prefer steadfast faith, light overcoming darkness.
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom:** **vágyakozás / vágy** (deep yearning) and the distinctly Hungarian **honvágy** (homesickness) and the noble-melancholy mood of **bánat / búsulás** (the "Hungarian sorrow," *sírva vigad a magyar* — "the Hungarian rejoices while weeping"). Channel longing through *vágyakozás* and a dignified ache, not loud display.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** **sors / végzet / szerencse** ("fate/destiny/luck") — keep God's providence above it (§0A.9). **Haza / nemzet** (homeland/nation) and Trianon-era loss are emotionally and politically charged — keep "every tribe" warmly inclusive, avoid blood-and-soil nationalism. Be sensitive about the Roma minority.
- **Reverence forms required when addressing God:** intimate-reverent **te** (not the formal *ön/maga*), with capitalized **Te/Téged/Hozzád/Ő/Őt/Néki/Övé** for God; vocatives *Istenem! Uram! Úr Jézus! Atyám!*
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** the heightened, archaic register of the **Károli Bible** for sacred lines (it functions for Hungarians much as the KJV does for English), the dignified cadence of the **Református énekeskönyv** (Genevan psalter / hymnbook) and Hungarian hymnody, and the folk-song parallelism and **rím** tradition. Allude to recognizable Scripture/hymn cadences — don't quote wholesale.

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (Hungarian-specific)

- **Match syllable count — Hungarian agglutinates, so words get LONG.** A single English word can become a long suffix-stack (*"in your kingdom"* → *országodban*, 4 syllables in one word). Watch the syllable budget closely. Compress with the contractions Hungarian singers actually use: **s** for *és* ("and"), **'sz** / dropped linking vowels in fast lines, **hozzád/benned** as single sung units, elided **a/az** before vowels, **nincs/nem's** colloquial fusions. Choose **shorter synonyms** and lighter suffixes to fit the bar — never pad with filler (*hát, no, ám*).
- **Hungarian stress is FIXED on the FIRST syllable of EVERY word** (always). Put first syllables on strong beats; do NOT force an English-style later-syllable accent — it will sound foreign and wrong. With long agglutinated words this means the downbeat lands on the word-initial syllable and the suffix-tail trails over weaker beats. Read every line aloud in rhythm.
- **Vowel length is phonemic** (a/á, e/é, o/ó, ö/ő, u/ú, ü/ű are DIFFERENT vowels, not just stress) — keep the long vowels (á é í ó ő ú ű) on longer notes where natural; *"orom"* ≠ **"öröm"** (joy). Always write the correct length.
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes** — land big held notes on **a, á, o, ó, e, é** (*dicsőség, öröm, Bárány, Halleluja*); avoid landing a long note on a thick consonant cluster. The hook "Jubiluyah" ends open (-ah) — exploit it.
- **Vowel harmony must hold even under compression** (see §0A.2) — when you pick a shorter suffix to save syllables, keep front/back harmony (*-ban/-ben*).
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot** (lands 0:00–0:08); the chorus must arrive by the same bar.
- **Rhyme is OPTIONAL and last.** Hungarian rhymes readily (suffix-rhyme is easy — *too* easy), so resist lazy grammar-rhyming on identical suffixes (rhyming *-ban/-ban* is weak). Preserve AABB/ABAB *only with words that genuinely belong*; else near-rhyme/assonance/blank verse. **Rhyme stance for Hungarian: do NOT force end-rhyme, and avoid trivial same-suffix rhyme** — recast the OTHER line of a couplet before cramming a bad word.
- **Diacritics MANDATORY** (á é í ó ö ő ú ü ű) in all sung text and titles — they mark distinct vowels; Suno/TTS pronounce from text (*"oro"* vs **"öröm"** vs *"őröm"*).
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing each line aloud slurred, as an AI vocal would; check blurred syllables across word boundaries don't form a vulgar/comic word (Hungarian taboo words like *fasz, geci, szar* can surface accidentally through elisions). Reject and recast if so.

## 5. IDIOMS, IMAGERY & CULTURAL FIT

- **Translate the sense, not the words** → native Hungarian idioms. "make a difference" → *számít / változtat valamin*; "Sunday morning bell" → *vasárnapi harangszó* (church bells universal — works); "I'll be there for you" → *melletted leszek*.
- **Keep concrete sensory anchors** (bathroom floor at 3 AM, cage door, wedding feast) — render vividly (*a hideg csempe hajnali háromkor*).
- **Localize culture-bound daily-life anchors** (carpool line, diner, prom, credit-card debt) to emotionally equivalent native scenes (*a reggeli villamos, a konyhaasztal, a ballagás/szalagavató, az adósság*) — but do NOT localize away global/heaven imagery (every-tribe, throne, Lamb, wedding feast).

**HU (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jézus a miért, ezért ujjongunk ma! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / A Király visszatér — eljön, hidd el, ma!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim, same beat; *Jézus* (consistent name); "the reason we are dancing now" rendered as **a miért, ezért ujjongunk ma** ("the why — that's why we jubilate today") — **ujjongunk** (biblical "we jubilate/exult") fits the **reverent-to-warm Hungarian temperature** (§1A) and crosses Reformed/Catholic/Pentecostal lines better than literal nightclub "dancing" (*táncolunk*) while keeping the joy; "the King is coming back" → *A Király visszatér* (majestic, reverent, first-syllable stress on **KI**-rály and **VISZ**-szatér on the downbeats); *ma / ma* would be too trivial, so the near-rhyme rides on *ma* with varied lines (avoid lazy same-word rhyme — vary the couplet if it gets repetitive); vowel harmony and long/short vowels correct; zero offense across traditions; no Marian import.

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0A):** suppose you need a rhyme for *ég* ("sky/heaven").
- ❌ Forced: *"…a szerencse vezet, és ég a **lég!**"* — "luck leads us, and the *air burns*!" *lég* (air) is filler **and** "luck leads us" elevates *szerencse* over God (§0A.9).
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"…Jézus vezet fel oda, hol vár az **ég.**"* — "Jesus leads us up to where heaven waits": real words, sound doctrine, clean rhyme, vowel harmony intact.
- ✅ Also fine (rhyme dropped): *"…Jézus vezet, a győzelem az Övé."* — no rhyme with *ég*, but clear and true. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**
