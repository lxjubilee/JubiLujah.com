## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to meaning & the gospel** — theology must survive intact (the cross, the resurrection, the name of Jesus, repentance, judgment, the King's return). Never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels must fit the original melody. Russian carries ONE stress per word and reduces unstressed vowels (*аканье*), so a line that fights the melody's accents has failed even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Russian** — it must sound like a native believer wrote it; no calques, no English word order; uses the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture (bathroom floor, cage door, wedding feast). Swap an image only if untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook** — match the rhyme scheme ONLY with real, sensible words; always keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** every line must make complete, natural sense to a native Russian speaker *with the rhyme ignored*. Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement. Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar (especially aspect or case endings), or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT** — use blank verse, assonance, or near-rhyme. Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line: delete the final rhyme word; does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?

> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Russian culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. NARRATOR GENDER — FIX THIS FIRST (Russian past-tense verbs are gendered)

**Before translating a single line, decide the singer's grammatical gender** and lock it for the whole song. Russian marks gender in the **past tense and short adjectives/participles** of a first-person singer:
- Male singer: *я пел, я молился, я был потерян, я устал, я плакал, я нашёл, я готов, я прощён.*
- Female singer: *я пела, я молилась, я была потеряна, я устала, я плакала, я нашла, я готова, я прощена.*

The EN source is genderless in past tense, so this is invisible until you write it wrong. **Read the VOCAL GENDER footer; if absent, ask.** Mismatched gender (*"я нашла"* sung by a male vocalist) is an instant, glaring defect to every native ear. For a congregation/"we" voice, use **мы** + plural (*мы пели, мы были потеряны*) and the problem disappears — prefer the plural where the EN is communal. When God is the subject, He is masculine (*Он пришёл, Он спас, Он воскрес*).

## 0B. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

1. **No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** Never end a line with a word that exists only to rhyme. Real Russian offenders to watch: tacked-on **же / ведь / вот / уж / то** as syllable-padding ("вот", "ведь" jammed in to fill a beat); archaic filler **днесь, паки, зело** dropped in for an "old" sound; abstract dangling nouns like **благодать** or **бытие** appended with no grammatical home; **навек / вовек** used as a meaningless tail. Cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no real meaning, cut it and recast.
2. **Only REAL words & correct verb forms — especially ASPECT.** Russian's perfective/imperfective pair is the #1 trap: *спасать* (ongoing) vs *спасти* (accomplished) — "Он спас меня" (done) NOT "Он спасал меня" (was saving) for the finished work of the cross. Don't invent verbs to fit; don't mix aspect for rhyme. Correct case endings after prepositions (*к Тебе*, *с Тобой*, *в Тебе*, *о Тебе*).
3. **Correct prepositions & set phrases — no calques.** Caught calque traps: *"в конце дня"* (English "at the end of the day" = filler) → drop it or use *"в итоге"*; *"делать смысл"* (calque of "make sense") → *"иметь смысл"*; *"я в порядке"* is fine colloquially but reads flat in worship; *"на самом деле"* as throwaway filler. Use *за* not *для* where Russian idiom requires (*благодарю за всё*).
4. **No English-calque or tech/telephone-metaphor images.** Don't render "on the line to heaven" as *"на линии"* (reads as a phone line); don't calque "reach out" as *"протянуть наружу."* Hyphen-stacked English compounds (*"ответ-в-темноте"*) must be de-stacked into a natural clause.
5. **Loanwords/register that misread in Russian** (see §5): *рок* = "fate/doom" as well as the music genre — dangerous near "судьба"; *парти* (party) is slang-flat; *шанс/удача* (luck/chance) must stay subordinate to God's sovereignty, never the cause of salvation.
6. **Line-end adjectives/participles agree** in gender, number, and case with their noun. A rhyme-chosen adjective that breaks agreement (*"любовь… вечный"* — любовь is feminine → *вечная*) is rejected.
7. **No grammatically stranded tails** — a genitive or instrumental left attached to nothing.
8. **Imagery internally consistent** — no image that contradicts the scene or has no antecedent.
9. **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word distorts doctrine; *судьба / рок / удача* (fate/luck) stay subordinate to God's sovereignty, never credited with salvation.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** delete the final rhyming word; if the rest doesn't still say something true and natural — and if that word isn't one you'd actually use there — the line is forced. Rewrite it meaning-first.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

The Russian-speaking world is **overwhelmingly Russian Orthodox (Русская Православная Церковь)** by cultural identity, with a real and growing **evangelical / Protestant minority** — Baptists (баптисты, ЕХБ), Pentecostals (пятидесятники), and the broad self-identifier **евангельские христиане**. Jubilee/Inspire songs are CCM/evangelical in origin, but the RU version must **hold for the whole Russian-speaking Body of Christ.**

**Cross-tradition safety rules (RU-specific):**
- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** that Orthodox and evangelicals both own: *Бог, Господь, Иисус, Христос, Дух Святой, Отец, Спаситель, благодать, слава, Царство, крест, Воскресение, Агнец, Аллилуйя, Аминь.*
- **Anchor Scripture echoes to the Синодальный перевод** (Synodal Bible) — the single translation respected across virtually all traditions. When a line quotes/echoes Scripture, echo Synodal phrasing: John 19:30 → **"Совершилось!"**; Rev 22:20 → **"Гряди, Господи Иисусе!"** (Synodal; the modern *"Приди, Господи Иисусе"* is acceptable but less iconic). Alluding to a known Synodal cadence builds instant trust.
- **Do NOT import tradition-specific practices** the source lacks: **no icon or saint veneration, no "Богородица"/Marian devotion, no liturgical formulas of one church (e.g. ектения), no glossolalia naming.** Avoid Orthodox-only liturgical vocabulary (*акафист, тропарь, литургия*) and Pentecostal-only markers alike. Keep it Christ-centered, Bible-plain — the common ground.
- **Reverence register toward God — use respectful forms and capitalize divine pronouns.** Russian Christian print capitalizes *Ты, Тебя, Тебе, Тобой, Твой; Он, Его, Ему, Им* when referring to God; do the same. The intimate **Ты** (not formal *Вы*) is the correct, universal address to God in Russian prayer/worship across all traditions — God is addressed with the singular *Ты*, as in the Lord's Prayer (*"Отче наш… да святится имя Твоё"*). Never use crude, vulgar, or street slang anywhere.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Невеста / Жених*) is biblical and fine; keep it pure.
- **Keep the offense of the cross, remove the offense of bad form** (1 Cor 1:23). Don't dilute sin, blood, repentance, judgment, or Christ's exclusivity — say them in clean, dignified Russian.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family handling:** defer to THIS community's norms (traditional; do not import source-culture debates).

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame — a blend of HONOR–SHAME and GUILT–INNOCENCE.** Russian spirituality carries a deep guilt-and-conscience axis (*совесть, грех, покаяние, прощение* are central, weighty words) **and** a strong honor-shame axis (*стыд, позор* vs. restored dignity, the family/homeland belonging). **Foreground:** the prodigal returning and the **Father running to embrace** (adoption, belonging, restored honor); the conscience cleansed and the legal debt cancelled (guilt-innocence); the cross as the place where shame is borne and honor restored. Both entries reach the same doctrine — choose per song's emotional center.
- **Native ache word: ТОСКА.** This is the untranslatable Russian sacred-longing/ache word — a spiritual yearning, heart-heaviness, longing-for-something-more (cf. Romanian *dor*, Portuguese *saudade*). Reach for it where the EN expresses ache, homesickness for God, or the soul's restlessness. Related: *томление* (longing), *печаль* (deep sorrow). Use *тоска по Тебе* / *душа томится* for "my soul longs for You" (echoes Ps 42).
- **Worship temperature is bimodal:** **reverent / lament** (the Orthodox-shaped majority — restrained awe, *благоговение*, the minor key, the cry of repentance) vs. **warm** (the evangelical congregation — heartfelt, intimate, joyful but not raucous). Default to **reverent warmth**; avoid American exuberant "hype" framing. Lament and contrition are deeply native and powerful — do not flatten them into upbeat positivity.
- **Taboo / danger images → safe native substitutes:**
  - "buddy / homeboy Jesus," backslapping casualness → keep Christ exalted; intimate but reverent (*Ты* yes, slang no).
  - "dancing before the Lord" — acceptable as joy (*ликовать, торжествовать, радоваться*); but among Orthodox-leaning listeners literal "танцевать" in worship can read frivolous — prefer **ликуем / торжествуем / поём** unless the song's celebration genuinely calls for dance.
  - "wine flowing like a river" — handle soberly; *вино* is culturally loaded (alcoholism is a national wound); keep biblical-feast imagery but don't glamorize drink. Prefer *река жизни / источник* where the image is abundance.
  - fire → keep it **redemptive/refining** (*огонь очищающий, Дух как огонь*) not casually destructive.
  - homeland / nation / "blood and soil" — **handle with extreme care** (see charged vocab); keep belonging spiritual (*Царство, дом Отца, небесная родина*), not national-patriotic.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** military/"conquering/army" imagery (**войско, завоевать, победа** in a martial sense) reads as state/patriotic rhetoric — keep spiritual-warfare imagery as **Christ over darkness / light swallowing darkness**, never nationalistic conquest. *Родина / отечество* (homeland) is heavily politicized — avoid as a God-substitute. *Рок* (= fate/doom) must never frame salvation. Keep king/kingdom imagery (*Царь, Царство*) — it reads majestic and biblical in Russian, not odd.
- **Reverence/honorific forms required when addressing God:** singular intimate **Ты** + capitalized divine pronouns (Ты/Твой/Тебя; Он/Его) and *Господи, Отче, Боже* (vocative). Never the formal *Вы* to God.
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** Russian verse is classically **syllabo-tonic** (regular metrical feet — iambic/trochaic) with strong, satisfying end-rhyme; Russian ears *expect* clean rhyme and meter more than English does — so honor rhyme where honest words allow, but never at meaning's expense (Law #1). **Parallelism and anaphora** (repeated line-openings, as in the Psalms) land beautifully and are deeply native.
- **Native hymnody to allude to (not quote):** the cadence of the Synodal Lord's Prayer (*Отче наш*); the great evangelical/Baptist hymn tradition (e.g. the Russian "Tвоя, Господь" / "Великий Бог" = "How Great Thou Art," itself Slavic-rooted) — a believer carries these rhythms; echo their dignity, don't lift lines.

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (RU-specific)

- **Script: Cyrillic (кириллица).** All sung text and titles are written in Cyrillic. Suno/TTS pronounce from the text, so write proper Russian orthography — including the letter **ё** where it changes both sound and stress (*Бог даёт*, *Он берёт*, *слёзы*); writing *е* for *ё* mis-sings the vowel and can shift the word. Use ё in the sung body.
- **Stress is everything.** Russian has ONE stress per word, unpredictable position, and **unstressed *о* reduces to "а"** (*аканье*: "хорошо" sings ха-ра-шо). Put the word's natural stress on the strong beat — a misplaced stress (*"любОвь" → "лЮбовь"*) is the most common amateur defect. Read every line aloud in rhythm. Russian words run longer than English, so **compress** (see elisions) rather than pad.
- **Native elisions/contractions singers actually use** to fit syllables: drop the copula (Russian present "to be" is normally null — *"Он Господь"* not *"Он есть Господь"*); use short adjectives where natural (*"Ты свят"* vs *"Ты святой"*); cliticize particles (*"я ж", "так и", "вот и"*) ONLY when they carry real meaning, never as filler; merge prepositions phonetically (*"со мной", "ко мне", "во мне", "надо мной"* — these vocalized prepositions add a syllable and are useful for fitting the meter). Prefer one-/two-syllable synonyms on tight bars (*путь* over *дорога*, *свет* over *сияние* where it fits).
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes** — land big held notes on **а, о, э, у, и/ы** (*слава, любовь→на "-вовь" avoid; prefer slavА, осаннА, аллилуйЯ, Тебя*). The hook "Jubiluyah" ends open (-ah) — exploit it.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot;** the chorus arrives on the same bar as the EN.
- **Rhyme stance for Russian: HONOR rhyme more than in English — but never force it.** Russian verse traditionally rhymes cleanly and the ear expects it; rich, true rhymes are abundant (Russian's case endings give many natural rhyme families). So aim to keep AABB/ABAB **with real words** — but the instant a rhyme demands awkward syntax, wrong aspect/case, an invented word, archaic filler (*днесь, зело*), or distorted doctrine, **abandon it** (Law #1): use near-rhyme/assonance. Better to recast the OTHER line of the couplet than to cram a bad word. Reach for **anaphora/parallelism** (Psalm-style) as a native device.
- **Diacritics/special letters mandatory:** write **ё** (not е) where the word has it; the soft sign **ь** and hard sign **ъ** change pronunciation and meaning — never drop them.
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing the line aloud slurred (as an AI vocal would) and check that blurred syllable-junctions don't form a vulgar Russian word (the language's three-letter obscenity roots can surface from innocent word-boundaries) or an unintended comic/sacred-taboo word. Recast if so.

## 5. IDIOMS, IMAGERY & CULTURAL FIT

- **Translate the sense, not the words → native idioms.** "permission slip" → a natural RU equivalent (*мне не нужно разрешенье / не надо спрашивать*); "did the flip" → *всё перевернулось / радость вернулась*; "Sunday morning bell" → *колокол воскресный* (church bells are universal in RU and resonate deeply — but they read Orthodox; fine as shared imagery).
- **Keep concrete sensory anchors** (bathroom floor at 3 AM, cage door, wedding feast) — render vividly (*холодный пол в три часа ночи*, *дверь клетки*).
- **Localize culture-bound daily-life anchors** (carpool, diner, prom, credit-card debt) to emotionally equivalent native scenes (*кухонный стол*, *дорога домой*, *долги, что давят*) — do NOT localize away the global/heaven imagery (every-tribe, throne, Lamb, wedding feast).
- **Loanword/register misreads:** *рок* (fate/doom AND rock-music) — avoid in sung text near *судьба*; *шанс/удача* (luck) must never cause salvation; *вино* loaded (see §1A); *парти* flat → *праздник*.

## 9. WORKED EXAMPLE (chorus)

**EN (Jubilujah, T1 chorus):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus is the reason we are dancing now! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / The King is coming back — somehow, somehow!

**RU (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, communal "we" to dodge gender, frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Иисус — наша радость, ликуем сейчас! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Вернётся наш Царь — Он услышит нас!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim, same beat; **Иисус** (consistent name); "the reason we are dancing now" → *Иисус — наша радость, ликуем сейчас* — **ликуем** ("we exult/rejoice") replaces literal "танцуем," which can read frivolous to Orthodox-leaning ears (§1A taboo flip), and the **"we" form sidesteps narrator gender** entirely; "the King is coming back" → *Вернётся наш Царь* (majestic, biblical *Царь*); rhyme *сейчас / нас* is a real, natural pair; reverent-warm temperature; zero offense across traditions. ё/stress checked; open vowels on held notes (*сейчА́с, нА́с*).

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0B):**
- ❌ Forced / wrong aspect / fate-theology: *"Иисус нас спасал, и судьба нам дала́ этот шанс!"* — *спасал* (imperfective = "was saving," wrong for the finished work), and *судьба… шанс* credits **fate/luck** with salvation (forbidden, §0B-9), with *шанс* jammed in to rhyme.
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"Иисус нас спас, и дарует нам жизнь без конца!"* — *спас* (perfective, accomplished), grace from Christ alone, real words, sound doctrine, rhymes *конца* with a natural line-partner.
- ✅ Also fine (rhyme dropped): *"Иисус нас спас — и Царство Его уже здесь."* — no end-rhyme, but clear, true, and natural. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**

## 10. ADDICTIVENESS / RE-LISTENABILITY PASS (after the album is produced in Russian)

Run AFTER the Russian album is produced. This tunes the **English `Style` block (§6)** and re-gen prompts ONLY — never doctrine, never the locked translation.

**Three listen passes:**
1. **Feeling pass** — does it move you emotionally on first listen? Mark where attention drifts.
2. **Craft pass** — hook clarity, vocal delivery, mix, arrangement; note weak bars.
3. **Replay-triggers pass** — what makes you want to hear it again (the loop point, an ear-candy moment, a payoff)?

**Then amplify:**
- **Hook & delivery** — make the hook land harder and earlier; sharpen the lead vocal phrasing.
- **Groove & pulse** — target a head-nod pocket (~100–128 BPM where the style allows); tighten drums/bass.
- **Texture & ear-candy** — add culturally resonant instrumentation from §1A where it fits (Slavic textures: bayan, domra/balalaika, low male-bass / Orthodox-style harmony, bell tones) plus tasteful production candy.
- **Dynamic arc & payoff** — build → drop → final-chorus lift; ensure a clear emotional climax.
- **Loop point & album flow** — make the end invite a restart; sequence tracks for momentum.
- **Emotional pull & relatability** — lean on §1A's frame (honor-shame / guilt-innocence) and the ache word **тоска** to deepen the emotional hook.

**Re-rate each track + the album BEFORE/AFTER** across: hook strength, groove, replay value, dynamic payoff, emotional pull, loop-ability. Deliver a summary of changes, **why each boosts re-listening**, and the measurable before→after effect.
