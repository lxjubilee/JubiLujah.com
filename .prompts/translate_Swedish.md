## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to meaning & the gospel** — theology must survive intact (the cross, the resurrection, the name of Jesus, repentance, the King's return); never alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels fit the original melody. A line a native can't sing comfortably has failed, even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Swedish** — sounds like a native Swedish believer wrote it; no calques, no English word order (Swedish is **verb-second / V2** — the finite verb is the second element in main clauses); uses the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the concrete picture; swap an image only if untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook** — match the rhyme scheme ONLY with real, sensible words; always keep the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** every line must make complete, natural sense to a native Swedish speaker *with the rhyme ignored.* **Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement.** Ship a clear unrhymed/near-rhymed true line over a forced one. Never invent a word, bend grammar, or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. If no honest rhyme fits, **DROP IT.** Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line (delete the final rhyme word; does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?).
>
> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to the culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect.

## 0A. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (check every line)

These are the exact failure modes that make a Swedish believer instantly hear "translated English." Check every line.

**1. No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** Never end a line with a word that exists *only* to rhyme. Real Swedish offenders to watch: stranded particles **"ju," "väl," "nog," "då," "nu," "minsann"** jammed at line-end to fill a beat; archaic/elevated tags **"förvisso," "fordom," "städse" (always), "ack"** dropped in only for rhyme; throwaway **"o Gud"** as a rhyme-plug; emphatic **"sannerligen"** padded to fill a bar. **Test:** cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no real meaning, cut it and recast.

**2. Only REAL words & correct forms.** Keep Swedish verb forms correct — Swedish verbs do **not** conjugate for person (one form per tense: *jag är, du är, han är*; *jag sjunger, vi sjunger*), so don't invent person-endings by analogy with English/German. Past tense and supine must be right (*sjunga → sjöng → sjungit*; *gå → gick → gått*; never *"gådde," "sjungde"*). Don't drop the supine after *har/hade* (*"har frälst," "har sjungit"* — not *"har frälsa"*).

**3. Correct prepositions & set phrases — no calques.** Swedish prepositions don't map 1:1 to English. Caught-style traps: *"vänta **för** dig"* (English "wait for") → **"vänta **på** dig"**; *"tänka **om** dig"* in the remembering sense → **"tänka **på** dig"**; *"beroende **på**"* is correct for "dependent on" — but *"lita **på**"* (trust in/rely on) NOT *"lita i"*; *"full **av** nåd"* is fine; the English calque *"Det gör ingen mening"* → **"Det är ingen idé"** / **"Det betyder inget."** *"Jag saknar dig"* (I miss you) NOT *"jag missar dig"* (= I miss/skip it).

**4. No English-calque word order or tech/telephone-metaphor images.** Swedish is V2: when a line starts with anything other than the subject (an adverb, object, time phrase), the verb still comes second and the subject **inverts** after it (*"Nu **sjunger vi**"* not *"Nu vi sjunger"*; *"I dag **kommer Kungen**"*). Never copy English SVO after a fronted element. Don't render "reach out" literally; don't render "on the line to heaven" as a phone line → **"jag ropar mot himlen."**

**5. Loanwords/register that misread in Swedish.** False friends: English **"eventually"** ≠ Swedish **eventuellt** (= *possibly*; "eventually" → *till slut/så småningom*); **"actual"** ≠ **aktuell** (= *current/topical*; → *verklig/faktisk*); **"gift"** = Swedish **gift** (= *married* OR *poison*! — never the English "present" sense → use *gåva*); **"barn"** = Swedish *child* (not a farm building); **"slut"** = Swedish *end/finished* (looks vulgar to an English eye but is normal Swedish — fine in Swedish, just be aware in mixed contexts); **"kock"** = chef. Don't drop casual Anglicisms (*cool, chilla, najs, vibe*) or slang into a reverent line. Keep register elevated, not vardaglig-casual.

**6. Adjective & noun agreement must hold.** Swedish has two genders (**en**-words / **ett**-words) and the adjective inflects: *"en helig Gud"* (en-word) vs *"ett heligt ord"* (ett-word, +t) vs plural/definite *"den helige Guden / heliga"* (+a/+e). A rhyme-chosen adjective that breaks gender/number is instantly wrong — fix the rhyme partner, not the grammar. The **definite suffix** is attached (*kung → kungen, nåd → nåden*) — don't strand a free-floating article.

**7. No grammatically stranded tails.** Don't strand a dangling genitive or relative clause with no head. Every word needs a grammatical home in the line or the next.

**8. Imagery internally consistent.** Don't append an image that fights the scene (cold floor + a sudden *låga/eld* only for rhyme) or introduce an object with no antecedent. Match the picture already established (the dawn = *gryningen*, not a random *blomma*).

**9. Theology over rhyme.** No rhyme word may distort doctrine. **"Tur/lycka"** = luck/fortune/happiness — keep luck/chance subordinate to God's sovereignty (prefer **"välsignelse," "nåd," "frälsning"**); don't imply salvation is luck. **"Öde"** (fate/destiny) must never replace God's providence. Grace comes from Christ, never circumstance.

**The DROP-THE-RHYME TEST (run on EVERY line):** silently delete the final rhyme word. Does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use? If no — rewrite meaning-first, even with no rhyme.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Sweden is **one of the most secular societies on earth**, yet retains a deep Lutheran cultural backbone: the **Svenska kyrkan** (Church of Sweden, Lutheran — the former state church, to which a large share of Swedes still nominally belong and which shapes life-rituals: baptism, confirmation, weddings, funerals, Advent/Lucia/Christmas). Active, believing faith is concentrated in the **frikyrka** stream — **Pingst (Pentecostal), EFK (Evangeliska Frikyrkan), Equmeniakyrkan, the Baptist and Mission tradition, evangelical Lutherans (EFS)** — plus a smaller **Roman Catholic** minority (growing with immigration). Jubilee/Inspire songs are CCM/evangelical in origin, but the Swedish version must **hold for the whole Swedish Body of Christ** and read with dignity in a secular culture that prizes understatement.

- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** all traditions own: *Gud, Herren, Jesus, Kristus, Fadern, den helige Ande, nåd, härlighet, Guds rike, korset, uppståndelsen, Lammet, Halleluja, Amen, frälsning.* Anchor Scripture echoes to a standard Bible: **Svenska Folkbibeln** (the literal, evangelical-favoured translation, beloved in the frikyrka) and **Bibel 2000** (the broad ecumenical/state translation). Pick one register per album. Iconic phrasings: John 19:30 → **"Det är fullbordat"**; Rev 22:20 → **"Kom, Herre Jesus"**.
- **No tradition-specific imports the source lacks.** No Marian devotion, no saint/relic veneration, no Mass-specific or high-liturgical formulas beyond what the source carries, no charismatic glossolalia naming. Christ-centered and Bible-plain is the common ground.
- **Reverent register toward God.** Address God respectfully; capitalize divine pronouns where it aids reverence (*Du, Din, Dig, Han, Hans, Honom* when referring to God — common in Swedish Christian print). Swedish dropped the formal/informal "you" distinction decades ago (the *du-reformen*) — **"du"** to God is normal and warm, not disrespectful; capitalize *Du* for reverence. No slang anywhere.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Bruden / Brudgummen*) is biblical and fine; keep it pure.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family handling:** Sweden is highly gender-egalitarian and the Svenska kyrkan is progressive on inclusive language, while the frikyrka is more traditional. **Defer to THIS community's norms** — choose the broadest, most natural Scripture-plain form; do not import the source culture's gender debates, and avoid forcing the gender-neutral pronoun *hen* into divine reference (God keeps *Han/Fadern*).

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP (the relatability layer)

- **Lead with the culture's worldview frame: guilt–innocence** (Lutheran heritage) — *skuld* (guilt/debt), *nåd* (grace), *förlåtelse* (forgiveness), *rättfärdiggörelse* (justification). **Foreground these images:** sin as debt → **debt paid / skulden betald**; the conscience accused → **acquitted (frikänd)**; declared righteous; a clean conscience (*ett rent samvete*); peace with God. BUT note a strong secular overlay: many Swedes feel guilt/shame around *otillräcklighet* (not-being-enough), performance, and isolation — the gospel of grace as **rest from striving / unconditional acceptance** lands powerfully. The cross as the place guilt is settled, and grace as gift not achievement, are the deepest entries.
- **Taboo/danger images → safe native substitutes:**
  - **"Buddy/homeboy Jesus"** / over-casual *"tjena Jesus, kompis"* → keep warmth but reverent: *"Jesus, min vän"* / *"min Frälsare"* — never matey slang. Swedish secular reflex distrusts gushing piety; sincere understatement reads as MORE credible than hype.
  - **Loud triumphalism / hard-sell evangelism** reads poorly in a culture shaped by *Jantelagen* (the unwritten "don't think you're better/special" social code) — avoid boastful, superior, or pushy framing; humility and honesty land.
  - **Triumphalist warfare / "conquering nations"** — prefer victory **over sin and death** (*seger över synd och död*) to militaristic/nationalistic conquest; Sweden's long peace and anti-militarist culture make army-march imagery feel off.
  - **King/Kingdom** is natural (*Kung/Konung, Guds rike*) — Sweden has a beloved monarchy, so royal imagery reads warm; *Guds rike* is core vocabulary.
  - **"Dancing before the Lord"** — *dansa* is fine in frikyrka/Pentecostal settings; in Svenska kyrkan worship it's less native (restrained) — *jubla, prisa, sjunga av glädje* carry the energy broadly.
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom: "längtan"** (deep longing/yearning) — reach for it where the EN aches for God or heaven (*"längtan efter Dig"*). **"vemod"** is a gentle, beautiful melancholy/wistfulness (lighter than full grief) that pervades Swedish art and hymnody — useful for lament-tinged hope. **"hemlängtan"** (homesickness/longing for home) fits longing for heaven.
- **Worship temperature: restrained.** Default Swedish expression is **understated, sincere, melancholy-tinged, anti-hyperbolic.** A true, quiet, well-crafted line beats American-CCM superlatives — overstatement reads as fake. The frikyrka runs somewhat warmer/expressive, but even there, dignity and honesty are prized. Lean into the beautiful Swedish tradition of **lament that turns to quiet hope** (think the minor-key warmth of Swedish hymns).
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** avoid nationalistic *fosterland/blod-och-jord* framing; keep "blood" strictly the **blood of Christ** (*Jesu blod*), reverent; militarist imagery soft; *Jantelagen* means avoid superiority/boasting; don't let *lycka/tur* (happiness/luck) crowd out *nåd*.
- **Reverence/honorific forms required when addressing God:** Swedish has no honorific grammar, so reverence is carried by **register and capitalization** — use **"du/Du"** (intimate but capitalized for God), *Din/Dig/Han/Hans/Honom* capitalized. Title address: **"Herre" / "Herren,"** vocative **"o Herre," "Herre Jesus," "Fader."** Keep vocabulary elevated and clean; never slangy or casual.
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** the rich Swedish hymn tradition (**psalmboken** — *"Den blomstertid nu kommer," "Tryggare kan ingen vara," "Härlig är jorden," "O store Gud"* which became *"How Great Thou Art"*) uses **clean end-rhyme, regular meter, and nature imagery** (light, summer, dawn, the deep forest, the sea). Reach for honest end-rhyme, steady meter, and Sweden's beloved **light-out-of-darkness / nature** imagery. Note the Swedish **pitch (tonal) accent** below (§4).

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (Swedish-specific)

- **Match syllable count; compress with the elisions Swedish singers actually use.** Drop weak endings and use clitics/contractions: ***'n* (en), *'t* (det), *sen* (sedan), *nån/nåt/nånstans* (någon/något/...), *å* (och, colloquial), *re'n* (redan), *ta'r/gå'r* shortened, *je'/ja'* for jag in fast lines.*** Elide vowel clashes (*"så att" → "så'tt"* only if natural). Prefer a short native synonym over padding. Never pad with filler particles (*ju, väl, nog*) to fill a bar.
- **Respect Swedish stress AND pitch accent.** Swedish is a **pitch-accent (tonal-ish) language**: most words carry **accent 1 (acute)** or **accent 2 (grave, the characteristic "two-peak" melody)**. This is real and audible — *anden* with accent 1 = "the duck," with accent 2 = "the spirit"! Stress is usually on the **first/root syllable** (*NÅ-den, HÄR-lig-het, KO-nung, FRÄL-sa-ren, GLÄD-je*); some loans shift (*Halle-LU-ja*). Put the stressed syllable on the strong beat. Because pitch accent rides the melody, **don't fight a word's natural rise/fall** — choose words whose stress lands cleanly on the beat and whose melody doesn't clash awkwardly with the tune. Read aloud in rhythm.
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes.** Land big held notes on open vowels — *-a, -o, -å, -e* (*nåden, härlighet, Halleluja, glädje*). Swedish has **long vs short vowels** that change meaning (*"Gud"* long-u vs short; *"vit/vitt"*) — put a **long vowel** on a sustained note (a long held note on a short-vowel word sounds wrong/comic). The hook "Jubiluyah" ends open (-ah) — exploit it.
- **The å/ä/ö caveat for AI vocals:** the vowels **å [oː], ä [ɛ], ö [ø]** are distinct phonemes — if you write bare *a/a/o* instead, Suno/TTS will mis-sing the word and may change its meaning (*"far"* = father/go vs *"får"* = sheep/may; *"hal"* vs *"häl"* vs *"hål"*). **Always write å, ä, ö in full** in sung text and titles. Avoid stacking the **"sj/stj/skj/tj" (sje-/tje-sounds)** on fast runs — they can smear in TTS.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot** (lands at 0:00–0:08). The chorus arrives by the same bar.
- **Rhyme stance for Swedish: USE end-rhyme (the psalmbok ear expects it), but never force it.** Aim to preserve AABB/ABAB **with words that genuinely belong.** The moment rhyme demands an invented word, a filler particle (*ju, väl, minsann, förvisso*), a broken inflection, or distorted meaning — **abandon it**: near-rhyme, assonance, or recast the OTHER line. A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.
- **Reach for the native poetic device (§1A):** regular hymn meter, clean end-rhyme, Sweden's light/nature/dawn imagery, and the lament-to-hope arc.
- **Diacritics mandatory** (å, ä, ö, and é where it appears, e.g. *idé, armé*). Suno/TTS pronounce from text — bare vowels mis-sing and can change meaning. Always full diacritics in sung body and titles.
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing the line aloud (slurred, as an AI vocal would) and check blurred syllables don't form an unintended vulgar/comic word, and that long/short-vowel and pitch-accent minimal pairs aren't garbled (*anden* duck vs spirit!). Re-voice if so.

- **Do NOT localize away the global/heaven imagery** — every-tribe, throne, Lamb, wedding feast, New Jerusalem stay intact.

## 9. WORKED EXAMPLE (chorus)

**EN (Jubilujah, T1 chorus):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus is the reason we are dancing now! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / The King is coming back — somehow, somehow!

**Swedish (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus är skälet vi jublar här och nu! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Konungen kommer åter — på nytt, på nytt!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim and on the same beat; *Jesus* (consistent name); "Jesus is the reason we are dancing now" → *Jesus är skälet vi jublar här och nu* — I rendered "dancing" as **jubla** ("rejoice/shout for joy"), which fits the restrained Swedish worship temperature and reads natively across Svenska kyrkan and frikyrka alike (§1A), and SAID so; word order is V2-natural; "the King is coming back" → *Konungen kommer åter* (majestic *Konungen*, reverent); the resolve "somehow, somehow" → *på nytt, på nytt* ("anew, anew") keeps the repeated, hopeful tail; *nu / nytt* is honest assonance (true near-rhyme over forced); zero offense across traditions, no boastfulness.

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0A):** suppose you need a rhyme for *"nåd"* (grace).
- ❌ **Forced:** *"…vår tur gav oss nåd, förvisso, på rad!"* — *tur* (luck) implies salvation is luck (bad theology, §0A.9), *förvisso* is archaic filler, *på rad* ("in a row") is jammed in only to rhyme.
- ✅ **Meaning-first (rhyme kept):** *"…på korset ger Jesus oss nåd — han bär all skuld och skada."* — real words, sound doctrine (grace from Christ at the cross), *nåd/skada* honest near-rhyme, guilt–innocence frame (He bears all guilt and harm).
- ✅ **Also fine (rhyme dropped):** *"…på korset ger Jesus oss nåd; min skuld är helt betald."* — no rhyme, but vivid guilt–innocence imagery (my debt fully paid) and crystal-clear. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**

## 10. ADDICTIVENESS / RE-LISTENABILITY PASS (after the album is produced in Swedish)

After the Swedish album is produced, run the full addictiveness pass. This tunes the **English `Style` block (§6)** and the Suno re-gen prompts ONLY — **never** doctrine or the locked translation.

**Three listen passes:**
1. **Feeling pass** — listen as a normal Swedish believer (and as a secular Swede). Where does it move you? Where does attention drift? Does the *längtan*/guilt–innocence (grace-vs-striving) payload land (§1A)?
2. **Craft pass** — hook strength, groove/pocket, vocal delivery, arrangement, mix clarity, ear-candy.
3. **Replay-trigger pass** — what makes you hit repeat? Identify the most addictive 2–4 seconds; make sure it's not buried.

**Amplify (via the English Style block + re-gen only):**
- **Hook & delivery** — make "Jubilujah"/the chorus hook land harder and earlier; sharpen vocal attack while keeping Swedish understatement.
- **Groove & pulse** — target a head-nod pocket (~100–128 BPM where the style allows; keep hymnic/folk tracks at their proper tempo).
- **Texture & ear-candy** — tasteful, culturally resonant instrumentation (Nordic folk textures, organ/choir for hymnic; modern Scandi-pop production for CCM) and small surprises; lean on minor-to-major lifts Swedes love.
- **Dynamic arc & payoff** — restrained verse → fuller chorus → bridge lift → final-chorus payoff (Swedish restraint rewards a delayed, earned swell; the lament-to-hope arc).
- **Loop point & album flow** — make the end invite a replay; sequence the album to flow.
- **Emotional pull & relatability** — using §1A's guilt–innocence frame and the *längtan/vemod* ache words; the light-out-of-winter-darkness arc.

**Re-rate each track + the album BEFORE/AFTER** across **hook strength, groove, replay value, dynamic payoff, emotional pull, loop-ability** (e.g. 1–10). Deliver a summary of changes, why each boosts re-listening, and the measurable before/after effect.
