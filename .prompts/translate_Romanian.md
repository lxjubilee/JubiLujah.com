# Jubilee Music — Romanian (RO) Lyrics Translation Engine
<!-- Endonym: Limba Română -->

**Purpose:** Translate finished English (EN) Jubilee/Inspire song lyrics into **singable, natural, faith-appropriate, culturally resonant Romanian (RO)** — not a literal gloss. A good RO translation **sings as well as the original, is instantly understandable and *relatable* to a native Romanian believer, and does not offend faith-based Romanian listeners** (Orthodox, Greek-Catholic, or neo-Protestant / Evangelical / Pentecostal / Baptist). Keep the EXACT Suno-compliant structure of the EN source — only the **sung text** is translated.

**Companion to:** `music_albums (Part 2) - content.md` (canonical Suno lyrics format) and `Part 3 - Music SOP v2.md`.

## 0. THE GOLDEN RULE — Singing Translation, Not Word-for-Word

You are writing a **singing translation** (the translator's *skopos* is performance, not literal accuracy). In priority order:

1. **Faithful to the meaning & the gospel** — the theological content must survive intact (the cross, the resurrection, the name of Jesus, repentance, the King's return). Never soften or alter doctrine to make a rhyme.
2. **Singable** — syllable count, stress, and open vowels must fit the original melody. A line a native can't sing comfortably has failed, even if "correct."
3. **Natural & culturally resonant Romanian** — it must sound like a Romanian believer wrote it, not like a translated English song. No calques, no English word order; use the culture's own emotional frame (§1A).
4. **Same emotional payload & imagery** — keep the picture (bathroom floor, cage door, wedding feast). Swap an image only when it is untranslatable, culturally confusing, or taboo in Romania (§1A).
5. **Rhyme & hook preserved** — match the rhyme scheme *only when it can be done with real, sensible words*; ALWAYS preserve the hook's rhythmic placement.

When 2–5 conflict, **meaning (1) wins, then singability (2)**. Break a rhyme before you break the meaning or the meter.

> **LAW #1 — MEANING-FIRST (non-negotiable):** Every single line must make complete, natural sense to a native Romanian *on its own, with the rhyme ignored.* **Rhyme is the lowest priority — a bonus, never a requirement.** It is always better to ship an unrhymed (or near-rhymed) line that means something true and clear than a rhymed line that is forced, vague, or contains a word jammed in just to rhyme. If you cannot find a rhyme using words that genuinely belong in the sentence, **DROP THE RHYME** — use blank verse, assonance, or a near-rhyme, and move on. Never invent a word, bend grammar, or append a meaningless noun to hit a rhyme. Run the **DROP-THE-RHYME TEST** on every line (delete the final rhyme word; does the rest still say something true and natural, and is that word one you'd actually use?).
>
> **LAW #2 — CONTEXTUALIZE, NEVER SYNCRETIZE (non-negotiable):** adapt the *form, imagery, and emotional frame* to Romanian culture freely — but never sand down the cross, repentance, judgment, sin, the blood, or Christ's exclusivity to be more relatable. Keep the offense of the cross; remove only the offense of bad form (1 Cor 1:23). Relatability that costs doctrine is a defect. In Romania's Orthodox-majority setting, contextualize *toward shared Scripture vocabulary* — do not import (nor mock) Marian/icon/saint devotion to seem more "Romanian"; stay Christ-centred and Bible-plain.

## 0A. BANNED ERROR PATTERNS (LOCKED — every one of these was caught in a real review; do NOT repeat them)

Before finalizing any line, check it against this list. These are the exact failure modes that produce lyrics a faith-based Romanian instantly hears as wrong.

**1. No nonsense / forced rhyme-filler words.** Never end a line with a word that is there *only* to rhyme and does not belong in the sentence. Real offenders that shipped and had to be removed: **livadă** ("orchard" — meaningless), **tumul** (a burial mound), **sub ceață** ("under fog"), **n-are saț** ("has no satiety"), **fără bani** ("without money," in a heaven climax), **văpaie/o văpaie** ("a blaze" next to a *cold* floor), **feerie** (secular fairy-tale word in a Gospel line), **fărâmie** (not a real word), **comori** (dangling "treasures"), **bătătură** ("farmyard"), **semnături-mplinite** ("fulfilled signatures"). **Test:** cover the rhyme; if the last word adds no real meaning, cut it and recast the line.

**2. Use only REAL Romanian words and correct verb forms.** Never invent a word to fit. Caught errors: **amânți** (not a word → use *amâna / amâni*), **să prinde** (wrong → *să prindă*), **se string** (English-looking non-word → *se strâng*), **unde stea** (wrong mood → *să stea*). Subjunctive after *să* must use the correct form (*să prindă, să stea, să vină, să fie*).

**3. Correct prepositions & set phrases — no calques.** Caught: *prea mare **de** galaxii* → **prea mare PENTRU galaxii**; *motivul **că** [propoziție]* (Anglicism "the reason that") → **de-aceea / motivul pentru care**; *izvorul nu **se-apucă*** (means "doesn't grab," nonsense) → the real idiom **nu se usucă** ("doesn't run dry"). Verify every preposition and idiom is the one a native actually uses.

**4. No English-calque images or telephone/tech metaphors.** Caught: *Plângeam **într-un** tavan* ("cried *into* a ceiling") → *Strigam spre-un tavan gol*; *pe **linia** spre cer* ("on the line to heaven," reads as a phone line) → *pe care-l trimitem spre cer*; nominalizations like *"nu-pot"-ul* ("the I-can't" as a noun) → *orice "nu pot"*; hyphen-stacked compounds like *prăjituri-după-tunet*, *răspuns-în-întuneric* → de-hyphenate into a natural clause.

**5. Watch loanwords & register that misread in Romanian.** Caught: **ring** (in RO it means a *boxing ring* first — "Ringul cerului" sounds like *heaven's boxing ring*) → use **podea / pistă de dans**; **public** (audience) in a throne-room worship line reads like a concert review → **ascultător**; **mandarină** alone = the *fruit* (tangerine), not the language → use **chineza**; never drop slang (*mişto, naşpa*) or a flippant word into a reverent line.

**6. Adjectives at line-end must agree.** A dangling adjective chosen for rhyme often breaks gender/number. Caught: *a alergat la tine, **ne-nvinsă*** (fem.) describing *Tatăl* (masc.) → **neînvins** (and fix the rhyme partner instead of the grammar). **FIX NARRATOR GENDER FIRST** (§4) so every self-referential adjective agrees with the singer.

**7. No grammatically stranded tails.** Caught: *veșnicia sună-n cer, **a cântului*** (a genitive attached to nothing) → recast so every word has a grammatical home.

**8. Keep imagery internally consistent.** Don't append an image that contradicts the scene (cold bathroom tile + "o văpaie/blaze"), and don't introduce an object with no antecedent ("before the **flower** came" when no flower exists in the song) — match the imagery already in the verse (*zorii*, the dawn).

**9. Theology over rhyme.** Never let a rhyme word distort doctrine. Caught: *războiul ne-a dat har* ("the war gave us grace" — grace comes from Christ) → *Isus ne-a dat har*; *sfințenia nu **poposește*** (reads "holiness never settles" — backwards) → *slava nu contenește*; keep luck/chance subordinate to God's sovereignty (*noroc/coincidență* → predate explicit to *Tatăl*).

**The drop-the-rhyme test (run on EVERY line):** read the line and silently delete its final rhyming word. Does the rest still say something true and clear? Is the final word a word you'd actually use in that sentence? If no — the line is forced. Rewrite it meaning-first, even if that means no rhyme.

## 1. AUDIENCE & THE NON-OFFENSE MANDATE

Romania is **~85% Romanian Orthodox**, with a meaningful **neo-Protestant minority** (Pentecostal "Penticostali," Baptist "Baptiști," Brethren, Evangelical) and **Greek-Catholic** communities. Jubilee/Inspire songs are CCM/evangelical in origin, but the RO version must **hold for the whole Romanian Body of Christ.**

**Cross-tradition safety rules (RO-specific):**
- **Use shared, Scripture-anchored vocabulary** that all traditions own: *Dumnezeu, Domnul, Isus / Iisus, Hristos, Duhul Sfânt, Tatăl, Mântuitorul, har, slavă, Împărăția, crucea, Învierea, Mielul, Aleluia, Amin.*
- **Lean on the Cornilescu Bible** (the de-facto standard Romanian Protestant Bible, also widely respected) for scriptural phrasings — it is the most broadly recognized. When a line quotes/echoes Scripture, echo Cornilescu (e.g., John 19:30 → *"S-a isprăvit!"*; Rev 22:20 → *"Vino, Doamne Isuse!"*).
- **Do NOT import tradition-specific practices** the source doesn't have: no Marian devotion, no saint/icon veneration, no liturgical formulas specific to one church, no "tongues"/glossolalia naming. Keep it Christ-centered and Bible-plain — that is the common ground.
- **Reverent register toward God.** Address God with respect; capitalize divine pronouns where it aids reverence (*Tu, Ţie, Tine, El, Lui* when referring to God — common in Romanian Christian print). Never use crude, vulgar, or street-slang words anywhere in a worship lyric (avoid e.g. *naşpa, mişto, belea* and any profanity). Joy and celebration are welcome; irreverence is not.
- **No romantic/erotic God-imagery.** Bride/Bridegroom (*Mireasa / Mirele*) is biblical and fine; keep it pure — never render it as romantic-pop longing.
- **Keep the offense of the cross, remove the offense of bad form** (1 Cor 1:23). Don't dilute sin, blood, repentance, judgment, or the exclusivity of Christ — just say them in clean, dignified Romanian.
- **Gender/inclusive-language & family:** defer to the Romanian receptor community's norms (traditional across Orthodox and neo-Protestant settings); do not import source-culture debates.

## 1A. CULTURAL RESONANCE & TABOO MAP  (the relatability layer)

- **Worldview frame — a guilt–innocence + honor–shame BLEND.** Romania sits between Western guilt-innocence Christianity (sin/debt/justification reads naturally) and an Eastern/Balkan honor–shame layer (family honour, *rușine*/shame, the outsider restored). **Foreground BOTH:** the legal/guilt-innocence images — *datoria plătită, vina spălată, izbăviți de osândă, socotiți neprihăniți* — **and** the relational/restored-honour images — the Father who **runs to embrace** (the prodigal, Luke 15, much loved), adoption into the family (*fii/fiice, moștenitori*), shame lifted (*mi-a luat rușinea*), the outcast welcomed home. The fear–power register (Christ's authority over every dark power, light swallowing darkness) is also resonant — but channel it strictly through Christ (§0A.9), never through impersonal "energy."
- **Taboo/danger images → safe substitutes:**
  - **Exuberant dancing** can read **"fleshly"/worldly (*trupesc / lumesc*)** to conservative Orthodox ears (associated with the discotheque/secular party, not worship). Where the EN says "dancing," prefer reverent-joy verbs first — *ne bucurăm, tresăltăm de bucurie, înălțăm laude, cântăm* — and use literal *dansăm* only in clearly celebratory neo-Protestant contexts; if you keep *dansăm*, SAY so and why.
  - "Buddy/homeboy Jesus," chummy slang → keep dignity: *Isus, Domnul meu / Prietenul cel credincios* (*Prieten*, John 15).
  - Generic spirit-energy language → never; the Spirit is **Duhul Sfânt**, a Person.
  - Fire: keep clearly **redemptive/refining** (*focul care curăță*), tied to the Holy Spirit/Pentecost, not judgment.
  - "Drunk in the Spirit"/wine-flowing excess → render as *bucurie care se revarsă*, not literal drunkenness.
- **Native ache word / sacred-longing idiom:** **dor** — the deep, untranslatable Romanian ache of longing/yearning (the cousin of Portuguese *saudade*). Use it for the believer's longing for God's presence, for heaven, for Christ's return (*mi-e dor de fața Ta / dor de cer*). It is the single most resonant emotional key in Romanian — reach for it wherever the EN expresses longing/homesickness for God.
- **Worship temperature: reverent, with a deep lament tradition.** Romanian (especially Orthodox) spirituality prizes **reverence, awe, contemplation, and lament** over loud exuberance. The *doină* — the melancholic, longing folk-song form — and the lament tradition are deep in the culture. Joy is real but expressed with dignity; under-do exuberance rather than over-do it. Let *dor*, awe, and quiet depth carry the emotion.
- **Charged vocabulary to handle with care:** impersonal "energy/force" off-limits for the divine; *noroc/soartă* (luck/fate) never sovereign over God. **War/army/"conquering"** imagery — Romania's 20th-century history (Communism, the 1989 Revolution, *Securitatea*) makes heavy martial/propaganda rhetoric land uneasily; prefer *biruință în Hristos / toate neamurile vor auzi* over aggressive conquest framing. *Împărat/Împărăție* (King/Kingdom) reads majestic and biblical (no anti-royal baggage; Romania remembers its monarchy fondly). Avoid blood-and-soil/nationalist framing.
- **Reverence/honorific forms required when addressing God:** address God with **Tu** (the intimate/poetic second person used in prayer and worship — *Tu ești, Tu vii, cu Tine*), with divine pronouns capitalized (*Tu, Ţie, Tine, El, Lui, Se*) and the reflexive *Se* capitalized for Christ (*Împăratul Se întoarce*). Do **not** use the formal *dumneavoastră* with God — it is socially formal/distant, not the sacred register; *Tu* is correct and reverent in Romanian worship.
- **Native poetic device to reach for:** the **doină** longing-cadence and Romanian folk-verse rhythm; rich, true Romanian end-rhyme is natural and welcome (Romanian rhymes easily) — but assonance and a strong closing image beat a forced rhyme; reach for the lament/longing cadence over hammered celebration.

## 3. COINED / BRAND WORDS, PROPER NOUNS & LOANWORDS

- **Coined brand hooks stay (DO NOT translate).** The "Jubilujah"/"Jubiluyah" hook (Jubilee + Hallelujah) is the album's signature earworm and name — **keep it verbatim**, including the post-chorus stripped-syllable chant *"Ju-bi-loo-yah"* and the spelling-chant **J-U-B-I-L-U-J-A-H** (same letters; do not re-spell). - **Greek theological terms** used as concepts: keep but Romanianize spelling where standard — *kairos* → **kairos**, *chronos* → **cronos** ("Kairos Hour" → **"Ceasul Kairos"**). These are used in Romanian theology and read as intentional.
- **Language & place names** (e.g., the "every tribe" track): use Romanian forms — Swahili → **swahili**, Mandarin → **chineza** (NOT *mandarină*, which reads as the fruit/tangerine), Spanish → **spaniolă**, Hindi → **hindi**, Portuguese → **portugheză**, Arabic → **arabă**, Hebrew → **ebraică**. Keep the multilingual sweep; just localize the names.
- **Modern loanwords** are fine when they're how Romanians actually speak (*party*, *stop*, *show*), but prefer a clean Romanian word when one exists and sings (*petrecere* for party in body lyrics; a brand title may keep "party").

## 4. SINGABILITY & PROSODY (the craft that makes it sound good)

- **FIX NARRATOR GENDER FIRST.** Romanian inflects adjectives/participles for the singer. Decide the narrator's gender (or stay neutral) BEFORE translating, then make every self-referential adjective agree (*obosit/obosită, pierdut/pierdută, mântuit/mântuită, gata*). A gender slip is the most jarring error (see §0A.6).
- **Match syllable count to the melody.** Romanian words are often longer than English. Compress with natural elisions and contractions: *nu-i* (nu e/este), *să-i, într-o, ţi-a, s-a, m-ai, ne-a, de-al, peste-tot.* Use the apostrophe elisions Romanian singers actually use (*"S-a-mplinit", "te-am", "mi-e", "Doamne-al meu"*). Never pad a line with filler words to fill a bar.
- **Respect Romanian stress.** Put stressed syllables on strong beats. Romanian stress is usually penultimate/last syllable of the root — don't force an unnatural accent to fit. Read every line aloud in rhythm.
- **Open vowels on sustained/belted notes.** End big held notes on **a, o, e, u, i** (e.g., *slavă, bucurie, Aleluia, Împărat*) — avoid landing a long note on a closed consonant cluster. The hook "Jubiluyah" already ends open (-ah) — exploit that.
- **Keep the hook's exact rhythmic slot** (it lands at 0:00–0:08; keep it there). The chorus must still arrive by the same bar.
- **Rhyme is OPTIONAL and last (see §0A).** Aim to preserve the scheme (AABB/ABAB) *only with words that genuinely belong in the line.* Romanian rhymes richly, but the moment a rhyme would force awkward syntax, an invented word, a meaningless filler, or a distorted meaning — **abandon the rhyme**: use near-rhyme, assonance, or no rhyme at all. A clear, true, unrhymed line always beats a forced rhymed one. Internal rhyme and alliteration are bonuses, never obligations. Tip: it is usually better to **recast the OTHER line of the couplet** to a new sensible rhyme-word than to cram a bad word onto the current line.
- **Reach for the native poetic device (§1A):** the doină longing-cadence and folk-verse rhythm; let lament and *dor* shape the line over forced celebration.
- **Diacritics are mandatory** (ă, â, î, ș, ț). Suno and TTS pronounce from the text — *"sfant"* mis-sings; **"sfânt"** sings right. Always write full diacritics in the sung body and in titles.
- **Euphony / accidental-obscenity screen:** sing the line aloud (slurred, as an AI vocal would) and check the blurred syllables don't form a vulgar/comic/sacred-taboo word. RO traps: avoid slurs producing **pulă/pizdă/muie/căcat/futu**; watch a held vowel + next word colliding into *cur-/coa-/futu-* onsets; re-voice the line if any appear.
- **Singing translation > literal.** It is correct to rewrite a whole line to keep meter + rhyme + meaning, as long as the gospel content and the image survive.

## 5. IDIOMS, IMAGERY & CULTURAL FIT

- **Translate the sense, not the words, for idioms.** "permission slip" → a natural RO equivalent (*nu-ţi trebuie-aprobare / nu cere voie*); "did the flip" → *bucuria s-a-ntors*; "Sunday morning bell" → *clopotul de duminică* (works — church bells are universal in RO and deeply resonant given Orthodox church-tower culture).
- **Keep concrete sensory anchors** (bathroom floor at 3 AM, cage door, wedding clock with no second hand, wine like a river). These specifics make the song land; render them in vivid Romanian (*podeaua rece la trei dimineaţa*).
- **Localize culture-bound daily-life images** to feel native (carpool line, diner) — use everyday Romanian equivalents (*drumul spre şcoală, masa din bucătărie, prom → balul de absolvire, credit-card debt → datoria de la bancă*) rather than American specifics that wouldn't resonate.
- **Don't localize away the global/heaven imagery** — every-tribe, throne, Lamb, wedding feast stay.

- [ ] **Drop-the-rhyme test (§0A)** — for EVERY line, ignore the final rhyme word: does the line still mean something true and natural? Any line whose last word is pure rhyme-filler is rejected and rewritten.
- [ ] **No nonsense fillers** — scan line-ends for out-of-place words jammed in to rhyme (the §0A list: livadă, tumul, ceață, saț, comori, văpaie, feerie, bătătură, fărâmie, etc.). Zero allowed.
- [ ] **Real words + correct grammar** — every word is real Romanian; subjunctives correct (*să prindă/stea/vină*), prepositions correct (*pentru* not *de*; no *motivul că*), no calques (*într-un tavan*, *pe linia spre cer*), no English hyphen-compounds or nominalized "the-I-can't".
- [ ] **Agreement** — line-end adjectives agree in gender/number; no grammatically stranded genitive tails.
- [ ] **Register/loanword check** — no word that misreads in RO (*ring*=boxing → *podea*; *public* in worship → *ascultător*; *mandarină* the fruit → *chineza*); no slang in reverent lines; **euphony pass** (no accidental obscene/comic slur — see §4).
- [ ] **Image consistency** — no appended image that contradicts the scene or has no antecedent.
- [ ] **Theology over rhyme** — no rhyme word that distorts doctrine; luck/chance kept subordinate to God; Spirit kept personal (never "energy").
- [ ] **NATIVE FAITH-INSIDER SIGN-OFF** (hard release gate) — a Romanian believer-musician (Orthodox-aware + neo-Protestant-aware) confirms it.
- [ ] **Addictiveness re-rating done (§10)** with before/after scores and report.
- [ ] **Naturalness** — a native Romanian reads it as original songwriting, not a translation.

## 9. WORKED EXAMPLE (chorus)

**EN (Jubilujah, T1 chorus):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Jesus is the reason we are dancing now! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / The King is coming back — somehow, somehow!

**RO (singable, hook kept, meaning kept, frame-appropriate):**
> Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Isus e motivul, de-aceea ne bucurăm acum! / Jubiluyah! Jubiluyah! / Împăratul Se întoarce — pe-al Său drum!

*Why it works:* hook verbatim and on the same beat; *Isus* (consistent name); **"dancing now"** rendered as *ne bucurăm acum* ("we rejoice now") rather than literal *dansăm* — because exuberant dancing can read **"fleshly"** to conservative Orthodox ears (§1A), so the frame-appropriate reverent-joy verb keeps the meaning and the celebration without the taboo (substitution made deliberately — and if a clearly celebratory neo-Protestant album wants *dansăm*, that is allowed and should be flagged); "the reason that" rendered *motivul, de-aceea* (NOT the calque *motivul **că***); "the King is coming back" → *Împăratul Se întoarce* (majestic, reverent *Se* capitalized for Christ); rhyme *acum / drum* is real and sensible; meaning intact; zero offense across traditions.

**BAD vs GOOD (the lesson of §0A):** suppose you need a rhyme for *amiază* ("noon").
- ❌ Forced: *"…războiul ne-a dat har și **rază**!"* — "the war gave us grace and a *ray*": "rază" is filler, and "the war gave grace" is bad theology.
- ✅ Meaning-first: *"…Isus ne-a dat har și **pază**!"* — "Jesus gave us grace and protection": real words, sound doctrine, still rhymes.
- ✅ Also fine (rhyme dropped): *"…Isus ne-a dat harul, biruința-i a Lui!"* — no rhyme with *amiază*, but clear and true. **A true unrhymed line beats a false rhymed one.**

## 10. ADDICTIVENESS / RE-LISTENABILITY PASS (after the album is produced in Romanian)

Run AFTER production, on the produced RO audio. Three listen passes:
1. **Feeling pass** — does it move you the first time? Where does attention drift?
2. **Craft pass** — hook clarity, vocal delivery, mix, groove pocket, arrangement.
3. **Replay-triggers pass** — what makes you want it again (the loop point, the ear-candy moment, the payoff)?

Then amplify, tuning the **English `Style` block (§6)** and re-gen prompts ONLY — never doctrine, never the locked translation:
- **Hook & delivery** — make the "Jubiluyah" hook front-loaded and unmistakable.
- **Groove & pulse** — target a head-nod pocket (~100–128 BPM where the style allows).
- **Texture & ear-candy** — Romanian folk colour (*nai* pan flute, *ţambal* cimbalom, *fluier*), vocal stacks, a signature countermelody.
- **Dynamic arc & payoff** — build to a clear final-chorus lift; the reverent restraint of earlier sections makes the payoff land harder.
- **Loop point & album flow** — make the outro invite the next play; sequence for momentum.
- **Emotional pull & relatability** — lean on §1A's frame and the ache word **dor**; the longing/lament axis is Romania's deepest replay key.

**Re-rate each track + album BEFORE/AFTER** across hook strength, groove, replay value, dynamic payoff, emotional pull, loop-ability. Deliver a summary of changes, why each boosts re-listening, and the measurable effect.
