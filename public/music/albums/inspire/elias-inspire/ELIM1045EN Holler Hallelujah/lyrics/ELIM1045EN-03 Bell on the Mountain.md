## Lyrics

[Intro — lone cracked bell + clawhammer banjo, ≤0:10]
Hear that bell — it's callin'…

[Verse 1]
There's a bell up on the steeple with a hairline crack,
Rings a little crooked, but it always rings you back —
Old deacon hauls the rope like he's wrestlin' a steer,
And that off-key clangin's the prettiest sound up here

[Pre-Chorus]
It ain't just iron knockin' on the evenin' air,
It's a Shepherd's whistle sayin' "son, I know you're there"

[Chorus]
Hear that bell — (it's callin'!)
Past the briar and the gully where your pride keeps fallin'
Jesus is the ringer and the ringin' is grace,
Climb the crooked shale, come and find your place

[Verse 2]
Knew a man so stubborn he'd out-mule a mule,
Swore he'd freeze on the mountain 'fore he'd play the fool —
But that cracked bell kept a-coaxin' through the sleet and stone,
And he climbed up bawlin' like a calf and didn't climb alone

[Pre-Chorus]
The lamp's still throwin' yellow on the chapel floor,
And mercy left the latch-string hangin' out the door

[Chorus]
Hear that bell — (it's chasin'!)
Like a love-struck hound dog that you can't out-race-in'
Down the dark hill bottom where the lost ones roam,
The bell don't quit till every stray walks home

[Bridge — stop-time, bell tolls in the quiet]
(bell) ...I used to think it tolled for braver men than me —
(bell) ...then it cracked the silence and it set my stubborn free —
That cracked old bell's been clangin' my name since I was born,
(full band returns) and I'm done outrunnin' the kindest sound I've known!

[Final Chorus — full, warm]
Hear that bell — (it's mine!)
Ringin' all the wanderers up the wore-down line
Jesus in the iron, and the iron sings me home —
Hear that bell on the mountain — and quit runnin', roam!

## Scoring Justification

Through-composed: the response evolves ("callin'" → "chasin'" → "mine") and every chorus image is fresh (love-struck hound, crooked shale). Christ stays central — "Jesus is the ringer and the ringin' is grace." P/W 70% is the Act-1 transition. The cracked-bell whimsy and the out-mule-a-mule line add the requested silliness while the stop-time bridge keeps the gospel weight. The grace-pursues-the-stubborn frame honors all traditions (no works-salvation, no contempt).
