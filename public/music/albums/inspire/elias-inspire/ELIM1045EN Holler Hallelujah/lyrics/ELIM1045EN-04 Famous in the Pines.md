## Lyrics

[Intro — banjo roll + fiddle, ≤0:08]
He's famous in the pines — and the thunder!

[Verse 1]
Wind come combin' through the pines like it's late for church,
Whippoorwill's already up there on the highest perch —
Katydids are keepin' time like a tiny brass band,
And I'm the only mute thing standin' here with empty hands

[Pre-Chorus]
If a beetle drums a hymn on a hollow log,
I won't get out-praised by a chorus in the fog

[Chorus]
He's famous in the pines — (and the thunder!)
Spellin' out His glory in a sky tore asunder
Famous where the dogwood pops its pink-white seam,
He's famous in the pines — Lord, fold me in the team!

[Verse 2]
Mountain never went to school to learn to lift its voice,
Been bustin' out in worship since the world had a choice —
So I'll take my lessons from the laurel and the rain,
Learn to holler hallelujah like a thunderhead in pain

[Pre-Chorus]
If the rocks would do my shoutin' when my words run thin,
Then catch me up, you hickories, and let me in

[Chorus]
He's famous in the pines — (and the crickets!)
Singin' in the briar where He plants the wild thickets
Famous in the frost and the dragonfly's wing,
He's famous in the pines — so I've got no choice but sing!

[Bridge — creation-hoedown build]
The sycamores have known His name a thousand springs longer than me,
The river's been a-clappin' since before there was a "me" to be —
I'll out-warble a whippoorwill before I let Him go unsung,
And join the oldest anthem ever hummed by an untamed tongue!

[Final Chorus — full stomp + fiddle break]
He's famous in the pines — (and in me!)
Last one to the hoedown, but I came a-runnin', see
Let the maples drop their gold and the whole ridge ring —
So I'll make God famous in the pines — now this fool's gonna sing!

## Scoring Justification

A Psalm-19 creation-praise romp, fully through-composed — the "famous in the…" list never repeats (thunder, crickets, frost, dragonfly's wing) and the response evolves to "and in me!" His renown is the object of every line, holding Fully God-Centered. The beetle-drumming-a-hymn and "thunderhead in pain" images deliver the requested whimsy; the singer is the comic butt ("only mute thing… empty hands"), never God. P/W 80% keeps Act-2 praise high.
