## Lyrics

[Intro — banjo roll + stomp, ≤0:08]
No empty seats — pass the biscuits!

[Verse 1]
Saw a table run so long it left my eyes behind,
Place-cards in the Father's hand, every name a-signed —
And the boy whose chair stayed empty all them frozen years?
He's down front in his Sunday best, laughin' off the tears

[Pre-Chorus]
Every name we wore our knees out on the floor,
Got hollered at the supper as they shouldered through the door

[Chorus]
No empty seats — (pass the biscuits!)
Every prodigal a chapter that the angels couldn't risk it
Mama's wildest, the drunkard, the widow worn and worn —
No empty seats in glory, the whole dern house reborn!

[Verse 2]
Copperhead Gus is elbow to elbow with the King,
That flint-hard widow's two-steppin', can't stop, she's got to sing —
The chair we kept for grace has a sunburnt face in it now,
And the One who walked him home is takin' every bow

[Pre-Chorus]
What looked like a stone-cold loss on a frostbit hill,
Got answered at the banquet — and it's fillin' fuller still

[Bridge — roll-call build]
He carved my name in the cedar before I knew how to spell it,
Stitched your name in the linen and He couldn't wait to tell it —
Called the whole crooked roll out loud and never skipped a one,
Not a chair left cold in glory now the gatherin' is done!

[Final Chorus — double-time, full stomp]
No empty seats — (the house is full!)
Wall to wall with the rescued and the wonderful
From the cradle-robbed to the gallows-bought and worn —
No empty seats in glory, hallelujah, reborn!
No empty seats — so somebody pass the cornbread down!
