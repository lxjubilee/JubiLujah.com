## Lyrics

[Intro — lone fiddle, ≤0:12]
Set the chair, keep the light…

[Verse 1]
Cane-bottom chair at the head of the table, set,
Plate turned face-down for a boy we couldn't forget —
Mama tucks a biscuit warm beneath the checkered cloth,
Whispers "in case he comes" and stirs the candle soft

[Pre-Chorus]
Eight cold suppers, eight Decembers thin,
We kept the door unlatched for the wind to bring him in

[Chorus]
So we set the chair and we trim the wick down low,
Say his name to Jesus in the language only mamas know —
We can't go drag him off that far-country floor,
But You can walk a dead man clean through a bolted door

[Verse 2]
Daddy quit his cussin', wore a groove in the pine,
Said "Lord, I built this farm but I can't fix this child of mine" —
We don't know the country, can't name the chain he's in,
But the Shepherd's out in weather that'd drown an ordinary man

[Pre-Chorus]
Lamp burned to a nub but it would not die,
Eight years is an awful night — but You weren't standin' by

[Bridge — strip to lone voice, whisper to full]
Two a.m., the screen door shrieked like a fiddle string come loose,
And there stood a ghost of a boy, all bone and rain and bruise —
Eight years I set a chair for grace, and grace walked in with rain,
Knelt my prodigal at the table and called him by his name again

[Final Chorus — band blooms, family harmony]
So we set the chair, throw the door back wide,
Heap his plate to fallin' while the whole house cried —
We couldn't reach that far-country floor, not one inch more,
But Jesus, You went and walked him home through that bolted door —
Set the chair — (he's home!)
