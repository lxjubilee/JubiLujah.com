## Lyrics

[Intro — banjo + fiddle, ≤0:08]
The river washed me — white as snow!

[Verse 1]
Waded in a-totin' forty years of bricks,
Every dumb regret and all my favorite tricks —
Current grabbed my knees on the moss-green stone,
Said "Lord, I can't lug this another mile alone"

[Pre-Chorus]
Preacher's palm behind my skull, the sky wide blue,
"Buried with Him, raised with Him" — and under I flew

[Chorus]
The river washed me — (white as snow!)
Snatched the old fool under and it would not let go
A snappin' turtle blinked from his sun-warmed log,
While Jesus pulled a new man clean up out the fog!

[Verse 2]
Went down a debtor with my ledger in the red,
Came up a son with "it is finished" in my head —
Sun cracked the tree-line just as my face broke air,
And the weight that nearly drowned me — brother, it wasn't there

[Pre-Chorus]
Didn't scrub myself — the Lamb's blood did,
Grace dove deeper than the deepest sin I hid

[Bridge — build]
My old self went floatin' off and waved at me goodbye,
Bobbin' down the riffles like a hat too proud to die —
Jesus held me under and He hauled me up to stand,
Now I'm drippin' wet and laughin' like a brand-new man!

[Final Chorus — key change, full stomp + fiddle break]
The river washed me — (brand-new man!)
Cold as January but I came up grinnin' grand
White shirt clingin' and the world gone bright and wide —
The river washed me new where my old life died!
The river washed me — and I left him in the tide!
