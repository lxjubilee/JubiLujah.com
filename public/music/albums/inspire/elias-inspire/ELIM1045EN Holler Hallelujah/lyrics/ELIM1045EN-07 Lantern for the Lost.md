## Lyrics

[Intro — clawhammer banjo, minor, ≤0:10]
Lift the lantern for the lost…

[Verse 1]
Took a railroad lantern past the rusty cattle gate,
Swingin' a wobblin' coin of light where the wet leaves wait —
Somebody's runaway is shiverin' in the pines,
And I aim to comb this ridge till the moon resigns

[Pre-Chorus]
But the higher that I climb up the moonless track,
The more the briars grab like the year I never made it back

[Chorus]
Lift the lantern for the lost — (out past the porch light!)
Past the leanin' headstones where the foxfire burns white
Hold it up where the brambles claw and the cold tries hard to win,
Lift the lantern for the lost — go fetch a wanderer in!

[Verse 2]
Flame went duckin' low when the north wind curled its lip,
Same blue it burned the night I lost my grip —
I wasn't searchin' then, I was the gone-wrong one,
And a Light came pickin' down the hill before the dark was done

[Pre-Chorus]
That's the moment that it spun me clean around:
This fire ain't mine — it was handed down

[Bridge — stop-time, then key change to major]
(silence) ...all them years I called myself the brave one with the flame,
when I was just a frostbit boy who couldn't crawl out of his shame —
I never struck this match — He came down the black hill and lit me,
(full band, major key) and a found man's the only kind the Father ever sends to seek!

[Final Chorus — major, full, lifted]
Lift the lantern for the lost — (He lit it first for me!)
Out past where the porch light quits, past the cold and the debris
Every soul I drag from the dark, He dragged me through the same —
So lift the lantern for the lost, and shout the Rescuer's name!

## Scoring Justification

Through-composed; the call-and-response inverts only at the twist ("out past the porch light" → "He lit it first for me"), so even the hook never repeats verbatim. Christ is the searching Shepherd ("a Light came pickin' down the hill before the dark was done"), holding Fully God-Centered. The foxfire/"leanin' headstones"/"beggars' hands" imagery sharpens the eerie drama the directive asked for. P/W 55% suits the minor-to-major pivot. This remains the album's cinematic twist (Luke 15): the seeker was first the sought.
