## Lyrics

[Intro — banjo + stomp, ≤0:06]
Tell it on the ridge — and don't you hush!

[Verse 1]
Old folks sang it soft, "go tell it on the hill,"
I'm-a crank it up the ridgeline where the north wind's shrill —
Telephone wire's hummin' a note that no man dialed,
And I can't choke this good news down — it's drivin' me wild

[Pre-Chorus]
Like a grass fire hoppin' fence-rows in a hurry to be heard,
Once this gospel catches, brother, you can't un-light the word

[Chorus]
Tell it on the ridge — (and don't you hush!)
Up the gravel switchback through the laurel and the brush
Holler it at the cabins where the lonesome lamps go dim,
Tell it on the ridge — He paid the debt, so spit it out for Him!

[Verse 2]
There's a cabin two coves over ain't heard a single word,
Old man chewin' on regret like the bone of a winter bird —
So I'll lace my muddy boots and hike the dark mile through,
'Cause somebody hiked it once and dragged it home to you

[Pre-Chorus]
I won't sit on a hilltop with my lantern hood pulled down,
While there's porches goin' midnight all over this town

[Bridge — build]
I'd sooner bust wide open than die with good news in my teeth,
Sooner wear my shoe leather plumb through than leave one soul beneath —
Hand me the lantern, point me at the blackest holler you've got,
'Cause a man who's been forgiven can't shut up about a lot!

[Final Chorus — full stomp + fiddle break]
Tell it on the ridge — (till the last hill hears!)
Roll it through the hollers, beat it up against the years
Down the gravel grade, up the rooster-crowin' rim,
Tell it on the ridge — the tomb's tore open, so go brag on Him!

## Scoring Justification

Through-composed Great-Commission anthem — every chorus and the response are fresh ("don't you hush" → "till the last hill hears"). Christ's finished work stays the reason ("He paid the debt," "the tomb's tore open"). The "good news in my teeth" and "bone of a winter bird" images add the requested grit and oddity; the comedy is the singer's holy inability to shut up. P/W 85% drives the build into Act 3. No partisan/heritage conflation in the mountain imagery.
