## Lyrics

[Intro — banjo + stomp, ≤0:06]
One more soul — haul him in!

[Verse 1]
Cracked-chimney lantern throwin' crooked yellow round,
Guitar case yawnin' open with two buttons on the ground —
I ain't here to pass the hat, I'm here to give Him free,
Got one fool ambition 'fore the rooster crows on me

[Pre-Chorus]
Don't matter if it's the drunkard or the deacon's secret pride,
Heaven holds its breath the second a sinner's gonna decide

[Chorus]
One more soul — (haul him in!)
Off the bottle, off the gallows, out the snare of sin
Won't strike this brush-arbor tent or coil a single rope
Till one more crooked sinner gets a straightenin' dose of hope!

[Verse 2]
Widow on the back bench, hard as a January creek,
Forty years of "leave me be," ain't shed a tear in weeks —
But the hymn comes a-creepin' low and I feel it in my chest,
Tonight that flint-hard heart is fixin' to confess

[Pre-Chorus]
So I'll sing one more verse, wait one more hour in the cold,
'Cause a single rescued soul's worth more than the mountain's gold

[Bridge — count-up build]
That's one off the riverbed, soaked to the skin and grinnin' wide —
that's two crawled up the bank with the devil left to dry —
that's three, that's four, and the tally-book is gettin' tight,
I'll keep fishin' this dark river till the angels file complaints tonight!

[Final Chorus — double-time, full stomp]
One more soul — (count him saved!)
Whole front row a-weepin' where the wicked used to wave
Heaven's keepin' score and it can't write near as quick —
One more soul, then one more soul — the harvest's comin' thick!
One more soul tonight — and we ain't goin' home!

## Scoring Justification

Through-composed — the count-up never repeats a line ("off the riverbed… up the bank… file complaints") and the response evolves ("haul him in" → "count him saved"). The saving Christ stays central. The "angels file complaints" gag and "straightenin' dose of hope" add the requested fun without mocking the lost (the comedy is the preacher's holy stubbornness). P/W 88% — fastest stomp; the participatory count remains the most clip-able moment. Triumphalism check: no contempt for sinners — the door stays open, the drunk gets dignity.
