## Lyrics

[Intro — flat-top guitar + mandolin, ≤0:10]
Carve His name — deeper…

[Verse 1]
Granddad cut the headstones up on Sawyer's hill,
Chisel ringin' steady when the night went still —
Said "boy, every letter that a mortal hand can write
Wears down to a riddle in the rain and spite"

[Pre-Chorus]
"But there's one Name the weather cannot rub —
Cut it to the marrow, son, you can't cut it too much"

[Chorus]
Carve His name — (deeper!)
Deeper than the fence rot, deeper than the sleeper
Outlast my own letters when they've blurred and flown,
Carve His name in granite, and it carries on alone

[Verse 2]
I've watched the gray lichen eat my family line,
Livin' names go soft, one curlicue at a time —
But I read it in the boulder where the moss won't crawl:
Jesus — and beneath it, "He came and loved them all"

[Pre-Chorus]
So if I get one short life and a cuttin' tool,
I'll spend it makin' famous what'll outlast the school of fools

[Bridge — harmony stack build]
My letters wash to nothing — His outlast the wind and worms,
Sharper than the granite, surer than my borrowed terms —
Deep as the empty tomb that He walked out of in the dark,
I'll cut that Name in everything before they cut my mark!

[Final Chorus — full, lifted, harmony]
Carve His name — (it's all that stays!)
Long past the cabin wall and the last of my days
When my stone's a blur and my fence is overgrown,
That Name'll still be ringin' off a thousand hills of stone!
