What "updated" means in the v3.0 pass

The v3.0 audit was an analytical & architectural pass — it added per-album dimensional scoring (composite + 6 dimensions) to all 30 album.meta.json files. No lyric .md files were modified. But the worker analysis formalized new production direction for specific track positions across every album:

  • CAIM1030 T12 + CAIM1001 T1 received the brand-new catalog-loop closure spec (5 handshake fields). These two tracks must be regenerated for the loop to land.
  • All T7 tracks (30 across 30 albums) received the formalized "pastoral testimony anchor" signature — pastor-voice spoken-word over Rhodes/Hammond/cello with named pastoral composite. Hits EmPull 96–97 when correctly produced.
  • All T8 tracks (30 across 30 albums) received the formalized "cinematic peak strip-down + gospel choir bloom" signature. The kit cuts, single Rhodes/Martin carries one bar of silence-the-room, then choir+brass bloom returns.
  • All T12 tracks (30 across 30 albums) received the formalized "T12 album-loop architecture" — outro pulls back to T1 via Hammond pad sustain or Rhodes hold.
  • All other tracks (T2–T6, T9–T11; 238 tracks) received per-track ear-candy + dynamic-arc + emotional-anchor scoring but no fundamentally-new production direction. Regenerate only if you want incremental refinement.

Why these are "low priority"

The Caleb lyric .md files were not modified by the v3.0 pass — they remain the canonical Caleb-format Suno-compliant exemplar that the entire family's other personas were later converted to match. Every Styles field, every section tag, every production cue in those 360 lyric files is the same as before v3.0.

What changed for these 238 tracks is the per-album album.meta.json 6-dimension scoring — an analytical layer that informs future craft passes but does not alter the existing Suno generation inputs (Styles + Lyrics).

If you regenerate all 360 tracks anyway (e.g., for a full catalog refresh), the new Suno cuts will be re-rendered from identical inputs to the prior cuts — the variation will come from Suno's stochastic generation, not from v3.0-introduced changes.

Recommended regeneration order if you want to prioritize impact

Step 1 (must do): Regenerate the 2 Priority-1 tracks (CAIM1001 T1 + CAIM1030 T12) so the catalog-loop closure lands.

Step 2 (high ROI): Regenerate the 6 Priority-2 catalog peaks — visible quality lift across the most-listened albums.

Step 3 (architectural lift): Regenerate the remaining 88 unique T7/T8/T12 tracks (28 T7 + 28 T8 + 28 T12, after removing the 6 duplicates from Step 2 and the 2 from Step 1). This locks the formalized v3.0 architectural-position signatures.

Step 4 (full refresh, optional): Regenerate the remaining 238 lower-priority tracks for a full catalog refresh. No urgency — lyric inputs are unchanged.
