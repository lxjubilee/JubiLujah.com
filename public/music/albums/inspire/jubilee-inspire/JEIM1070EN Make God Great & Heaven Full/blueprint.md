## Album Vision Statement

This is Jubilee throwing a stadium-sized party for the only Name that was famous before the stars — and pulling chairs up to the table for everyone who hasn't arrived yet. It holds the album's gorgeous tension in both hands: the roar of celebration and the ache of the empty seat, the comedy of being God's publicist and the gravity of a fame built on a cross. From the first downbeat it means to make a crowd sing His greatness louder by making themselves smaller, until "make God great" and "fill heaven" turn out to be the very same sentence — love calling people home by name.

## Structural Rationale

### Why the Three-Act Model Was Chosen for This Album
The title is two clauses — "make God great" / "heaven full" — and the album proves they're one. Act 1 throws the party (great), Act 2 walks out to find the missing (the cost, the ache, the search), Act 3 fills the house and reveals the two clauses were always the same act. 3+6+3 lets the cross sit dead-center as the hinge.

### Emotional Progression
Roar (1) → joyful smallness (2) → laughter (3) → defiance of the feed (4) → late awe (5) → kneeling hush (6) → tender ache (7) → belonging (8) → held breath of intercession (9) → reunion (10) → one quiet name (11) → soaring reframe (12).

### Spiritual Progression
The covenant truth: God's greatness and God's mercy are not two campaigns. He is magnified precisely in the gathering of the lost; His glory is most visible in a filled chair. The Ruach does Her deepest work at the cross (T6) and the empty chair (T9), where awe and intercession meet.

### Cinematic Twist / Revelation Moment
**Track 6, "Crown on a Cross."** Five tracks of party and hype suddenly kneel: the most famous moment in history was a public execution, fame bought by surrender, not triumph. It reframes every "make Him famous" line that came before — we're not promoting a celebrity, we're proclaiming a Lamb — and gives the back half its gravity and its grace.

## Genre Fusion Laboratory

#### Stage 1: Parent Genre Selection
- **Umbrella:** Contemporary Christian Music (CCM) as the connective tissue across all 12.
- **Per-song rule (honored):** each track fuses CCM with exactly **one** partner style from {Contemporary Worship · Hymns Reimagined · Contemporary Gospel · Anthemic Arena Worship · Praise Break} — never more than two styles in one song.

#### Stage 2: Sonic DNA Extraction
- **CCM:** polished pop production, four-chord progressions, radio-bright lead vocal, big relatable hooks.
- **Contemporary Worship:** ambient guitar swells, build-and-release dynamics, congregational octave range.
- **Hymns Reimagined:** classic hymn melodic/textual DNA in a modern band setting, cello/piano warmth.
- **Contemporary Gospel:** Hammond B3, choir runs, walk-ups, hand-clap pocket, call-and-response.
- **Anthemic Arena Worship:** stadium toms, four-on-the-floor lift, huge gang-vocal choruses, key-change finales.
- **Praise Break:** double-time shout section, brass stabs, organ shout, tambourine.

#### Stage 3: Fusion Rules
CCM owns the verses and the hook architecture (singability + radio gloss); the partner style owns the choruses/bridges (the emotional payload). Collision points: praise-break double-times dropped under arena choruses; hymn quotations surfacing in modern bridges.

#### Stage 4: Fused Genre Identity
- **Genre Name:** **Famous-Maker Worship** (CCM arena-worship that magnifies by making the singer small).
- **Identity Statement:** Radio-bright congregational anthems engineered to give glory away and pull chairs up to the table.
- **Sonic Mood Board:** stadium lights over a kitchen table · confetti and candlelight · a choir that whispers then roars · four chords that feel like home.
