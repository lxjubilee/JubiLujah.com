LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, sound of water poured from bronze)
Yahuah... my mind has been a room that will not quiet...
the same thoughts on a loop... for so long...

[Verse 1]
I come to the laver, Avinu,
not for my hands and not for my feet.
I come for the part of me
that will not stop spinning at three in the morning.
The voice in my head has been mean to me, Yahuah -
the same lie on repeat -
and Your living water knows the difference
between my voice and the voice that is not mine.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim Chayim, living water,
pour it on my mind, oh Yahuah,
by the blood of the Lamb that opened the laver,
by the body of Yeshua washed before me -
I bow my head to the basin,
You wash the part that hurts.

[Chorus]
Pour the water on the mind,
pour the water on the loop,
pour the water on the thought
that will not stop, that will not stop.
Mayim Chayim, Yahuah,
the water remembers Eden,
wash my mind back to before the lie
became my voice. Even here, even here.

[Verse 2]
I have tried to do the renewing on my own strength, Avinu,
I tried to think different, do better, choose joy.
But the muscle for that is the muscle that is tired,
and the choosing is the part that broke.
So You renew, You renew,
You sprinkle, You sprinkle.
I bow my head; You do the washing.
Your Living Water has the strength my mind ran out of.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim Chayim, living water,
pour it on my mind, oh Yahuah,
by the blood of the Lamb that opened the laver,
by the body of Yeshua washed before me -
I bow my head to the basin,
You wash the part that hurts.

[Chorus]
Pour the water on the mind,
pour the water on the loop,
pour the water on the thought
that will not stop, that will not stop.
Mayim Chayim, Yahuah,
the water remembers Eden,
wash my mind back to before the lie
became my voice. Even here, even here.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun mournful, ney flute breath response, tof heartbeat steady, sound of water faintly returning under the strings — 30 seconds)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The room behind my eyes has not had quiet in years.
The voice that lives there has not been Yours.
But the Mayim Chayim has a memory older than the lie,
the water remembers the breath You put in Adam -
so receive the breath I am putting back into Your hand,
and pour the Living Water on the part the lie has lived in.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense)
By Your Word, it is done.
By Your stripes, my mind is washed.
By Your blood of the Lamb, the Mayim Chayim is poured.
In Yeshua's Name, the renewing is mine - sealed, received, signed in His blood.

[Bridge]
(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
(Shema melody contour hummed; the descending minor third lingers on Echad)
Yahuah Echad... lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Ezekiel 36:25-26 personalized as finished-reality reception)
You have given me a new heart, Yahuah - I receive it.
You have put a new ruach within me - I receive Her.
You have taken the stone, You have given me flesh -
You have washed, You have renewed, You keep me even here.

[Chorus]
(Final chorus, full ensemble, weeping strings, layered Shekhinah harmonies)
Pour the water on the mind,
pour the water on the loop,
pour the water on the thought
that will not stop, that will not stop.
Mayim Chayim, Yahuah,
the water remembers Eden,
wash my mind back to before the lie
became my voice. Even here, even here.

[Outro]

(breath returning to silence)

[Outro]
(Whisper-of-Elijah - almost inaudible)

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Eden — the garden where Yahuah walked with humanity in the cool of the day
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Im Imadi — "with me" (the Emmanuel root: "God with me")
- Mayim Chayim — "living water" (cf. John 4:10)
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
