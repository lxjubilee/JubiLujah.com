SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The depressed one cannot eat; cannot taste; appetite has been taken; the kitchen feels foreign
- Escalate: Yeshua arrives at the table of Lechem HaPanim with bread already broken; He does not ask the listener to cook, to crave, or to perform appetite — He feeds from His hand
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge insists the bread of His Presence is enough even when the body cannot want it; the listener receives the Bread that nourishes what the cereal cannot

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Lamentations 3:22-23 — "It is of Yahuah's hesed that we are not consumed, because His mercies fail not; they are new every morning - great is Your faithfulness"
- Brit Hadashah: John 6:35 — "I am the bread of life: he that comes to Me shall never hunger; and he that believes on Me shall never thirst"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said You are the Bread of Life - and Your mercies are new every morning - so even on mornings I cannot eat, You feed me from Your hand"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The smell of fresh-baked bread breaking the morning of a long depression
2. The weight of a hand placing bread directly on a tongue that has forgotten how to want
3. The texture of crust pulled by warm fingers
4. The taste of nourishment that the body did not believe could land

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Lechem (לֶחֶם) - bread. Landed at the chorus. The simplest word for the simplest provision. Bread is what a parent gives a child who has not eaten.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Im Imadi — "with me" (the Emmanuel root: "God with me")
- Lechem — "bread"
- Lechem HaPanim — "Bread of the Face / Bread of the Presence" (the Showbread)
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Shewbread — the Bread of the Presence (Lechem HaPanim) on the golden table
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
