SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The depressed one arrives at the bronze altar with the fine-fine-I-am-fine performance still tight on the face — the smile that has been eating them alive
- Escalate: Teshuvah as honesty, not as penance — the surrender is not of sin, but of pretending; the altar fire burns the mask, not the worshiper
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge insists that the access to the altar is the blood of Yeshua, not the quality of the surrender; the listener receives that this is acceptable even when offered through tears

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 51:17 — "the sacrifices of Elohim are a broken spirit; a broken and a contrite heart, O Elohim, You will not despise"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 John 1:9 — "if we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You will not despise this — the smile that is not a smile, the fine I have been saying when I was not fine; the broken thing in my chest is the sacrifice You accept"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The brass weight of the smile worn so long it has shaped the jaw
2. The smell of bronze and ash at the altar — older than the listener, older than the depression
3. The texture of a paper-thin mask peeled off a face that did not know it could come off
4. The taste of salt — actual tears finally permitted to land

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Teshuvah (תְּשׁוּבָה) - turning, returning. Landed at the pre-chorus. The repentance is not of moral failure; it is the turning away from the performance, the returning of the actual self to the altar.

- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
