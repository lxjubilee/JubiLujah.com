LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single frankincense ember crackle)
Avinu... I have no words tonight...
only the breath leaving... and a small ache...
let that be enough... at the Altar...

[Verse 1]
I sat down to pray, Yahuah,
and nothing came up the throat.
The words I had memorized for occasions like this
all left like birds I forgot to feed.
But there is an Altar of Incense
in front of the veil in Your house,
and the smoke that rises from it
does not require my mouth to speak.

[Pre-Chorus]
Ruach HaKodesh - my Helper,
You make intercession with groans I cannot say.
By the blood of the Lamb on this Altar,
by the body of Yeshua who is my High Priest -
take the breath I cannot shape,
make it the prayer I could not say.

[Chorus]
The prayer I could not say, Yahuah,
You hear it as Ketoret rising.
The groan that has no language,
the breath that has no word -
even here, even here,
the Altar receives the unutterable.
Ruach HaKodesh carries it up,
the prayer I could not say.

[Verse 2]
The people who love me have asked what to pray for,
and I have not known what to tell them.
The depression has not had a single sharp request -
just a fog where the prayer used to be.
But the smoke knows what the lung forgot,
and the Helper knows what the depression hid.
The articulate prayer is not the only prayer;
the groan is also a covenant offering.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aharon — Aaron — the first High Priest, who carried twelve names on his breastplate
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Im Imadi — "with me" (the Emmanuel root: "God with me")
- Ketoret — "incense" — the prayer-aroma offering
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
