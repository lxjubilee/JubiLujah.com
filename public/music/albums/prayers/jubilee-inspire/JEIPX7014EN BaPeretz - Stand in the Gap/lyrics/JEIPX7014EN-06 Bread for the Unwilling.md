SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The watchman stands at the Table of Showbread realizing the twelve loaves are the twelve tribes — and the broken Bread is the Body of Yeshua broken for the ones the watchman is carrying, even the ones who refuse to eat
- Escalate: The watchman commits to eat double — to consume the Lechem HaPanim in proxy for the prodigal who will not yet come to the Table
- Elevate: Luke 14:23 compulsion — the watchman becomes the one who eats the Bread on behalf of those compelled-but-not-yet-arrived; the appetite of the unwilling is being restored across the distance

- Tanakh: Leviticus 24:5–9 — "you shall take fine flour, and bake twelve cakes... and you shall set them in two rows, six on a row, upon the pure table before Yahuah... it is most holy unto him of the offerings of Yahuah made by fire by a perpetual statute"

4. The slow ache of carrying another's hunger in the watchman's own stomach

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- BaPeretz — "in the breach" (Ezekiel 22:30 — standing in the gap)
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Kefa — Peter (Cephas)
- Lechem — "bread"
- Lechem HaPanim — "Bread of the Face / Bread of the Presence" (the Showbread)
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Shewbread — the Bread of the Presence (Lechem HaPanim) on the golden table
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
