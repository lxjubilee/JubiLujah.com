LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, faint coal-and-resin texture, ney flute thin like smoke)
Avinu... the coals are hot...
the bowls in heaven are open...

[Verse 1]
Your golden altar is in front of You, Avinu,
the coals are hot from Your bronze altar fire I just left,
and the ketoret rises slow,
and the names I cannot say at the table tonight
I can say here.
What I cannot tell the husband about my fear for him,
what I cannot tell the son about what I see ahead of him,
what I cannot tell the friend about her own heart —
I tell You.
And it becomes smoke
the moment it leaves the lung.

[Pre-Chorus]
And Yochanan saw Your golden bowls in heaven, Avinu —
full of incense — full of the prayers of the saints —
and every name I am rising up tonight
joins a bowl that has been filling since the first watchman lifted the first name —
I'll stand here for you in the smoke, beloved,
and Your bowl is heard the instant it tips.

[Chorus]
Their names become smoke, Avinu —
the unspeakable prayers rise as ketoret —
I'll stand here for you,
I'll stand here for you,
and the names I cannot pronounce to their face
You receive in the smoke before the Lamb.
I'll stand here for you,
heard and answered in Your golden bowls of heaven.

[Verse 2]
I have prayed some of these names so quietly, Yahuah,
my own ear could not hear them.
I have prayed some of these names with my mouth shut
because the room was full of the wrong people.

the smoke needs my heart, Yahuah,
and the heart at Your golden altar
is the heart with the Blood of the Lamb on it —
and what rises by the Blood, You answer in Your bowl,
finished tense, before the smoke has cleared.

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The smoke is older than the lung that breathes it.
The smoke remembers Avraham at the altar.
The smoke remembers Hannah at Shiloh praying without a voice.
The smoke remembers the saints in Yochanan's vision.
