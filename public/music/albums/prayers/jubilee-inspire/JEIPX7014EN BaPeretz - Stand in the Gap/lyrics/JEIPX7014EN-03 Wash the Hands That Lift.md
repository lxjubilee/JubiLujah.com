LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, sound of water poured into stone basin)
Yahuah... wash the hands
that are about to lift them...

[Verse 1]
I come off Your altar lighter, Avinu —
the thing I confessed is gone in the smoke —
but my hands still carry the dust of the road
and the names I am about to lift
are too holy for unwashed palms.
You said Aharon's sons must wash
or they would die at the threshold.
I do not want to die at the threshold, Yahuah —
I want to live to bring them in.

[Pre-Chorus]
And Your laver is here, Yahuah —
You sanctify me by the washing of water by the Word —
You speak that promise, and Your promise is the basin —
so I dip my hands, I dip my feet,
I dip the lifting before the lifting,
and the Word You wash me with stands as Receipt.

[Chorus]
Wash the hands that lift, Avinu —
make me tahor, make me fit, make me clean —
I'll stand here for you,
I'll stand here for you,
and the hands lifting their names are holy now
because Your basin has already made them so.
I'll stand here for you,
washed by the Blood of the Lamb at Your laver.

longer than the bruises,
and what the Blood of the Lamb has cleansed
is cleansed in finished tense, not in trying.
