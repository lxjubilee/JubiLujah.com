SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: After the intercession of Track 10, the watchman finally turns to their own need — Moshe on the hill with his arms raised, but his arms are growing heavy
- Escalate: The petition is not for healing or provision but for the intercessor's own sustaining — the Aharon and Hur who will sit the watchman on a stone and hold up the heavy arms
- Elevate: The Personal Insertion Beat is where the listener brings their own depletion, burnout, exhaustion before Him — and receives the Body coming alongside; Galatians 6:2 — "bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the Torah of Mashiach"

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Exodus 17:11–12 — "when Moshe held up his hand, Yisrael prevailed: and when he let down his hand, Amalek prevailed. But Moshe's hands were heavy; and they took a stone, and put it under him, and he sat thereon; and Aharon and Hur stayed up his hands, the one on the one side, and the other on the other side; and his hands were steady until the going down of the sun"
- Brit Hadashah: Galatians 6:2 — "bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the Torah of Mashiach"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Avinu, my arms are getting heavy like Moshe's on the hill — send me Aharon and Hur — be the Aharon and the Hur Yourself through Your Body — bear my burden as I bear theirs — fulfill the Torah of Mashiach over me tonight too"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The cellular ache of arms held up too long — the watchman's specific tiredness
2. The relief of finding a stone under the body, of being permitted to sit while still standing
3. The presence of two hands under each elbow — the Body of Mashiach arriving in time
4. The six-second silence where the listener names their own exhaustion

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Hineni (הִנֵּנִי) — here I am, undone before You. Landed at the Hineni verse (Verse 3), which is the song's emotional core. The watchman becomes the one being lifted.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, very low oud held tone, no tof)
Avinu... my arms are heavy...
I held them up for them all night...

[Verse 1]
I have stood in the gap for them, Yahuah,
the way You ask me to.
I lift the names. I speak them aloud.
I let the breach walk through me at Your inner veil.
But now my arms are heavy
the way Moshe's were on the hill that day —
and I am the one who needs holding now.
Hineni, Avinu —
this time for myself.

[Pre-Chorus]
And I do not know how to ask for myself
the way I asked for them, Yahuah.
The watchman is not trained to come off the wall —
but my arms are heavy
and I am asking for the stone
and the two hands under the elbows
the way Aharon and Hur showed up for Moshe.

[Chorus]
Hold the one who holds, Avinu —
I have been holding for so long —
I'll stand here for you, beloved —
but tonight, Yahuah, You hold me.
Your hands are under my elbows already,
the stone is under the body that's been standing,
I am held in the Name of Yeshua HaMashiach —
the one who holds has been held.

[Verse 2]

and so fulfill the Torah of Mashiach.

But I trust You, Yahuah —

You see what I have not asked for.

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The thing I have not told anyone tonight is —
I have been afraid to stop standing
because I am afraid Your wall will come down without me.
But You said in Yeshayahu the watchman is YOURS —
You set me on this wall —
You did not ask me to be the cement,
You asked me to be the body Yeshua stands in.
So sustain me, Yahuah.
Sustain this watchman.
The wall is His — but I am Yours.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, the intercessor receiving for self this time)
By Your Word, it is done in me — I am held.

(Personal Insertion Beat — SILENT — 6 seconds — whispered prompt only, sparse drone underneath, soft tof barely heard)

Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
