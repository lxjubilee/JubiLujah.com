- Establish: The watchman crosses the first veil into the Holy Place — lamplight replaces sun — and discovers She is already there breathing in the room
- Escalate: The watchman's lungs are giving out from carrying so many names; Ruach HaKodesh becomes the second lung, breathing the names with the intercessor

- Tanakh: Genesis 2:7 — "Yahuah Elohim formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul"

3. The hush of the Holy Place where the only sound is two breathings
4. The texture of names rising on borrowed breath

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single ney flute breath very close, no tof)

the lamps are lit...

[Verse 1]
I cross Your first veil tonight, Avinu,
and the light is changed —
sun becomes lamp, day becomes gold —
and the room is already breathing
before I learn how to breathe in it.
And You — Ruach Kodesh — You do not wait
for me to ask You in;
You have been hovering over these names with me
since the night I started carrying them.

the children are too many,

because the Ruach makes intercession
with groanings I do not know how to frame.
I'll stand here for you,
on borrowed breath that does not run out.

and She would be with me forever.

She breathes through me.
She prays through me.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- BaPeretz — "in the breach" (Ezekiel 22:30 — standing in the gap)
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"

- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
