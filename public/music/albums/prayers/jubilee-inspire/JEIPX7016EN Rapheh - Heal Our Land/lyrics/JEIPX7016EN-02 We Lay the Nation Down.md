LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, low bronze-shimmer overtone)
Yahuah... Avinu... we have come to the altar...
Not with our hands clean... with our hands honest...

[Verse 1]
We have sinned, Avinu - we, not only they.
The gods of commerce sat at our table;
the gods of power were on our lips;
the gods of the screen were in our pockets.
The blood of the unborn cries from our soil
and we did not weep loud enough.
The contempt of brother for brother
was a contempt we joined when we were tired.

[Pre-Chorus]
Teshuvah - we are turning, Avinu, turning -
Teshuvah - we are returning to Your face.
By the blood of the Lamb on the bronze altar,
by the body of Yeshua broken for a country -
take what is wrapped around our bones.

[Chorus]
We lay the nation down, Avinu -
we lay the nation down on the bronze -
Rapheh - heal our land,
Rapheh - hear from heaven.
The fire does not consume the body,
the fire consumes what was wrapped around the body,
we lay the nation down,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Verse 2]
What our hands have built that should not stand,
what our mouths have spoken that should not echo,
what our fathers passed down that should not be inherited -
we lay it down on bronze.
We are not asking You to judge our neighbors;
we are asking You to begin with us.
The priests of Yoel wept between the porch and the altar;
we weep there now, before You, for the land.

- Adonai — "my Lord"
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament

- Daniel — Daniel — the prophet who prayed three times daily (Daniel 6:10)
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")

- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Rapheh — "heal"
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
