SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC
- Establish: After the breastplate of cities, the priest turns to her own watch - "what is my part, Avinu?"
- Escalate: Nehemiah 3 - every wall is rebuilt one stone at a time; the listener does not need a national platform, the listener needs to know which stone is hers
- Elevate: Yeshayahu 6:8 lands - "Hineni, send me" - not for greatness, for clarity; for the inch of wall assigned

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)
- Tanakh: Yeshayahu 6:8 - "I heard the voice of Yahuah, saying, Whom shall I send, and who will go for us? Then said I, Hineni, send me"
- Brit Hadashah: Philippians 2:13 - "Yahuah works in you both to will and to do of His good pleasure"
- Personalized translation used: "Hineni, Yahuah - send me to the inch of wall You assigned me; work in me both to will and to do; my part is small and my watch is mine"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY
1. The texture of a single stone in the hand - heavier than expected
2. The visual of Nehemiah's wall rising one section at a time, by family, by neighbor
3. The sound of the listener finally asking the only question that matters - "what is my part?"
4. The relief in the body when the answer is small, specific, and assigned

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Calvary — the place of Yeshua's crucifixion — where the veil was torn
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Rapheh — "heal"
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
