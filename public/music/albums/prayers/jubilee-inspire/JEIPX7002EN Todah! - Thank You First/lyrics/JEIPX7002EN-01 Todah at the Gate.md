SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A weary intercessor arrives at the Temple gate exhausted from a season of asking — and remembers Paul and Silas, who sang thanksgiving at midnight and the prison gates flew open
- Escalate: The singer realizes the gates of Yahuah open the same way — by todah, not by lobbying; the claps come in, the choir blooms
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full while the listener crosses the threshold; the gladness is not the reward of the entry, the gladness IS the entry

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 100:4 — "Enter His gates with todah, and His courts with tehillah"
- Brit Hadashah: Acts 16:25-26 — "at midnight Paul and Silas prayed and sang praises... and suddenly there was a great earthquake... and immediately all the doors were opened"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said Your gates open to todah — so I'm bringing thanks and the gate is already swinging — I sing what Paul sang at midnight and the chains forget my wrists"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of hand-claps on 2 and 4 starting in the courtyard before I crossed the gate
2. The smell of bread baking in the priest's quarter at the threshold
3. The texture of a Hebrew word on the tongue at 6 in the morning
4. The taste of laughter mixed with tears when the gate opens before I knock

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Todah (תּוֹדָה) — thanksgiving, the thank-offering. Landed on the chorus high note and repeated as the song's hook. The Name of the offering IS the prayer at that moment.

so I'm singing before I knock today,

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Rapha — "Healer; the One who knits, mends, restores"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Todah — "thank you / thanksgiving"
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
