SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener arrives at the bronze laver expecting to stop the song to be cleaned — and finds that the laver joins the song
- Escalate: The water remembers Eden; the singing did not have to stop for the washing because gratitude survives the rinsing — the dirt comes off but the todah stays in the mouth
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge declares cleansing complete by the blood of Yeshua; the listener rises wet and still singing

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of water poured slow from a bronze bowl onto the back of the neck
2. The visual of a face surfacing in the laver and seeing it remembers Eden
3. The texture of skin going from dust to dew without a break in the song
4. The weight of a clean back that has just enough room left for a chorus

- Mayim Chayim (מַיִם חַיִּים) — living waters. Landed at the chorus. The water is not still water; it is the moving water of Yahuah's mercy.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, water-droplet sample fading in)
Avinu... the laver is full this morning...
And the water... is listening to my song...

[Verse 1]
I come to the laver, Yahuah,
half-expecting You to ask me to stop singing
before I go under the water.
But Your water joins the song instead.
The mayim chayim know Eden by heart —
they were there at the beginning —
and they have been singing under the dirt
since before I was born to need them.

[Pre-Chorus]
And the laver knows the song
because the song is older than my dirt...
By the blood of the Lamb at the laver,
by the body of Yeshua under the water,
I do not stop the todah to be made clean —
I sing harder, and the dirt slides off.

[Chorus]
Washed and still singing, Avinu —
mayim chayim, mayim chayim —
todah while You scrub me,
todah on the rising side.
You do not require my silence to be made clean —
You require my todah while the water moves,
washed and still singing,
in Your courts, Yahuah Echad.

[Verse 2]
You meet me at the laver carrying yesterday's grime, Yahuah,
the language I should not have used,
the look I should not have given,
the weight I should not have stored under my collarbone.
But You hear me singing
while my hands are still dirty —
and You do not wait for me to be clean enough.
You wash me BECAUSE I am singing,
not before.

[Pre-Chorus]
And You said You sanctify and cleanse me
with the washing of water by Your Word, Avinu...
By the blood of the Lamb at the laver,
by the body of Yeshua under the water,
I do not stop the todah to be made clean —
I sing harder, and the Word rinses harder.

[Chorus]
Washed and still singing, Avinu —
mayim chayim, mayim chayim —
todah while You scrub me,
todah on the rising side.
You do not require my silence to be made clean —
You require my todah while the water moves,
washed and still singing,
in Your courts, Yahuah Echad.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, water-bowl sample, ney flute response, soft tof, oud counterpoint — 30 seconds)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
Wash the rooms I did not show the priest.
Wash the rooms I forgot were there.
And let the song keep going
because the song is the only thing keeping me upright
while the water finds the corners no soap has touched.
I do not stop the todah, Yahuah —
I do not stop.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, rising)
By Your Word, it is done.
By Your stripes, I am washed.
By the blood of the Lamb in the water, I have access.
In Yeshua's Name, I rise — still singing — I have received.

(Niggun Bridge — wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed, water-droplet samples ornamenting)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
(Shema melody contour hummed)
Mayim chayim... Echad... lai-lai-lai...
Todah, todah...
Lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Ephesians 5:26 personalized)
Wash me with the water of Your Word, Avinu,
sanctify me by the rinsing of Yeshua's promises over my back,
and let my todah keep time
with the bowl that has not run dry since Eden.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping-laughing strings, layered Shekhinah harmonies, water-bowl reverb on tail)
Washed and still singing, Avinu —
mayim chayim, mayim chayim —
todah while You scrub me,
todah on the rising side.
You do not require my silence to be made clean —
You require my todah while the water moves,
washed and still singing,
in Your courts, Yahuah Echad.

[Outro]

(breath returning to silence)

(Whisper-of-Elijah — almost inaudible)
"Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and you shall be clean." — Yechezkel 36:25
