SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener brings her own petition — the specific, vulnerable, personal one — and remembers the album-anchor pairing: Psalm 100:4 (enter with todah) + 1 Thessalonians 5:18 (give thanks in everything)
- Escalate: She refuses to ask without thanksgiving in front of the ask; she will not let a petition leave her lung without todah on both sides; Hineni verse arrives
- Elevate: Personal Insertion Beat — the listener inserts her own specific petition, with thanks first, then the ask, then thanks again; Faith Declaration Bridge seals reception

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 100:4 — "Enter His gates with todah, and His courts with tehillah: be thankful unto Him, and bless His Name" (ALBUM ANCHOR 1)
- Brit Hadashah: 1 Thessalonians 5:18 — "in every thing give thanks: for this is the will of Elohim in Mashiach Yeshua concerning you" (ALBUM ANCHOR 2)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said in every thing give thanks — so in every ask I will give thanks first; You said enter Your gates with todah — so I bring my petition through the same gate I come in through, and the gate is thanks"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The texture of a specific written petition folded and held in the closed fist
2. The sound of a mouth opening to ask but starting with thanks instead
3. The visual of two cups on the altar — one labeled "the ask," one labeled "the thanks" — and the thanks cup pouring out first AND last
4. The weight of a petition wrapped in todah on both ends, lighter than the petition alone

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Hineni (הִנֵּנִי) — "here I am." Landed at the Hineni verse (the song's emotional core; per master manual the surrender word at every Verse 3, but the Hebrew at peak for this album's S11). Plus the personalized Psalm 100:4 / 1 Thess 5:18 anchor pairing.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single oud pluck, gentle ney flute)
Avinu... I have something specific to ask You tonight...
But the thanks goes first...

[Verse 1]
I come to bring You my own ask tonight, Yahuah —
the one I have been holding too tightly to write down,
the one I have been carrying so long
it has worn a groove inside my chest.
But Paul wrote to Thessalonica — "in EVERY thing give thanks" —
and David wrote — "enter His gates with todah" —
and both verses arrived at my front door this morning
holding hands like sisters.
So the thanks goes first.

[Pre-Chorus]
I do not let my ask leave my mouth
without thanks in front of it...
By the blood of the Lamb that bought me this access,
by the body of Yeshua that opened these courts,
I sandwich every petition in todah —
gratitude in front, gratitude behind.

[Chorus]
Asking with thanks in my mouth, Avinu —
todah, ask, todah —
You said in every thing give thanks,
so my asking is wrapped in praise.
You did not give me a Mercy Seat to negotiate at —
You gave me a Mercy Seat to thank You at while asking,
asking with thanks in my mouth,
in Your courts, Yahuah Echad.

[Verse 2]
The specific ask is still specific, Avinu —
I am not pretending it is not here.
But the thanks I owe You from yesterday
arrives at the same altar
and the two of them rise together.
You said the will of Elohim concerning me
is thanksgiving in everything —
and "everything" includes the specific ask I am holding.
So I bring it with thanks on both sides
and I bring it with my mouth full.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- David — King David
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Mashiach — "Anointed One" / Messiah
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Todah — "thank you / thanksgiving"
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
