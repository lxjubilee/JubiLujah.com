SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener stands before the menorah aware she has been thanking Yahuah for big things and forgetting the daily mercies — the small bowls of oil that have kept the lamp going
- Escalate: One by one the seven flames illuminate gifts she had forgotten — wisdom received, understanding given, counsel kept, might-when-she-was-weak, knowledge unearned, fear of Yahuah that became reverent love, Spirit of Yahuah Herself; the catalogue of mercies rises and the chorus rises with it
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge declares all gifts received and all thanks rendered; the menorah burns brighter because the gratitude has been honest

- Tanakh: Isaiah 11:2 — "the Ruach of Yahuah shall rest upon Him, the Ruach of wisdom and understanding, the Ruach of counsel and might, the Ruach of knowledge and of the fear of Yahuah" (the seven Spirits of the menorah)

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single oud pluck, candle-light texture)
Avinu... seven flames are burning...
And tonight... I am counting...

[Verse 1]
I stand before Your menorah, Yahuah,
and I realize I have been thanking You
for the loud things —
the breakthrough, the answered prayer,
the door that finally opened —
and forgetting the small bowl of oil
that has kept the lamp lit all the years
I was not paying attention.
The seven flames are counting tonight.

[Pre-Chorus]
And the first flame names wisdom received —
the second flame names understanding given...
By the blood of the Lamb on the menorah's gold,
by the body of Yeshua at the Light's source,
the seven flames are counting my gifts —
and I am running out of fingers.

[Chorus]
Seven flames count the gifts, Avinu —
or, or, or, the Light is showing me what I have already received —
todah for the wisdom,
todah for the understanding,
todah for the counsel and the might,
todah for the knowledge and the fear of Yahuah,
todah for the Ruach Herself,
in Your courts, Yahuah Echad.

[Verse 2]
Thank You for the counsel You send as the right friend, Avinu —
thank You for the might You send as the second wind —
thank You for the knowledge You send as the verse I need —
thank You for the fear of You that turns into reverent love —
You keep giving while I keep asking for things You have already given,
You keep burning every flame I forgot to look at —
Your light shows the gifts, Yahuah,
every gift good and perfect comes down from You, Father of lights,
and I have so many.

Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
Your flames are not accusing me for the years I forgot —
they are illuminating gifts that survived my forgetting.
You keep giving while I keep missing.
You keep burning while I keep blinking.
And tonight the seven flames are counting,
and my todah is finally catching up.
