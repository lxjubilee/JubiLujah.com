SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The wounded one arrives at the bronze altar and remembers that this is the place where the body has historically resisted laying things down
- Escalate: The reframe — the altar is not feared because there IS a thank-offering (korban todah, Lev 7:12) and the listener can thank Yahuah for the very fire that consumes the wrong things
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge declares the offering received; thanksgiving for the altar becomes the gladdest surrender the listener has known

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Leviticus 7:12 — "If a man offer it for a thanksgiving (todah), then he shall offer with the sacrifice of thanksgiving unleavened cakes mingled with oil"
- Brit Hadashah: Romans 12:1 — "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of Elohim, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto Yahuah, which is your reasonable service"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said the korban todah is acceptable to You — so I bring my body, my wound, my will of my own, AS thanksgiving — and I thank You for the very altar I am laying them on"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The smell of bronze warmed by long fire
2. The texture of unleavened cakes resting on the priest's palms beside the offering
3. The sound of the knife laid down on the altar stone
4. The weight of a will-of-my-own finally lighter than the hand holding it

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Korban Todah (קָרְבַּן תּוֹדָה) — thank-offering. Landed at the pre-chorus reframe. The only Levitical sacrifice that explicitly included a sung thanksgiving.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Lev — "heart"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Todah — "thank you / thanksgiving"
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
