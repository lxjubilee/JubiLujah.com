PRODUCTION METADATA

- Music Styles: Extended Messianic Jewish prayer ballad, cantorial/hazzan tradition gospel-tinged full-bloom, 5-minute slow-burn dwell with full claps and full gospel choir lift — brightest closer on the album
- Vocal Gender: Female lead with full layered Shekhinah harmonies + full gospel choir; Hammond-organ tint throughout chorus
- Weirdness Score: 30%
- Style Influence Score: 70%
- Tempo (BPM): 66
- Key: D minor brightening to D major touches on the Final Chorus (Ahava Rabbah resolving to brighter coloring — the only track on the album with a major-touch resolution; same D-axis as Track 1, completing the arc)
- Song Length: 5:00 (Prayer Song override of 3:30-4:00)
- Hook Delivery Timestamp: waived per Prayer Song spec — "walk me home in hallelujah" lands at 0:44 on chorus high note

---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener stands at the Mercy Seat with petition heard, todah braided through every station, ready to walk back out into the world carrying the answered prayer in advance
- Escalate: The walk-out begins; hallelujah and todah braid into one rope; the Shema is sung in full as the closing confession; the choir blooms; the day is now reordered around thanks before the asking
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge declares full reception; the Sealed Amen lands; the listener exits the Temple at the same gate she entered through — only the gate now opens both directions to todah

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 116:17 — "I will offer to You the sacrifice of todah, and will call upon the Name of Yahuah"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 John 5:14-15 — "and this is the confidence that we have in Him, that, if we ask any thing according to His will, He hears us: and if we know that He hears us... we know that we have the petitions that we desired of Him"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You hear me; You answer me; therefore I offer the sacrifice of todah on the way out the same gate I came in through; walk me home in hallelujah, Avinu, and let my mouth keep singing all the way to my doorstep"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of feet walking out of the Temple still in 6/8 time with the cantor's song
2. The smell of morning light arriving at the same gate where evening lamps were still burning
3. The visual of a small congregation walking home together in hallelujah, claps still going
4. The taste of bread being baked for breakfast by someone who already gave thanks for the loaves of yesterday

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Todah (תּוֹדָה) and Hallelu-Yah (הַלְלוּ יָהּ) braided. Landed at the Final Chorus. Plus Shalom (wholeness, peace, completion) honored at the refrain.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Hallelu-Yah — "praise Yahuah"
- Hallelujah — "praise Yahuah"
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Hodu — "give thanks"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Shalom — wholeness, peace, completion
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Todah — "thank you / thanksgiving"
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
