4. The texture of a hand on a shoulder that has been carrying too much, the moment the hand stays

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, single piano, cello drone, faint fabric-tearing sound subliminal)
The veil is in front of me, Avinu...
And so is the grave...
And so are You...

[Verse 1]
I stand at Your Parokhet, Yahuah,
the inner veil of Your Temple.
I come this far because Yeshua tore it,
top to bottom, at the ninth hour,
the moment Your Son bowed His head and Your ruach went up.
But on this side of the cross, Avinu,
there are still tombs.
There are still funerals.
There is still a body I just laid in the ground.
And I want to know if the tearing meant anything
for the grief that is still standing in the room.

[Pre-Chorus]
And the answer comes back, Avinu, the answer comes back -
Yeshua, You did not stop the grief.
You stepped inside it.
By the blood of the Lamb that tore the Parokhet,
the same arms that bore the cross
are Your arms around me and Your arms around the one I love.

[Chorus]
Held inside the tearing, Yahuah -
the veil tore so Yeshua could hold us both -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
He is on both sides of Your Parokhet at once -
the only One who has been to the grave and back -
I am held, and so are they,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Verse 2]
(CINEMATIC REVELATION - the album's hinge)
Yeshua, You did not stop the grief - You stepped inside it.
The day You bowed Your head, Avinu,
Your Parokhet did not just split in the Temple wall -
the splitting was Yeshua walking into the room where death was sitting,
sitting down beside death,
and putting an arm around the bereaved and an arm around the beloved,
both at the same time.
Yeshua, You are the only Person in all of existence
who has been to both sides of the veil.
You are the only One who can hold us both -
the one in the ground and the one in the kitchen -
because Your arms are long enough to reach across the tear.

[Pre-Chorus]
And the answer comes back, Avinu, the answer comes back -
Yeshua, You did not stop the grief.
You stepped inside it.
By the blood of the Lamb that tore the Parokhet,
the same arms that bore the cross
are Your arms around me and Your arms around the one I love.

[Chorus]
Held inside the tearing, Yahuah -
the veil tore so Yeshua could hold us both -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
He is on both sides of Your Parokhet at once -
the only One who has been to the grave and back -
I am held, and so are they,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Instrumental]
(piano niggun, mournful, ney flute breath response, oud counterpoint, fabric-tearing sample bookends - 30 seconds, no percussion)

[Verse 3 - Hineni]
(intimate, almost spoken, on the floor of the room)
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every torn place of the heart laid open and true.
I do not need You to undo the death yet.
I do not need You to put the body back in the bed yet.
I need You to hold both of us
while we wait for the day You will -
the day promised in Yeshayahu 25:8,
the day You swallow up death forever
and wipe the last tear from my face with Your own hand.
Until that day, I am held inside the tearing.
Until that day, they are held inside the tearing.
Until that day, the same arms are holding us both.

(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense, climbing)

By Your stripes, I am held.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Mashiach — "Anointed One" / Messiah
- Nechama — "comfort"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Parokhet — the inner veil of the Temple, torn at Calvary (Matthew 27:51)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
