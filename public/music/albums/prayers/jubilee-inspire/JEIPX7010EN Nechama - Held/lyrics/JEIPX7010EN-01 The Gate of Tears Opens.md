SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A bereaved one walks toward gates that no one expects the weeping to enter, expecting the door to be closed because grief is the wrong currency at the King's house
- Escalate: The gate swings open anyway, and the Name above the lintel turns out to be Nechamah - the Comforter; the bouncer at the door turns out to be the Man of Sorrows Himself
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge - the bereaved confesses Echad at the threshold of grief; the body has not been comforted YET but the body has already been received

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 100:4 - "Enter His gates with thanksgiving" - personalized here because the gate of tears IS a gate of thanksgiving when the One inside is Nechamah
- Brit Hadashah: Matthew 5:4 - "Blessed are those who mourn, for they shall be comforted" (ALBUM ANCHOR 2)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said the mourners are blessed, Avinu - and You said Your Name is Nechamah - so the gate I am limping toward is a gate that opens for tears, and the Name on the door knows my grief by name"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of a single piano note held until the breath gives out, like the long held note of a kaddish chant
2. The smell of olive-oil lamps still burning at the threshold at three a.m., the hour the bereaved cannot sleep
3. The texture of stone steps worn smooth by every weeping one who came before me
4. The taste of tears mixing with the first word of the prayer, salt-water Hebrew

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Nechamah (נֶחָמָה) - comfort, consolation. Landed on the chorus high note. The Name IS the prayer at that moment.

[Intro]
(whispered, single piano note held, sparse cello drone underneath)
Yahuah... Avinu... I'm at the gate...
The gate that should be closed... is open...

I come weeping up the road, Avinu,
expecting the gate to be locked from the inside.
Expecting Your courts to be closed for grief,
expecting Your house to belong to the celebrating
and not to the ones still wearing the funeral clothes.
But Your lamps are still burning,
Your hinges are still oiled -
and the Name on the door, oh Yahuah,
Your Name on the door is Nechamah.

because Your Name is Nechamah...

in Your courts, Yahuah-Nechamah.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Nechama — "comfort"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
