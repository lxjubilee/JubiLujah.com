LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, single piano, sparse cello drone, soft smoke-rise sample)
I do not know what to pray anymore, Avinu...
The words run out at the second word...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your Incense Altar, Yahuah,
ready to bring a prayer.
But the prayer breaks in my mouth before the second word.
I have been starting sentences for weeks
that I do not finish.
"Heal them..." trails off.
"Bring them back..." cracks open.
"Why..." goes silent before the question mark.
Your Incense Altar should be full
and the bowl in my hands is empty.

[Pre-Chorus]
But the bowl is not empty, Avinu.
You said Ruach HaKodesh prays inside me
with groanings my mouth cannot finish.
By the blood of the Lamb, my unfinished prayer
is already on its way to Your throne -
She finishes it before I know I have stopped.

[Chorus]
The prayers too heavy to pray, Avinu,
the ones that break in my mouth at the second word -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
Ketoret rising from a bowl I could not fill -
the smoke is older than the lung that cannot breathe it -
She prays the prayer I cannot finish,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

She is praying in those silences.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Ketoret — "incense" — the prayer-aroma offering
- Nechama — "comfort"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
