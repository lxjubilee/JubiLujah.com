SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The bereaved comes to the laver expecting the tears to be washed away - and learns instead that the laver is where Yahuah collects them
- Escalate: The water of the Word reframes the tears - not as weakness to be erased, but as libation Yahuah is keeping in a bottle
- Elevate: The washing is the dignifying of the grief, not its erasure; the consecration is to learn to keep grieving without shame, until the day He wipes the last one

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 56:8 - "You number my wanderings - put my tears into Your bottle - are they not in Your book?"
- Brit Hadashah: Revelation 21:4 - "He shall wipe away every tear from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You count every tear, Yahuah - You keep them in a bottle, You write them in a book - and the day You wipe the last one is the day You wipe them all, but until then, the tears are not waste, they are libation"

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")

- Mayim Chayim — "living water" (cf. John 4:10)
- Nechama — "comfort"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
