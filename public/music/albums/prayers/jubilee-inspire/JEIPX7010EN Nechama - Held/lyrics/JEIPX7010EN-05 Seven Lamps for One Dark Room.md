- Establish: The bereaved enters the inner room where the menorah burns and notices the room they live in is darker than they have admitted
- Escalate: The seven flames of Yahuah are lit over the bedroom, the empty chair, the inside of the grieving chest - and the dark does not put them out
- Elevate: The light does not erase the dark; the light keeps burning IN the dark, and that is the promise the bereaved did not know to ask for

3. The smell of warm olive oil and pine smoke from a wick that has been burning a long time
4. The texture of one's own chest under a hand placed there at midnight, looking for warmth, finding light

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, single piano, sparse cello drone, faint wick-crackle sample)
The room is darker than I thought, Avinu...
And the lamps are burning anyway...

[Verse 1]
I am walking in the valley, Yahuah,
the valley You wrote about in the twenty-third Psalm.
I did not think it would be this dark.
I did not think the night after the funeral
would last this many months in a row.
But Your seven lamps are in the room,
and Your seven lamps are burning,
and the dark has not put them out.

[Pre-Chorus]
Wisdom, understanding, counsel, might,
knowledge, fear of You, Your Ruach -
seven flames over a body that has forgotten the light.
By the blood of the Lamb, Your seven are lit -
and Your seven do not need the dark to leave first
in order to keep burning.

[Chorus]
Seven lamps for one dark room, Avinu -
seven lamps over the bedroom, the empty chair, the inside of my chest -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
The dark has not overcome them -
the dark cannot reach the wick -
Your Or keeps burning,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Verse 2]
The room of the one I lost is dark, Avinu -
the bed where they last lay, the chair they last sat in -
and I have not been able to open the door for weeks.
But Your lamps are inside that room too.
Your seven flames of Your Ruach
do not need me to flip the switch.
They burn in the closed room.
They burn in the closed chest.
They burn even when I, the bereaved,
cannot yet bring myself to look.

Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every dark room of the house laid open and true.
I am not asking You to turn the lights on
the way they were before the loss.
I am asking only to remember Your lamps that ARE burning.
The kind eye that came by with food.
The verse that came up in a passage I did not plan to read.
The Sabbath candles still being lit on Friday night.
Your seven lamps were never out -
my eyes had only forgotten how to see them.

- Nechama — "comfort"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Or — "light"
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
