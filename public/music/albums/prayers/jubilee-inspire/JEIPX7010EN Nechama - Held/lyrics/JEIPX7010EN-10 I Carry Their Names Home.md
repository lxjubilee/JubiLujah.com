- Establish: The bereaved walks into the Mercy Seat room carrying the names of the dying and the dead the way Aharon carried the twelve tribes on his breastplate

- Tanakh: Exodus 28:29 - "Aharon shall bear the names of the sons of Yisrael on the breastplate of judgment upon his heart, when he goes in to the Holy Place, for a memorial before Yahuah continually"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 Thessalonians 4:13-14 - "I do not want you to be ignorant, brethren, concerning those who have fallen asleep, lest you sorrow as others who have no hope - for if we believe that Yeshua died and rose again, even so Elohim will bring with Him those who sleep in Yeshua"

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The weight of one's own hand placed over the chest where the choshen would sit
2. The sound of names being spoken aloud into an empty room, slowly, one by one
3. The texture of an embroidered cloth with twelve stones sewn onto it - smooth gems, rough thread
4. The six-second silence after each name is spoken, the silence in which the prayer rises

- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
