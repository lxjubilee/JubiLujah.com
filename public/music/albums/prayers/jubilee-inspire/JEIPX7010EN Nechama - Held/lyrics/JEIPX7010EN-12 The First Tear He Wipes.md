---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The bereaved walks back out of the Temple carrying not the answer that brings the dead back today, but the Person who will
- Escalate: The thanksgiving is restrained - it is dawn-light, not stadium-light; the grief has not ended, but the One who will end it is already in the room with His thumb moving toward the cheek
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in FULL as the closing confession; the album ends not at the grave but walking back out with Shalom in the body and the resurrection on the calendar

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Isaiah 25:8 - "He will swallow up death forever, and Yahuah Elohim will wipe away tears from all faces" + Psalm 116:15 - "precious in the sight of Yahuah is the death of His saints"
- Brit Hadashah: Revelation 21:4 - "He shall wipe away every tear from their eyes, and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain" + 1 Thessalonians 4:17 - "and so shall we ever be with the Lord"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said You would wipe every tear away - and the first tear You wipe is already on the way - Your thumb is already moving toward my cheek - and the day You wipe the last one is the day You wipe them all, and I will be with You, and they will be with You, and we will all be with You together"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of a thumb wiping a tear from a cheek - slow, tender, deliberate
2. The sound of dawn arriving at a window after a long night of weeping
3. The smell of fresh bread on Sabbath morning at a table where two are now seated, not one
4. The texture of a face being held by hands that have been to the cross and back

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Shalom (שָׁלוֹם) paired with Tehiyah (תְּחִיָּה - resurrection). Landed at the Final Chorus.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aharon — Aaron — the first High Priest, who carried twelve names on his breastplate
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Nechama — "comfort"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shalom — wholeness, peace, completion
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
