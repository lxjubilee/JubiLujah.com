- Establish: The bereaved sits at a kitchen table across from the chair that has been empty for weeks, or months, or years
- Escalate: They speak Hineni - not from a mountain like Avraham, not from a sanctuary like Yeshayahu, but from a kitchen chair across from an empty kitchen chair
- Elevate: The album's anchor pairing (Isaiah 25:8 + Matthew 5:4) lands as the foundation of the petition - the mourner is blessed; the tears will be wiped; comfort is coming

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of an empty chair across a kitchen table with a placemat still set
2. The sound of a coffee maker brewing for one person where it used to brew for two
3. The texture of a folded blanket draped over the back of the empty chair, kept exactly the way it was the last morning
4. The weight of a Hineni spoken not on a mountain or in a sanctuary but in a sock-foot voice at six a.m.

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Hineni (הִנֵּנִי) - here I am. Landed at the Hineni verse, which is the song's emotional core. Paired with Nechamah at the Faith Declaration Bridge.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, single piano, sparse cello drone, the sound of a kitchen at dawn faint underneath)
Avinu... I am at the table...
The chair across from me is still empty...
And I am still here...

[Verse 1]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am at the kitchen table,
across from the chair I have not been able to move.
The placemat is still set the way it was the last morning.
The folded blanket is still on the back of the chair.
The coffee maker is still set to brew for two,
and I have not been able to change the setting yet.
Avraham said Hineni on a mountain.
Yeshayahu said Hineni in a sanctuary.
I am saying Hineni in sock feet at six a.m.
across from a chair that does not answer.

[Pre-Chorus]
You said the mourners are blessed, Yeshua -
You said they shall be comforted.
By the blood of the Lamb that bought the comfort,
the Hineni I am saying from this kitchen chair
counts as much as the one from the mountain.

[Chorus]
Hineni in the empty chair, Avinu -
here I am with the empty plate, the empty cup, the empty seat -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
Bless me, the mourner You said is blessed -
comfort me, the mourner You said You would -
the Nechamah I come for is the Person of Yeshua,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Verse 2]
I have a specific petition, Avinu -
not a general one, a specific one.
There is a date on the calendar that is going to be hard.
There is a holiday I do not know how to do without them.
There is a conversation we never finished
that I am still having in my own head at night.
There is a specific tear I have not cried yet
because I am afraid of what will happen if I let it out.
You said You would wipe every tear away -
every one, Avinu, every one.
Even the ones I have not yet let myself cry.

[Pre-Chorus]
You said the mourners are blessed, Yeshua -
You said they shall be comforted.
By the blood of the Lamb that bought the comfort,
the Hineni I am saying from this kitchen chair
counts as much as the one from the mountain.

[Chorus]
Hineni in the empty chair, Avinu -
here I am with the empty plate, the empty cup, the empty seat -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
Bless me, the mourner You said is blessed -
comfort me, the mourner You said You would -
the Nechamah I come for is the Person of Yeshua,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Instrumental]
(piano niggun, mournful, ney flute breath - 30 seconds, no percussion)

[Verse 3 - Hineni]
(intimate, almost spoken)
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every empty seat at the table laid open and true.
I give You this specific grief.
I give You this specific date on the calendar.
I give You this specific unfinished sentence.
I give You this specific guilt I am still carrying.
I give You the chair that is empty - let it stay empty without breaking me.
Or fill it with Your presence the way You filled the deep at Creation.
Either way, Avinu - Hineni.
I am at the table.
I am not going anywhere.
And You said You would meet me here.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense)
By Your Word, it is done - the mourner is blessed.
By Your stripes, I am held in this kitchen chair.
By the blood of the Lamb, Nechamah has come to the empty seat.
In Yeshua's Name, every tear will be wiped - and I have received.

[Bridge]
(Personal Insertion Beat - 6 seconds, whispered prompt under sparse drone)
(silent - 6 seconds - whispered prompt only, sparse drone underneath)
Bring it before Him now...
(six seconds of held silence - the listener brings the specific grief, date, name, or unfinished conversation into the prayer)

[Bridge]
(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
Hineni... Nechamah... Echad... lai-lai-lai...
The empty chair... lai-lai-lai...
I am held, they are held...

[Refrain]
(Matthew 5:4 + Isaiah 25:8 personalized as personal petition received)
Yahuah - blessed is the mourner I am, for I shall be comforted -
You will swallow up death forever, You will wipe every tear from my face -
even the tear I am crying at this empty chair right now,
even the tear I have not yet cried,
even the tear I do not know is coming yet -
all of them, Avinu, all of them.

[Chorus]
(Final chorus, piano fuller, harmonies blooming on "I am held")
Hineni in the empty chair, Avinu -
here I am with the empty plate, the empty cup, the empty seat -
I am held, they are held,
I am held, they are held.
Bless me, the mourner You said is blessed -
comfort me, the mourner You said You would -
the Nechamah I come for is the Person of Yeshua,
in the blood of Yeshua HaMashiach.

[Outro]

[Outro]
