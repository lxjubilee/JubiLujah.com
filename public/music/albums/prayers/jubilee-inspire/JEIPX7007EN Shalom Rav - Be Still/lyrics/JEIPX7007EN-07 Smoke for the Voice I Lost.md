LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single ney flute exhale, no percussion)
Your altar is glowing, Yahuah...
Your smoke is already rising before You...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your incense altar with a half-prayer, Avinu,
the kind that gets stuck in the throat at three in the morning
between "I am afraid" and "help me."
And You take the half.
You do not ask for the whole sentence.
You take the syllable, the breath, the catch in my voice —
and You set it on Your coals as Ketoret
the way You always have.

[Pre-Chorus]
Your smoke is older than the lung
that could not finish the sentence...
By the blood of the Lamb that sweetened it,
by the body of Yeshua interceding above —
Your Ruach HaKodesh carries the syllable I cannot finish
all the way to where Your bowls are.

[Chorus]

take the half-prayer the chest cannot carry —
I am still, I am still and I know,
I am still, I am still and I know.

I am still, I am still and I know,
at Your altar, Yahuah.
