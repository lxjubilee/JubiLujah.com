LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single ney flute breath, distant water sound, no percussion)
Your laver is full, Yahuah...
Your water has been waiting...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your laver, Avinu,
with a mind that has rehearsed every disaster.
I lean over Your bronze
and Your water moves like Mayim Chayim,
like the creek behind Eden,
like the river that flows from under Your throne.
And the reflection in Your basin
is a face I have not seen in years.

[Pre-Chorus]
And Your water remembers Eden,
Your water remembers me before the worry...
By the blood of the Lamb that consecrates Your basin,
by the body of Yeshua, the Word poured out for cleansing —
I am being washed by Your Word
that washed the world before sin learned to spell.

[Chorus]
Wash the worry off my mind, Avinu —
let Your Mayim Chayim run over my forehead —
I am still, I am still and I know,
I am still, I am still and I know.
Your water remembers Eden,
Your water remembers me,
I am still, I am still and I know,
in Your basin, Yahuah.

[Verse 2]
I have been wearing the worry on my face,
a furrow between my eyebrows that did not used to be there.
I have been cataloging dangers in my sleep.
But Your laver is still here.
Your Mayim Chayim is still moving.
You said the river of worry will not overflow me —
and Your Word does not learn how to lie
just because my chest has rehearsed how to be afraid.

Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
Wash the inside of my skull
where the same six thoughts have been running like a hamster wheel.
You see the rumination on the chart;
You see the rumination under the chart;
You see the child under the rumination —
and the child is the one You called by name.
