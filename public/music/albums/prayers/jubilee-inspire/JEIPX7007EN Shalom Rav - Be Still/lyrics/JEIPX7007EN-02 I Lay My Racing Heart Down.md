LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, no percussion, single ney flute exhale)
Your altar is warm, Yahuah...
Your fire has been waiting for me...

[Verse 1]
I walk from Your gate to Your altar, Avinu,
carrying every what-if from Tuesday to Friday.
I expect You to ask me to lay down something small,
something I can spare without flinching.
But You point at the racing heart,
the closed fist, the rehearsed worry —
and You say, beloved, lay that one down,
that is the one I came for.

[Pre-Chorus]
Teshuvah, teshuvah —
the turning my chest has been waiting to make...
By the blood of the Lamb on the bronze,
by the body of Yeshua broken once for all —
my offering is accepted
because His was first.

[Chorus]
I lay my racing heart down on Your altar,
I lay the worry that has worn my face for years —
You take it, You take it, You take it,
You take it, You take it, You take it.
Your fire does not burn the body,
Your fire burns the worry wrapped around the body,
You take it, You take it, You take it,
to Your altar, Yahuah.

[Verse 2]
I have been carrying this for so long
I do not know my hand without it.
The diagnosis is anxiety —
the prescription is surrender, and I am bad at swallowing it.
But Your bronze does not flinch.
You have stood at this altar for centuries
waiting for the trembling ones
who finally bring the right offering.

- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
