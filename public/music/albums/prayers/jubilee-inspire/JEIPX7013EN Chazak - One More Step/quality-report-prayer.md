## Album-Level Assessment

Chazak - One More Step walks the full twelve-station Temple journey cleanly and without theological slippage. The listener arrives at the Mercy Seat (S9) and is pinned there by Kavod heavier than the weight, then walks back out into Monday morning (S12) with the synagogue cry Chazak, chazak, v'nit-chazek sealed over the next twenty-four hours. The Shema spine is intact (full in S1 and S12, fragment hummed in S2-S11), the Hour-With-Yahuah Frame holds at 60:00 exact, the Yeshua-quoted Gethsemane line is properly stationed at S9 (the only permitted location), and the Cinematic Revelation at S8 ("the strength was walking inside me, the strength was Yeshua all along") lands as a genuine hinge — not a clever rewrite of strength theology but a Galatians 2:20 personalization sung from inside the believer's chest. The album reliably holds the user-specified mood: marching, determined, not triumphant, the steady tread of a sung companion for the one whose only ministry today is to remain.

The strongest stretch is the Act 2 spine, S6 through S9. Bread for One More Mile reframes Eliyahu under the juniper tree as compassion not failure, The Strength Was Walking delivers the album's revelation hinge with the right hush, and Heavier Than the Weight lands the Kavod-as-mass paradox ("the falling was the rising") with the Gethsemane voice personalized in the Hineni — the four-song arc would survive on its own as a worship EP. The weakest stretch — and weakest is relative; nothing falls below 94 composite — is the Act 1 laver (S3) where the chorus tilts slightly toward imperative-voiced address to Yeshua, and Station 7 (Incense) where the petition section runs a notch thin on receipt grammar because the song is, by design, about the groan that cannot articulate. Both are correctable with a single-pass sharpening.

Stations 10 and 11 are correctly engineered: priestly intercession by name (Aharon-Hur archetype) with the Personal Insertion Beat at S10, childlike honest petition with the Personal Insertion Beat at S11, and the album anchor pairing (Isaiah 40:31 + Philippians 4:13) load-bearing in S11. The "I cannot do all things — I can do the next thing through Mashiach who strengthens me" reframe at S11 is the album's quotable theological correction of the most-misquoted verse in the Brit Hadashah and earns its keep. The wet-vocal lament posture is consistently directed in every Style Prompt, and the Whisper-of-Elijah outros are well-chosen (no generic reaches; each line is the right line for the station). The album ships at very high readiness with two small lifts in S3 and S7.

## SOP Compliance Findings

- T3 Wash the Soldier's Feet — MED severity: chorus addresses Yeshua in imperative voice ("wash the soldier's feet, Yeshua") which is technically a petition but reads slightly close to command rather than reverent appeal. Recommended fix: shift one repetition per chorus into receipt voice ("You wash the soldier's feet, Yeshua / and the soldier walks") so the section completes the Promise-Name-Receipt arc rather than staying in petition voice.
- T7 One More Breath of Prayer — MED severity: by design this song is about wordless groan, but the petition is so subdued that the Promise-Name-Receipt receipt beat reads thin. The Faith Declaration Bridge delivers the four beats but the chorus and refrain stay in observational voice. Recommended fix: tighten the FDB receipt language and add a single finished-reality declaration into the chorus tag.
- ALBUM-LEVEL — LOW severity: in T9 Pre-Chorus the phrase "the blood of the Lamb on the Mercy Seat that has spoken better things" is theologically rich (Heb 12:24) but the antecedent of "that" is ambiguous in sung delivery. Recommended fix: optional clarification in T9 ("the blood of the Lamb that speaks better things over the Mercy Seat") — kept LOW because the lyric is correct, only the syntax is dense. Not blocking; KEEP retained.
- No HIGH severity findings. No banned content. No real-artist name drops. Hebrew article rule honored throughout. Gethsemane voice correctly isolated to S9.
