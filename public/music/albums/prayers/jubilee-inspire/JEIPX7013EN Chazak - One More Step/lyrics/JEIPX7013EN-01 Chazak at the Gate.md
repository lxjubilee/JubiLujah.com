SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A bone-tired one limps toward Temple gates that should require the rested and the bright-eyed - and finds them swinging wide because Yahuah's threshold has never required arriving rested
- Escalate: Joy mixed with awe that the gate opens even for the half-step, the dragged foot, the one who almost did not come
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge - the listener confesses Echad while crossing the threshold; the strength has not been given YET but the gate has already been opened

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Joshua 1:9 - "Have I not commanded you? Chazak v'ematz - be strong and of good courage; do not be afraid, do not be dismayed, for Yahuah your Elohim is with you wherever you go"
- Brit Hadashah: Hebrews 12:1 - "let us run with endurance the race that is set before us, looking unto Yeshua the author and finisher of our faith"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You commanded me Chazak, Avinu - and I am limping toward Your gate because You also said You would be with me wherever I go - and the gate is one of the wherevers"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of a single foot dragging on the threshold stone
2. The smell of olive-oil lamps that have been left burning for the late-arriving
3. The texture of the doorpost worn smooth by every tired hand that has steadied itself there
4. The taste of breath that does not know if it is the last one or the first one

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Chazak (חֲזַק) - be strong. Landed on the chorus high note. The command IS the prayer at that moment - the listener is praying Yahuah's own command back to Him.

[Intro]

I come dragging up the road, Avinu,
not running, not flying, just walking.
I expect Your gate to require a rested face,
I expect Your lamps to have gone out by now.
But Your wicks are still burning,
Your hinges are still oiled -
and Your Word above the lintel, oh Yahuah,
Your Word above the lintel is Chazak.
