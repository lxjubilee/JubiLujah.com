LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, ney flute breath sustained, faint smoke shimmer in mix)
The coals are warm, Avinu... I have no eloquence left...
Only a lung that still moves and a groan that does not know its own words...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your Incense Altar with no prayer prepared, Avinu,
the kind of empty where even the request has gone silent.
Just a chest that rises and falls,
just a sigh that does not finish itself,
just a groan Ruach HaKodesh is carrying for me
because I have run out of vocabulary.
The smoke goes up anyway.
One more breath of prayer is already heard by You.

[Pre-Chorus]
The smoke does not require eloquence,
the smoke requires a lung that still moves,
by the blood of the Lamb sprinkled on the same horns of this altar,
by the body of Yeshua who groaned at Golgotha and was heard -
my groan is the prayer
and Ruach HaKodesh is the translator.

[Chorus]
One more breath of prayer, Avinu,
one more breath of prayer is enough -
one more step, one more breath,
one more step, one more breath -
the Ketoret rises off Your coals,
You have already heard the prayer, You have already received the smoke,
one more breath of prayer, Avinu,
in Your Holy Place, Yahuah-Shema.

[Verse 2]
I am bringing a sigh that the doctor would chart as "shallow,"
a request I cannot put into a sentence,
a hope I am too tired to name.
But the coals keep glowing.
Ruach HaKodesh keeps groaning inside the cage of my ribs.
Your altar does not need a finished prayer -
Your altar needs a lung that moves
and a groan She can translate.

Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense)

and offers them at Your altar before Your Throne,
and the smoke ascends, and Your answer descends,

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Chazak — "be strong"
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Ketoret — "incense" — the prayer-aroma offering
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
