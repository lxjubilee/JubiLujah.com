SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener crosses the first veil into the Holy Place - the light changes from sun to lamplight, the lungs are spent
- Escalate: Ruach HaKodesh is named as the second wind every marathon runner has felt without having vocabulary for - the breath that arrives after the breath has been spent
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge declares Romans 8:11 - the same Ruach that raised Yeshua quickens this mortal body now; the listener exhales and the next inhale is Hers

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Isaiah 40:29 - "He gives power to the faint, and to those who have no might He increases strength"
- Brit Hadashah: Romans 8:11 - "The Ruach that raised Yeshua from the dead dwells in you, He shall also quicken your mortal bodies by His Ruach that dwells in you"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You give power to the faint, Yahuah - and the power is a Person, and the Person is Ruach HaKodesh hovering over my emptied lungs the way She hovered over the deep"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The exact moment a runner's second wind arrives - the breath that returns when the breath was supposedly gone
2. The smell of lamp oil burning in a windowless room
3. The sound of Her hovering described as the rush in 1 Kings 19 - not the wind, the kol demamah dakah after it
4. The texture of breath that did not originate in the listener's own lung

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Ruach (רוּחַ) - Spirit, breath, wind. Landed at chorus. The same word for "breath" and for "Spirit" - and the listener's breath has been Her breath all along.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Chazak — "be strong"
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Melachim — Kings
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah-Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema confession)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
