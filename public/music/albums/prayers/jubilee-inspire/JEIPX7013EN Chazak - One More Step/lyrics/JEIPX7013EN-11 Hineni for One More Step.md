SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: After carrying others' names in S10, the listener brings their own depletion before Him - childlike, honest, the specific exhaustion
- Escalate: The Isaiah 40:31 eagle-imagery is reframed - the renewal is not a magic switch, it is a posture of waiting long enough on the wind for the molting to finish
- Elevate: The Personal Insertion Beat - the listener speaks aloud the specific next step they need; the Faith Declaration Bridge declares Philippians 4:13 reframed: "I can do the next single thing through Mashiach who strengthens me"

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Isaiah 40:31 - "They that wait upon Yahuah shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles; they shall run, and not be weary; and they shall walk, and not faint" (ALBUM ANCHOR 1)
- Brit Hadashah: Philippians 4:13 - "I can do all things through Mashiach who strengthens me" (ALBUM ANCHOR 2)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "I am waiting on You the way the eagle waits on the wind for the molting to finish, Yahuah - and Philippians 4:13 does not mean I am unstoppable, it means I can do the next single thing through Mashiach inside me; right now I only need to do the next single thing"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The exact shape of an eagle mid-molt - feathers half-out, half-in, the awkward in-between
2. The sound of wind under wings that have not flown in too long
3. The texture of a single next step measured by the toe of a sandal
4. The taste of breath that is willing to be honest about depletion

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Hineni (הִנֵּנִי) - here I am. Landed at the Hineni verse (which is the song's title and emotional core). Avraham's word, Moshe's word, Yeshayahu's word - and now the listener's word at the floor of the inner sanctuary.

- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
