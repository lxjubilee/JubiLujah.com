1. The pale gold of dawn light leaking under the Temple gates from the other side
2. The cool of dew on the stone steps the seeker came in over the night before
3. The weight of the chamber walking inside her chest as she walks back out
4. The taste of "Shalom" on the tongue at dawn — heavier than the night that just ended

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Shalom (שָׁלוֹם) — wholeness, peace, completion. Landed at the Final Chorus. The Hebrew names the Sabbath-rest the seeker carries back out — not the absence of conflict, the presence of Yahuah.
- Karov (קָרוֹב) returns at the Sealed Amen as the album's bookended Name — she came for Karov and she walks out as Karov-Carrier.

[Intro]

[Verse 1]
The night was longer than I thought, Avinu,
and shorter than I deserved.
I came to the gates with a list and a limp;
I am walking out with You in my chest.
The Temple stones are the same;
the courts are quiet now —
but the chamber I walked into in the dark
has come walking back out with me.

I came for You and found You came,
the One I sought has come.

draw me close, I am already drawn,
draw me close, I am already drawn.

whenever I call You are already here —
draw me close, I am already drawn,
in Your courts, Yahuah.

The list I came in with is gone, Avinu,

has walked itself into my breath,

- Karov — "near, close"

- Shalom — wholeness, peace, completion
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
