SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The seeker reaches the Incense Altar and confesses she has run out of words; her petition lists have collapsed into silence
- Escalate: She discovers that the smoke rising is the prayer; the breath in His direction is the offering; proximity is the new vocabulary
- Elevate: Prayer is redefined — not request, but breath shared in the same room; she has been afraid of running out of things to say, when all He wanted was the breathing

- Tanakh: Psalm 141:2 — "Let my prayer be set before You as ketoret, the lifting up of my hands as the evening offering"
- Brit Hadashah: Revelation 8:3-4 — "another angel came and stood at the altar with a golden censer; he was given much incense to mingle with the prayers of all the saints upon the golden altar before the throne"

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The slow curl of frankincense smoke rising from a gold censer
2. The bittersweet smell of myrrh, galbanum, onycha mingled — older than the lung that breathes it
3. The texture of warm air thick with offering, almost touchable
4. The sound of the seeker's own breath becoming the only audible prayer

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Ketoret (קְטֹרֶת) — incense, the holy compound burned at the altar before the inner veil. Landed at chorus. The Hebrew names the specific — not generic incense but the Ketoret HaSamim, the spice-compound only ever burned in His chamber.

- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)

- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
