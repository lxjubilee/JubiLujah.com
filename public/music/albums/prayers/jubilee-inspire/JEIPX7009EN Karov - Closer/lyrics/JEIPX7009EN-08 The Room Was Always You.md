SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The seeker stands at the inner veil and prepares to push through, expecting to find a chamber, a place, a room she finally arrives at
- Escalate: The veil parts and she does not find a place — she finds a Face; she walked through the curtain and walked into a Person; the room she has been chasing has been Him the whole time
- Elevate: Yeshua's High-Priestly Prayer lands — "that they may be one as We are One"; the Holy of Holies is Oneness; the seeker discovers she has been hidden in Him since before the chase began (Col 3:3)

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Deuteronomy 4:4 — "you who clung (devekim) to Yahuah your Elohim are alive every one of you this day"
- Brit Hadashah: John 17:21-23 — Yeshua's High-Priestly Prayer: "that they all may be one; as You, Father, are in Me, and I in You, that they also may be one in Us" — YESHUA-QUOTED LINE PLACEMENT
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You prayed that I may be one as You and Yeshua are One — and that Oneness is the Room — and the Room is You — devekut is the destination and Yeshua is the door that opens onto You"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The cool weight of a curtain parting against the cheek as it gives way
2. The change of air — not from outer to inner court, but from outside-of-Him to inside-of-Him
3. The texture of being held — a holding that has been happening for years and only now becomes visible
4. The sound of a Voice that says "you are alive every one of you, because you clung"

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Devekut (דְּבֵקוּת) — cleaving, clinging, joining-into. From the verb that describes Adam to Chava ("they shall be one flesh") and that Deuteronomy applies to the seeker clinging to Yahuah. Landed at the revelation moment, Verse 2 climax and the chorus. The Hebrew names the destination — it was never a room, it was always a cleaving.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Calvary — the place of Yeshua's crucifixion — where the veil was torn
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Karov — "near, close"
- Mashiach — "Anointed One" / Messiah
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Parokhet — the inner veil of the Temple, torn at Calvary (Matthew 27:51)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
