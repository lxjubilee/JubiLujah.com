SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The seeker arrives at the bronze altar carrying a stack of telegrams — every prayer she sent in place of coming, every gift offered to keep the distance polite
- Escalate: She recognizes that the religious activity has become the substitute; the telegrams have replaced the trip; the asking has replaced the leaning
- Elevate: She lays the stack on the altar — the fire does not consume her, only the wall she built out of words; teshuvah from transactional prayer into the posture of nearness

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single low tof pulse, distant ney exhale)
Avinu... I'm at the altar...
With the stack in my hands...

[Verse 1]
I have been writing You letters, Yahuah,
when what You want is for me to come.
I have been sending offerings ahead
to make the door less awkward when I arrive.
But the door has never been awkward —
only the daughter has been afraid.
And the telegrams I have been sending in place of coming
are a wall I have built out of words.

[Pre-Chorus]
Teshuvah, Avinu — I am turning,
I am bringing the body that has been writing the letters,
not the letters in place of the body...
By the blood of the Lamb that tore the veil,
I lay the stack down where it should have gone first —
the fire takes the wall, and the wall is gone.

[Chorus]
Burn the telegrams, Yahuah —
every prayer I have sent in place of coming —
draw me close, I am already drawn,
draw me close, I am already drawn.
I do not want to write You anymore —
I want to sit with You, I want to stay —
draw me close, I am already drawn,
on the bronze before Yahuah.

[Verse 2]
I have been offering You activity, Yahuah,
when what You want is attentiveness.
I have been busy in Your courts
because the courts have felt safer than Your chamber.
But the chamber is not far —
the chamber is the whole invitation.
And the gifts I have been sending ahead to keep the distance
are smoke that hides You from my own face.

[Pre-Chorus]

The fire does not consume me, only the wall;
the flame takes the stack and gives me back the door.
You see the daughter under the busyness;
You see the heart under the offering;
You see the want under the want —
and the want under the want is to be with You.

(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, rising)

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Karov — "near, close"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
