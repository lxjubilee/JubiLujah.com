SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A daughter knocks at the Father's chamber at midnight, afraid she has come too late and finding the door already open, the lamp already lit, the seat already pulled out for her
- Escalate: Joy crossed with awe — she came expecting to bargain for a hearing and discovers the Name on the door is Karov; nearness is not earned, it is offered
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge — the seeker crosses the threshold confessing Echad, discovering that nearness IS Oneness, and that the One she has been knocking for has been holding the door open for her since before she walked the road

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 145:18 — "Yahuah is karov to all who call on Him, to all who call on Him in emet"
- Brit Hadashah: James 4:8 — "Draw near to Elohim and He will draw near to you"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said You are Karov to all who call on You — and I am calling, Avinu — and the door of Your chamber is open before I finished knocking"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The hush of a doorframe at midnight after the house has gone quiet
2. The warmth of olive-oil lamp light spilling under a door that should be shut
3. The sound of a chair scraping softly because He has pulled one out for her
4. The taste of breath catching in the throat when grace arrives before the knock lands

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Karov (קָרוֹב) — near, close at hand, kin. Landed on the chorus high note. The Name IS the prayer at that moment — the seeker stops asking for nearness and sings the Name of the Near One.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Karov — "near, close"
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yahuah-Karov — "Yahuah is near"
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
