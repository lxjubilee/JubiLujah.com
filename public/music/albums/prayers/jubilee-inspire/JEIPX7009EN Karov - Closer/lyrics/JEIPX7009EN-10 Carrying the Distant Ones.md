---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The seeker who has just discovered her own nearness now turns toward those she loves who are far; she names them in her chest like Aharon named the tribes on his breastplate
- Escalate: She does not beg for their return from outside the chamber; she carries them into the chamber she has just been received into; the Personal Insertion Beat — listener speaks names of distant beloved aloud
- Elevate: She declares the receipt — Ephesians 2:13 — those once far off are brought near by the blood of Mashiach; she has the receipt and so she has the answer for them

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Isaiah 57:19 — "Shalom, shalom to him that is far off and to him that is near, says Yahuah, and I will heal him"
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 2:13 — "But now in Mashiach Yeshua you who were once far off have been brought near by the blood of Mashiach"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said shalom to him that is far off and to him that is near — and You promised to bring them near by the blood of Mashiach — so I carry their names into the chamber I am standing in"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The weight of a hand laid on the doorframe of a sleeping child whose heart is far from Yahuah
2. The specific names whispered into the dark — names the listener will insert during the Personal Insertion Beat
3. The texture of a breastplate's twelve gemstones over the heart — the priestly carrying made physical
4. The taste of intercession that has stopped tasting like begging and started tasting like welcoming

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Karov (קָרוֹב) returns — but now applied to the distant ones, not the seeker. Landed at Verse 2. The Hebrew word that opened the album as the Name of Yahuah at the door now becomes the answer she carries for those still on the road.

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, distant ney flute, faint tof heartbeat)
Avinu... I came near...
And I am not coming in alone...

[Verse 1]
I have been received, Avinu,
and the receiving has rearranged my chest.
I cannot stand in this chamber
without naming the ones I left at the door.
Sons whose hearts have gone cold,
daughters who do not call You Father anymore,
the spouse who stopped praying with me last winter,
the friend who is asleep in another life.

[Pre-Chorus]
I am not begging You from a distance, Yahuah —
I am carrying them in...
By the blood of the Lamb that brought me near,
by the body of Yeshua that tore the veil for all of them too —
I am Aharon at the breastplate,
twelve names over the heart, and one of them is theirs.

[Chorus]

the ones I carry over my heart like Aharon carried Yisrael,
draw them close, they are already drawn,
draw them close, they are already drawn.

the blood of Mashiach brings every far off near —
draw them close, they are already drawn,
in Your chamber, Yahuah.

[Verse 2]

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aharon — Aaron — the first High Priest, who carried twelve names on his breastplate
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Karov — "near, close"
- Mashiach — "Anointed One" / Messiah
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Shalom — wholeness, peace, completion
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yechezkel — Ezekiel
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
