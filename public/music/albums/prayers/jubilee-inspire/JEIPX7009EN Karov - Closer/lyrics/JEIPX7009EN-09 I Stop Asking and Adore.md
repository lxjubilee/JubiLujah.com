SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The seeker reaches the Mercy Seat and discovers she has run out of requests; the list of asks has collapsed
- Escalate: Only kavod remains; the weight of His Glory falls and presses every petition out of the way; the asking stops because the adoring begins
- Elevate: Gethsemane voice — "not what You give, but Yours; not Your hand, but Your face" — surrender into beholding, the climactic worship of the album

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 27:4 — "One thing I have asked of Yahuah, that will I seek after — to dwell in the house of Yahuah all the days of my life, to behold the beauty of Yahuah, and to inquire in His Temple"
- Brit Hadashah: Philippians 3:8 — "Indeed, I count everything as loss because of the surpassing worth of knowing Mashiach Yeshua my Lord"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "One thing I have asked of You, Yahuah, that I will seek after — to dwell in Your house and behold Your beauty — and Yeshua is the surpassing worth, the One Thing under every thing I used to ask for"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The granular weight of kavod settling on the shoulders like warm rain that has mass
2. The taste of every want dissolving on the tongue except one
3. The texture of a forehead pressed to the floor because legs no longer hold
4. The sound of the seeker's own voice falling silent because the worship is no longer words

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Kavod (כָּבוֹד) — weighty glory, heavy honor. Landed at chorus climax. The Hebrew names what English flattens — glory is not light alone, it has mass; the kavod is the weight that ends the asking.
