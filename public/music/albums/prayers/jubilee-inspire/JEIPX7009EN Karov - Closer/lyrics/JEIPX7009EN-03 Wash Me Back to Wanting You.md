SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The seeker leans over the laver and does not recognize the face the water reflects; the years have crusted over the original thirst
- Escalate: She remembers the night she first wanted Him; she asks for the broken cisterns to be rinsed out of her so the original longing can rise again
- Elevate: The Living Water remembers Eden; she is washed back to the daughter who wanted Him before she wanted anything He could give her

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Jeremiah 2:13 — "they have forsaken Me, the fountain of living waters, and hewn out cisterns, broken cisterns that can hold no water"
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:26 — "that He might sanctify her, having cleansed her by the washing of water with the Word"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You are the fountain of mayim chayim — I hewed out broken cisterns and tried to hold You there — wash the broken cisterns out of me and restore the thirst that only You can satisfy"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The cool weight of water in a bronze basin, still after the dipping
2. The reflection of a face the seeker has not seen in years — her own face before the wandering
3. The taste of water that remembers being a spring before it was poured
4. The texture of skin cleaned of the residue of every substitute well

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Mayim Chayim (מַיִם חַיִּים) — living waters, the running spring, the unsourced source. Landed on the chorus. The Hebrew names the difference between the cistern and the spring.
