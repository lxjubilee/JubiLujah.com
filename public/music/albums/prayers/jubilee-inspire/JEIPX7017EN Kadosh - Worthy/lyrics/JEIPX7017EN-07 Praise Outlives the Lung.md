SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener arrives at the incense altar with a chest that is already getting tired of singing - and learns the smoke is what carries the song after the lung gives out
- Escalate: She sees in her mind the golden bowls of Hitgalut 5:8 - the prayers of the saints are still rising long after the saints have laid down; every praise she sings tonight will outlive her
- Elevate: The bridge declares finished-reality: "the lung will stop one day - the praise will not"; the song that has been carried by her chest is now carried by the smoke that has been waiting for it

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Tehillim (Psalm) 141:2 - "let my prayer be set before You as incense, the lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice"
- Brit Hadashah: Hitgalut (Revelation) 5:8 - "the four living creatures and twenty-four elders fell before the Lamb, each having golden bowls full of incense, which are the prayers of the saints"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "every praise I sing tonight is being collected in a golden bowl - the smoke will outlast the lung that breathed it - the song does not end when the body does"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The smell of ketoret - frankincense, stacte, onycha, galbanum - drifting up from the golden altar
2. The texture of the smoke rising in slow, deliberate ribbons that never break before the curtain
3. The sound of the listener's own breath beginning to get shallow under the weight of the praise hour
4. The sight in her mind's eye of the golden bowls heavy with the incense of her own prayers, held by the elders

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, ney flute as thin smoke-line)
Ketoret... incense rising...
the praise the lung cannot carry...

[Verse 1]
Your altar is small and gold, Avinu,
right in front of Your inner veil -
and the coals are already lit
from a fire I did not start.
I come in to add my praise to the bowl,
and I notice the smoke is older than my breath.
Saints I have never met
were burning incense here
before I had a name.

[Pre-Chorus]
And the elders are holding the bowls, Yahuah -
golden bowls full of ketoret,
which are the prayers of the saints,
and one of the bowls is mine -
not because I am special,
because the blood of the Lamb
opened Your incense altar to me too.

[Chorus]
Praise outlives the lung, Yahuah -
ketoret, ketoret,
the smoke is older than the breath that breathes it.
My lung will stop one day,
my praise will not -
the golden bowls are full, Avinu,
and the song I sing tonight is rising forever -
You alone are worthy, Yahuah,
Praise outlives the lung.

[Verse 2]
You said it through Tehillim 141, Adonai -
"let my prayer be set before You as incense" -
and David said it from a cave,
and I am saying it from this courtyard,
and a child somewhere will say it
when I am dust under a Jewish star -
and Your same smoke
will carry all three of us
to Your same Throne.

[Verse 3 - Hineni]
(intimate, almost spoken, lung-shallow)
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every shallow breath laid open and true.
My chest is small tonight, Adonai -
the praise is bigger than the lung carrying it.
But the smoke is patient.
The smoke is faithful.
The smoke will carry what my breath cannot finish -
and one day the bowl will be full,
and the song I start tonight
will still be rising
when my breath is long since done.
