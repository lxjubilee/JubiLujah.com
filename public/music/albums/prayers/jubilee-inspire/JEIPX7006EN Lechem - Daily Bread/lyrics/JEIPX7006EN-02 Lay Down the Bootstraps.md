- Establish: The worker arrives at the bronze altar with thirty years of self-made provision pulled up by the bootstraps — and finds the altar fire is not consuming the body but the boot-straps themselves
- Escalate: Teshuvah — every idol of self-provision named aloud and laid across the bronze; the god called Hustle, the god called Side-Hustle, the god called I-Got-This
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge — the worker stops pulling and starts receiving; the hands that white-knuckled the ledger open palm-up for the first time in years

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The smell of leather burning on bronze — the bootstraps the worker pulled on for thirty years
2. The weight of the hands going slack for the first time in a working life
3. The sound of the altar fire that does not consume the body but the idolatry wrapped around it
4. The taste of teshuvah in the back of the throat — turning, returning, salt and ash

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Teshuvah (תְּשׁוּבָה) — turning, returning, repentance. Landed at the pre-chorus build. The act of turning is the prayer at that moment.
