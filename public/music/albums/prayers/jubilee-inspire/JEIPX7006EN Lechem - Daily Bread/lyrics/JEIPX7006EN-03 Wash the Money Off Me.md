SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The worker, having laid down the bootstraps, kneels at the laver and looks at hands that have learned the small daily compromises money begs us to make
- Escalate: The small money-lies named one by one — the rounding-up, the looking-away, the corner-cut, the resentment at the boss, the envy at the neighbor — the water of the Word washing each one off the hands
- Elevate: The hands rise clean, ready to hold the manna that has not yet fallen but is already in motion

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Ezekiel 36:25-26 — "I will sprinkle clean water on you and you shall be clean... a new heart will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you"
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:26 — "the washing of water by the Word" + 1 Timothy 6:10 — "the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yahuah, sprinkle clean water on the hands that handled the money — wash the small lies the dollar bills taught the fingers, wash the resentment off the wrists, wash the envy off the palms"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of water poured over copper basins — the laver
2. The texture of soap working between the fingers where the bills were counted
3. The smell of clean water after a long day of counting what was not enough
4. The sight of hands rising from the laver pink and new, the small lies rinsed off the cuticles

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, soft tof heartbeat, the sound of water poured low in mix)

[Verse 1]
I kneel down at Your bronze laver, Yahuah,
and I look at my hands in the water.
I see the small lies the dollar bills have taught my fingers —
the rounded-up receipt,
the looked-away coin,
the corner I cut on a Tuesday afternoon
that no one but You ever saw.
Your water is still rising in the basin.
Your water is still asking me to put my hands in.

the water that remembers Eden...

at Your laver, Yahuah-Yireh.

- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings

- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)

- Yechezkel — Ezekiel
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
