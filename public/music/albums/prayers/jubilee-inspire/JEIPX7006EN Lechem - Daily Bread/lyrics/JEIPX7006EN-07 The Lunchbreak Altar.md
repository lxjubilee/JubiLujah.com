LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, the soft hiss of a paper coffee cup being lidded, distant break-room hum)
Avinu... it's twenty minutes...
And the sandwich is small...

[Verse 1]
I park the car at twelve-fifteen, Yahuah,
and I unfold the sandwich on the dashboard.
The coffee is already going cold.
I start a prayer I know I will not finish
before the alarm sends me back inside.
And then I feel it — the steam from the cup,
the breath I am about to say next —
they are already up in Your bowls,
already incense in Your hand,
already received before I finish asking.

[Pre-Chorus]
Ketoret, Ketoret,
Your smoke that knows my name...
By the blood of the Lamb sprinkled on the altar,
by the body of Yeshua, my High Priest —
the half-prayed prayer is the whole prayer,
and the break room is Your Holy Place.

[Chorus]
The lunchbreak altar, Yahuah —
the smoke from the parked car rises like Aharon's —
give us this day our daily bread,
give us this day our daily bread.
The half-prayed prayer is the answered prayer,
the bowls already hold what I had not finished saying,
give us this day our daily bread,
in the Ketoret of Yahuah-Yireh.

[Verse 2]
I bring You the prayer I could not finish at the desk,
the petition I started in the elevator,
the cry I started in the break room and lost when the door swung open.
I push them up the column of smoke.
The Levites are still singing.
Your courts do not require a long prayer —
Your courts receive the short one
the way You received Aharon's incense.

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The alarm is going to ring in three minutes.
The prayer is going up faster than the second hand.
You see the man who prays in his car;
You see the woman who prays in the supply closet;
You see the worker who prays into the steam —
and the worker is the one You called by name.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, rising)
By Your Word, the Ketoret is rising — and so I have been heard.
By Your stripes, I am free to pray the short prayer in the parked car.

In Yeshua's Name, my great High Priest — the answer is already at the door of the break room.

(Psalm 141:2 personalized)
Yahuah, my prayer is set before You as incense,
the lifting of my hands as the evening sacrifice —
even when my hands are holding the steering wheel,
even when my hands are holding the folded sandwich,
even when my hands have only twenty minutes
to be lifted at all.

[Chorus]
