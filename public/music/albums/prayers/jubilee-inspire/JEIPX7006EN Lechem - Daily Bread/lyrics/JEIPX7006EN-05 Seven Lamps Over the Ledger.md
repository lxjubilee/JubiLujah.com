LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, the soft snap of a single match being struck, then a single flame)
Avinu... the ledger is open...
Light the lamps...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your menorah, Yahuah,
with my household ledger spread on the floor
and a pencil dull from too much erasing.
I do not come asking for more money.
I come asking for eyes.
Let me see what You see
when You read the page I have been mis-reading.
Light the first lamp — wisdom for the column.
Light the second — understanding for the row.

[Pre-Chorus]
And Your lamps are catching, Avinu,
one by one over the page...
By the blood of the Lamb that kindled the oil,
by the body of Yeshua, Light of the world —
the page is changing under Your light,
and Your verdict is not what I thought.

[Chorus]
Seven lamps over the ledger, Yahuah —
let me read the page the way You read it —
give us this day our daily bread,
give us this day our daily bread.
The columns are not what I read them as,
the famine is a feast already in motion,
give us this day our daily bread,
in Your light, Yahuah-Yireh.

[Verse 2]
I bring You the red ink and the second-guessed budget,
the line item I cannot make smaller,
the verdict I have been pronouncing over my own family.
I lift them under Your light.
The Levites are still singing.
Your courts do not require a closed column —
Your courts receive a page held up
under the seven lamps of Your Ruach.

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The ledger is still open. The numbers have not moved.
But Your light over the page has.
You see the man who has been pronouncing famine over a feast;
You see the woman who has been preaching scarcity over a sufficiency;
You see the household reading the page in the dark —
and the household is the one You called by name.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, climbing into the key change)
