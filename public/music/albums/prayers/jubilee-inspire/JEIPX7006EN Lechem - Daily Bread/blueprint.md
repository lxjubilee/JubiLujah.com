---

ALBUM VISION STATEMENT

This is the oldest prayer there is, sung over the longest worry there is. "Give us this day our daily bread" — eleven words Yeshua handed His own disciples when they asked Him to teach them how to pray. The album sets those eleven words inside the 12-station Temple journey for the worker whose paycheck does not stretch to the end of the month, for the mother who is rationing the pantry, for the father whose contract was not renewed. By the time the Sealed Amen of Track 12 has decayed into the Whisper-of-Elijah, Yahuah Yireh has been spoken over the bank account, the lunchbox, and the ledger; the manna has fallen for THIS day; and the listener has not heard a provision album — the listener has prayed one.

This is not music about money. It is daily bread, sung. Tefillah b'shir for the table that is set by the Father of the worker.

THREE-ACT MAPPING

ACT 1 — Outer Court (Tracks 1–3): Approach. The worker arrives at the gate of Yireh, lays the empty hands and the late paycheck on the bronze altar, is washed at the laver of the small daily compromises money begs us to make. Tone: bright on Track 1 (the gate of provision swings open), surrendered on Track 2 (every idol of self-provision laid down), cleansed on Track 3 (the small money-lies washed off).

ACT 2 — Holy Place to Mercy Seat (Tracks 4–9): The veil. The longest, deepest stretch — Ruach HaKodesh intimacy in the breadline, the seven lamps over the ledger, Yeshua as the bread the bread cannot replace, prayer-as-incense in the lunch-hour break room, the inner veil torn so the Table walks in, the kavod fall over the household. Tone: hushed, candlelit, steady-working, climactic on Track 9.

ACT 3 — Intercession, Petition, Thanksgiving (Tracks 10–12): The worker intercedes for other workers in lack by name, brings the household's own bills and rent and tuition before Him, walks back out into the workday carrying an answered prayer. Tone: surgical compassion (10), childlike honesty (11), released brightness (12).

TESTIMONY ANCHOR DOCUMENTATION

Testimony Source: The lived intercessory practice of the household — carrying other households' financial burdens before the Mercy Seat. Aharon carrying twelve names on his chest before the Mercy Seat is the priestly archetype. The household has paid other households' bills in seasons of plenty and has had its own bills paid by intercessors in seasons of lack; both sides of that intercession are sung into Track 10.
Sensory details drawn from the real practice:
1. The physical sensation of folding a check into an envelope for someone who does not know who is paying
2. The specific weight of intercession — heavier than emotion, lighter than the answered prayer; the weight of carrying a name and a dollar figure simultaneously
3. The six-second silence where the names AND the figures are spoken aloud (Personal Insertion Beat)
Universal bridge: every believer is a priest (1 Peter 2:9). The listener does not need a Temple to carry names — the listener IS the Temple (1 Cor 6:19), and the carrying happens at the kitchen table when the bills come in the mail addressed to someone else.

CINEMATIC REVELATION MOMENT

Track 08 "The Table Walked In" — Verse 2, second line.
"The Shulchan did not stay in the Temple after the veil tore — it walked itself into my kitchen and sat down next to the bills."
