LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, faint resin-burning sound)
Yahuah... I am breathing thin tonight...
But the smoke is already going up...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your incense altar, Yahuah,
because my lungs have been tight all week.
I have not finished a prayer in three days -
my sentences keep breaking
because the news keeps moving faster than my mouth.
And then I see the smoke at Your altar,
and the smoke is not waiting for me to finish.
The smoke leaves the lung the moment the prayer begins.

[Pre-Chorus]
Ketoret, Yahuah, ketoret -
my prayer is already smoke,
the smoke is already in Your angel's bowl,
the bowl is already at Your throne.

[Chorus]
Yahuah - the smoke rises faster than fear,
my prayer leaves the lung before the news arrives -
under Your wing, under Your wing,
You requisitioned the answer before I cried.
By the blood of the Lamb on Your altar of incense,
by Your Ruach who carries what the lung cannot,
under Your wing, under Your wing,
ketoret arrived; Your answer is on the way down.

[Verse 2]
The smoke is older than the news cycle, Yahuah -
older than the phone in my hand,
older than the threat in the email,
older than the fear that woke me at 4 AM.
You set Your altar before the world began arguing,
You set the bowls before the kingdoms started falling -
my prayer rises into a system
You built to receive it
before the trouble was ever named.

[Pre-Chorus]
Ketoret, Yahuah, ketoret -
I have received before the news reaches me,
You lit the bowl before the lung could finish,
and You sent the answer before the threat could speak.

every hidden room laid open and true.

I cannot finish the sentence about the diagnosis.
