LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, faint sound of bread being torn slowly)
Yahuah... I am hungry from the watch...
And the Table is set...

[Verse 1]
I have been a watchman, Yahuah,
of a body and a household and a phone that does not stop ringing.
I have been a watchman of the nursery,
a watchman of the highway my husband drives at night,
a watchman of the news cycle,
a watchman of the borders of my own peace.
And the watch has emptied me out.
And I come to Your Table because I have forgotten how to be fed.

[Pre-Chorus]
Lechem HaPanim, bread of the Presence,
bread of the face that does not sleep -
break Yourself for the watchman, Yeshua,
the way You broke for the body of every other watchman.

[Chorus]
Your Bread for the watchman's hour, Yahuah -
broken so the watchman does not have to break -
under Your wing, under Your wing,
You keep the city, I lay it down.
By the body of Yeshua, broken for the long night,
by Your Bread that does not run out,
under Your wing, under Your wing,
You, the Watchman of Yisrael, do not sleep.

[Verse 2]
You said in the Psalm, Avinu,
that unless You keep the city,
the watchman stays awake in vain.
And I have been awake in vain
because I have been the watchman of things
You never asked me to watch.
Take the watch back. Take the keys back.
Give Your beloved sleep -
and put Your Bread of the Presence in my hand on the way to bed.

[Pre-Chorus]

every hidden room laid open and true.
I have been a small Saul on the throne of my own watch,
a small David in the cave of my own panic,
a small Peter falling asleep in Gethsemane on the wrong watch -
forgive me, feed me,
break Your Bread that does not run out,
and put it in my mouth before I forget how to be fed.

[Bridge]
