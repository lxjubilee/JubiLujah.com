SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: 4 AM — the hour the body wakes uninvited and the mind starts; the watchman is most alone here; the listener says her own Hineni, not as ritual but as truth
- Escalate: Psalm 30:5 personalized — "weeping endured for the night, but joy comes in the morning" — sung from inside the night, not after it; the discovery that Yahuah is already awake at 4 AM with her
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge — the night the listener cannot rush, the morning she cannot fake; both are kept by the One who is awake at 4 AM with her; the morning is real even when it has not arrived; the Personal Insertion Beat is where she names what she has been carrying

1. The specific feel of a digital clock reading 4:00 AM — the cold blue of the display
2. The texture of cold floor under bare feet at the moment of getting out of bed when the body waked uninvited
3. The taste of half-cried tears at the edge of the lip
4. The sound of a furnace clicking on in an empty house — the only sound at 4 AM

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, faint furnace-click sample, cold-blue ambient texture)
Avinu... it's 4 AM again...
You are already awake...

[Verse 1]
Hineni, Yahuah — here I am at 4 AM,
the hour the body wakes uninvited,
the hour the mind starts before the eyes open,
the hour I have been most alone for months.
Except You were already awake at 4 AM —
Yeshua kept the same vigil in Gethsemane —
and the furnace clicking on in the empty house
is not the only sound after all.

[Pre-Chorus]
And I am bringing what I have been carrying alone —
the depression, the prodigal, the diagnosis,
the marriage in winter, the financial midnight —
by the blood of Yeshua at Your Mercy Seat,

I lay my own long night before You now.

[Chorus]

joy comes in the morning.

the morning I cannot fake,
boker comes, boker comes,
You are awake at 4 AM with me.

[Verse 2]

the way Yeshua was awake in Gethsemane
when nobody else would watch one hour with Him.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Boker — "morning"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament

- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")

- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
