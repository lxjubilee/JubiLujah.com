SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener arrives at the Bronze Laver in the dark; the laver fills with night-sweat and night-tears as much as it ever filled with priestly washing — the water knows what to do at midnight
- Escalate: The discovery that the water in the laver still runs clear by lamplight; the wash does not require daylight; Ezekiel's clean water is poured even in the dark hours
- Elevate: The Word washes the night-mind; the listener rises from the basin with a face that has been cleaned of the weeks of weeping, ready to cross into the Holy Place; tahor is the receipt beat

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Ezekiel 36:25 — "then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and you shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you"
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:26 — "that He might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the Word"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You sprinkle clean water on me even at this hour, Avinu — the Word washes what the dark has caked on my face — and I am tahor by lamplight, the laver did not need daylight to know me"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sight of water in a bronze basin reflecting lamp-flame, perfectly still
2. The cold of clean water on a face that has been weeping for nights
3. The sound of water poured from a clay pitcher onto bronze — the small ring
4. The sensation of salt being lifted off the skin and replaced with sweetness

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, water-poured-onto-bronze sample, gentle ney flute breath)
Avinu... the laver is full...
Even at this hour, the water is clear...

[Verse 1]
I come to the Bronze Laver, Yahuah,
not sure Your water would still run clean by lamp.
I have been weeping so long, I assumed the basin
would be salt by now, would be too thick to wash.
But Your laver does not need the sun to know my face.
Your water rises clear at midnight.
The clay pitcher tips and Your bronze rings —
and the night-tears on my face begin to lift.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim Chayim, Mayim Chayim —
Your living waters poured even in the dark,
Your Word is the water, the water is the washing,
by the blood of Yeshua at Calvary,
by the side of the Lamb that ran with water and with blood —
Your laver runs clear in the night.

[Chorus]
Your water runs clear at night, Avinu,
Your laver knows my face by lamp —
boker comes, boker comes,
joy comes in the morning.
The salt of the weeks lifts off my skin,
Your Word washes my face by flame,
boker comes, boker comes,
in Your basin, Yahuah, at midnight.

[Verse 2]
I have been wearing the weeks like sweat on the brow,
salt caked from the long-night weeping.
Your laver does not flinch from what I bring.
Your water remembers Eden before the wound;
Your water remembers my face before the long night.
You sprinkle clean water and the idols of fear
lift off my skin like salt rinsed clean.
Your wash is finished while Your lamps still burn.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim Chayim, Mayim Chayim —
Your living waters poured even in the dark,

[Chorus]
Your water runs clear at night, Avinu,

The salt of the weeks lifts off my skin,

boker comes, boker comes,

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, mournful and ornamented, water-droplet samples, oud counterpoint — 30 seconds)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
Wash my face that has cried into the pillow.
Wash my hands that have wrung themselves dry.
Wash my eyes that have not seen morning in too long.
Wash my mouth that forgot how to pray with sweetness.
Your Mayim Chayim poured from the side of the Lamb
runs over me and I am tahor.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, rising into the clean)
By Your Word, it is done — the laver has received me.
By Your stripes, the salt of the long night is washed and the wound is sweet.
By the blood and water from the side of the Lamb, I have access — the basin is full.
In Yeshua's Name, I am tahor — tahor — tahor — I have already been cleaned.

(Niggun Bridge — wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
(Shema melody contour hummed)
Tahor... Mayim... Echad... lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Ezekiel 36:25-26 personalized)
You sprinkle clean water on me and I am clean —
You take from me a heart of stone
that the long night has hardened —
and You give me a heart of flesh
that can feel the boker when it comes.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings turning sweet, layered Shekhinah harmonies, sense of skin being clean)
Water runs clear at night, Avinu,
the laver knows my face by lamp —
boker comes, boker comes,
joy comes in the morning.
The salt of the weeks lifts off the skin,
the Word washes the face by flame,
boker comes, boker comes,
in the basin of Yahuah at midnight.

[Outro]

(breath returning to silence, final water-droplet, then silence)

(Whisper-of-Elijah — almost inaudible)
"Wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow." — Tehillim 51:7
