SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener arrives at the Bronze Altar carrying the night itself — the lie that they have been holding the dark up alone; the weight of weeks of vigil they were not asked to keep
- Escalate: The teshuvah moment — the listener turns and lays down not their sin alone but their attempt to be their own Shomer; the fire is not lit by them, the watch was never theirs to keep
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge — the fire burns the burden, not the body; the listener is released from the weight of carrying the night

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 51:17 — "the sacrifices of Elohim are a broken spirit: a broken and a contrite heart, O Elohim, You will not despise"
- Brit Hadashah: Romans 12:1 — "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of Elohim, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto Elohim, which is your reasonable service"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "I lay down a broken and contrite heart, Avinu — I lay down the long night I was never supposed to carry alone — I lay down the watch I have been trying to keep without sleep, and I let Your altar receive what was always Yours"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The weight of an actual stone laid on the chest during long sleeplessness — the sensation of the burden becoming bronze under the hand
2. The smell of cedar smoke rising from the altar fire while the sky is still ink
3. The sound of cloth dropped on hot stone — the sizzle of something heavy released
4. The texture of cold sweat that finally stops because the body has been told it can let go

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Teshuvah (תְּשׁוּבָה) — turning, returning, repentance, coming home. Landed at the Pre-Chorus as the turning moment. The word IS the prayer at that turning.

- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
