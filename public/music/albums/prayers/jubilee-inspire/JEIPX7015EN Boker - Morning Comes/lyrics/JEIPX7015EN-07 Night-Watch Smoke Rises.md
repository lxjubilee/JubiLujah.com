LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, single coal-crackle, faint resin-smoke texture)
Avinu... the prayer is small...
Smaller than it should be...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your golden altar, Yahuah,
with a prayer too small to send.
My lung is short. My voice is thin.
The words I had at the start of the night
have worn down to a single sentence.
But Ketoret is not measured by lung capacity, oh Avinu —
Ketoret is measured by You who receive it,
and You receive even a whisper.

[Pre-Chorus]
Your angel at Your altar holds the censer,
Your angel adds the incense to my breath,
the smoke goes up far higher than my lung can lift it —
by the blood of Yeshua, my High Priest forever,
by Yeshua who ever lives to make intercession —
my small whisper reaches Your throne.

[Chorus]
My night-watch smoke rises, Avinu,
Your Ketoret carries my prayer to Your throne —
boker comes, boker comes,
joy comes in the morning.
My small lung lifts the whisper,
Your angel adds the incense to it,
boker comes, boker comes,
my Ketoret of midnight rises.

[Verse 2]
A choir at noon prays louder than I do —
but Your altar does not measure prayer in decibels.
You said the prayer of the righteous avails much, Yahuah —
not because of the lung, but because of the Lamb.
My whisper at 3 AM reaches Your throne
as far as the shout at the great festival,
because the smoke is righteous in Yeshua's Name,
not in mine.

[Pre-Chorus]
Your angel at Your altar holds the censer,
Your angel adds the incense to my breath,
the smoke goes up far higher than my lung can lift it —
by the blood of Yeshua, my High Priest forever,
by Yeshua who ever lives to make intercession —
my small whisper reaches Your throne.

[Chorus]
My night-watch smoke rises, Avinu,
Your Ketoret carries my prayer to Your throne —
boker comes, boker comes,
joy comes in the morning.
My small lung lifts the whisper,
Your angel adds the incense to it,
boker comes, boker comes,
my Ketoret of midnight rises.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, mournful and ornamented, smoke-rise texture, oud counterpoint, distant censer-chime — 30 seconds)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The prayer I tried to pray at the start of the night
has worn down to one word — but the one word is Yours.
Take the one word. Make it Ketoret.
Take the half-finished sentence. Make it smoke.
Take the prayer my chest cannot afford to finish —
and let Your angel finish it at Your Mizbach HaZahav.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, smoke rising)
By Your Word, it is done — Your censer is full.
By Your stripes, my small lung is healed enough to whisper.
By the blood of the Lamb on Your Mizbach HaZahav, I have access to Your throne.
In Yeshua's Name, my prayer is Ketoret — Ketoret — Ketoret — I have already been heard.

(Niggun Bridge — wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed; harmonies rising like smoke)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
(Shema melody contour hummed)
Ketoret... Mizbach... Echad... lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Hebrews 7:25 + Revelation 8:3-4 personalized)
Yeshua, You ever live to make intercession for me —
You stand at Your Mizbach HaZahav, the golden altar,
and the incense You add to my whisper
makes the smoke of my small prayer
rise higher than the cedars of Lebanon.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings, layered Shekhinah harmonies as smoke rising in column)
Night-watch smoke rises, Avinu,
the Ketoret carries the prayer to Your throne —
boker comes, boker comes,
joy comes in the morning.
The small lung lifts the whisper,
the angel adds the incense to it,
boker comes, boker comes,
the Ketoret of midnight rises.

[Outro]

(breath returning to silence, final resin-crackle)

(Whisper-of-Elijah — almost inaudible)
"He is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto Elohim by Him, seeing He ever lives to make intercession for them." — Ivrim (Hebrews) 7:25
