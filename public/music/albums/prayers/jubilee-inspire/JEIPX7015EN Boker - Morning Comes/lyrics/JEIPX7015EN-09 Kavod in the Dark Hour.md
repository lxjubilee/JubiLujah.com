- Establish: The listener stands before the Mercy Seat after the cinematic revelation of Track 8; the body knows resurrection is real even before sunrise; the knees give
- Escalate: The Gethsemane voice — "not my will, but Yours, even in the long night"; permitted only at this single station per master manual; Yeshua's own dark-hour prayer becomes the listener's
- Elevate: The kavod descends; it is heavier than the night; the body collapses under glory instead of grief; the diagnosis of the soul is finally weighed against something heavier than itself

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Exodus 33:18 — "I beseech You, show me Your kavod"
- Brit Hadashah: John 17:24 (Yeshua's High-Priestly Prayer) — "Father, I will that they also, whom You have given Me, be with Me where I am; that they may behold My kavod, which You have given Me: for You loved Me before the foundation of the world" — YESHUA-QUOTED LINE PLACEMENT
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Father — You gave Yeshua the kavod before the world was — let me behold it now, in the dark hour, before the morning — let me see what He sees on this side of the boker"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sensation of weight settling on the back of the neck — heavier than grief, slower than fear
2. The sound of forehead touching stone floor — a soft thud, then silence
3. The visual of light that has mass — not photons, presence
4. The feeling of the body collapsing toward a floor that holds, like falling into water that does not drown

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Kavod (כָּבוֹד) — weighty glory. Landed at the chorus climax.
- Secondary Hebrew: Kapporet (כַּפֹּרֶת) — Mercy Seat. Landed at refrain.

[Intro]

I come to Your Kapporet, Yahuah,
Your Mercy Seat between the cherubim,
and my knees give the way Moshe's gave at the cleft.

but I am past dangerous tonight.
I have been carrying the weight of the dark for so long

to press me to Your floor.

let Your kavod fall heavier than the dark.
