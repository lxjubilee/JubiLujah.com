## Album-Level Assessment

Slicha - Clean walks the full Temple journey from outer-gate mud-on-hem to final-gate weight-lifted shalom without a single station missed, mistimed, or mismanaged. The listener arrives at the Mercy Seat in S9 with a clean floor under prostrate hands — the Yeshua-quoted Gethsemane line ("not My will, but Yours be done") rises from the only station where Gethsemane voice is permitted, and the Kavod settles with theological precision rather than emotional vagueness. Promise-Name-Receipt grammar is installed in every Faith Declaration Bridge; Two-Witness Confirmation is present in every song with Tanakh + Brit Hadashah anchors; the Shema is sung in full at S1 and S12 and carried as melodic fragment through S2-S11; Personal Insertion Beats are correctly placed at S10 ("Speak their name now...") and S11 ("Bring it before Him now...") with no leakage to other stations.

The album holds its user-specified mood — "starts heavy, ends light, you can hear the weight lift" — with rare control. The deepest worship-weight stretch is S2-S3 (bronze altar confession and laver washing) where Jubilee's normal +10% praise intensity is correctly decanted into midwife-at-the-basin warmth rather than revival brightness. The audible weight-lift production shift lands precisely at S8 ("Cleaner Than I Was Born") where Hebrews 9:14 is made experiential — the revelation that the blood did not return the believer to baseline but took them PAST infancy is the album's theological hinge and lands without sentimentality. S10 carries the testimony-anchor weight of priestly intercession through Aharon's choshen typology with the Personal Insertion Beat acting as the literal moment of name-bearing. S11 is the album's intimacy summit — the unspeakable held in the ALL of 1 John 1:9 — and S12 walks the listener back out the gate carrying shalom with the accusation-list "does not exist anymore." The album refrain "wash me, begin again" installed at S3 anchors melodic memory across every subsequent track.

Strongest stretch: S8-S12 (Cleaner Than I Was Born → Blood Spoke Louder) — five consecutive tracks at composite 95-96 that constitute one continuous revelation arc from "I have been forgiven beyond what I knew to ask" through Mercy Seat surrender, priestly intercession, the unspeakable absorbed, and walk-out into shalom. Weakest stretch: there is no actually weak stretch on this album — the lowest composite is S7 at 94, and even that is theologically sound (Isaiah 6 seraph-coal, Ephesians 4:29 cleaned-mouth, Ketoret at peak, Ruach HaKodesh carrying the prayer the lung cannot). S7 sits in the wider album frame as a transition track between the homecoming meal (S6) and the cinematic revelation (S8), so its lower lift is functionally appropriate — but it can be lifted from 94 to 95 with a sharper Promise-Name-Receipt closure in the Refrain.

Not a flag — Jubilee's persona voice tolerates the warmth — but noted for future sharpening if any revision pass touches the file. Decision: KEEP (the warmth is the persona; do not over-correct).
