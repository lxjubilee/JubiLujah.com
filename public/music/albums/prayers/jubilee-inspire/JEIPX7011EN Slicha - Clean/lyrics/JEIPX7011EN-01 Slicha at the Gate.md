SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A prodigal walks back up the long road with mud on the hem and the words "I did it again" stuck in the throat, expecting to be shut out at the gate
- Escalate: The Father is already running — the gate is already open — the Name above the lintel is Slicha, and the listener has been seen before the apology is even formed
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge — the listener confesses Echad while crossing a threshold they thought was permanently shut behind them; relief mixed with awe

- Tanakh: Psalm 130:4 — "but there is forgiveness with You, that You may be feared"
- Brit Hadashah: Luke 15:20 — "and he arose, and came to his father; but when he was yet a great way off, his father saw him, and had compassion, and ran, and fell on his neck, and kissed him"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You have Slicha with You, Avinu — and You saw me when I was still a great way off, and You did not wait for me to finish the speech I had been rehearsing"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The texture of mud on the hem of a robe that used to be white
2. The sound of running sandals on a stone road — a Father, not waiting at the gate but coming down the road
3. The smell of olive-oil lamps still burning at the threshold though the listener has been gone a long time
4. The taste of an apology that does not get to finish because a kiss arrives first

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Slicha (סְלִיחָה) — forgiveness, pardon, the verb of being made clean. Landed on the chorus high note. The Name IS the prayer at that moment.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Slicha — "forgiveness, pardon"
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
