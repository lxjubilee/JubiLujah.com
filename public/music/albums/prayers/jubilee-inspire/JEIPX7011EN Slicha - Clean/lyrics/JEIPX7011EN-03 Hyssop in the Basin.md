---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The texture of hyssop branches dipped in the basin — the lowly weed Yahuah specified, not a cedar or a palm
2. The cold of laver water on a hot forehead, the shock of it, the way it stops the breath
3. The sight of mud running off the hem into the basin and the water remaining clean somehow
4. The sound of the listener's own breath catching when "whiter than snow" stops being a metaphor

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Tahor (טָהוֹר) — clean, ritually pure. Landed on the chorus high note where the album refrain "wash me, begin again" arrives.
