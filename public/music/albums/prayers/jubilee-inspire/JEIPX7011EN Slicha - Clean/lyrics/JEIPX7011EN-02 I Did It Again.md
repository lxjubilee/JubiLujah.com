SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The just-arrived prodigal kneels at the bronze altar — and instead of generic confession, names the specific thing they came back for
- Escalate: The fire of the altar does not consume the body — it consumes the sin wrapped around the body; teshuvah is not annihilation, it is separation
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge anchors 1 John 1:9 in finished-reality voice — confessed, forgiven, cleansed, in Yeshua's Name

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 51:3-4 — "for I acknowledge my transgressions, and my sin is ever before me; against You, You only, have I sinned, and done this evil in Your sight"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 John 1:8-9 — "if we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves, and the truth is not in us; if we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" (ALBUM ANCHOR 2 — installed here, reprised at S11)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "I acknowledge my transgressions, Avinu, and the sin is ever before me; against You, You only, have I sinned — and You said if I confess, You are faithful and just to forgive and to cleanse from all unrighteousness, and that ALL is doing all the work"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The texture of stone under bare knees at the bronze altar
2. The smell of altar fire — wood and the specific scent of something being burned that was not the body
3. The sound of the listener's own voice saying the sin out loud for the first time
4. The weight in the chest the moment before confession and the lightness the moment after

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Teshuvah (תְּשׁוּבָה) — turning, returning, the verb of coming back. Landed on the pre-chorus crest, then echoed in the Faith Declaration Bridge.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- David — King David
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- HaMashiach — "the Messiah" (Ha- is Hebrew "the")
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Slicha — "forgiveness, pardon"
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
