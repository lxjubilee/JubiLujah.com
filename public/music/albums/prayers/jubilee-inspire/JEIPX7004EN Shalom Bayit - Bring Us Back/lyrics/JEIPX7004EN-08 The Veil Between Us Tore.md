SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener stands at the inner veil, the second Parokhet, ready to ask for permission to cross — convinced the wall between her and the estranged one will only come down by years of work
- Escalate (REVELATION): The wall she has been beating on with her fists is the same Parokhet Yeshua already split top-to-bottom; the same tear ran across every wall built between two people on Calvary; the door has been open the whole time
- Elevate: Faith Declaration Bridge — the access is not a place, it is a Person who walked through both rooms; the listener IS the carrier of the torn veil; healing flows because she is the access point now

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Exodus 26:33 — "the veil shall divide unto you between the holy place and the most holy"; with Isaiah 59:2 — "your iniquities have separated between you and your Elohim — your sins have hid His face from you"
- Brit Hadashah: Matthew 27:51 — "behold, the veil of the Temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom"; with Ephesians 2:14 — "He is our shalom, who has made both echad, and has broken down the middle wall of partition between us"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yeshua, You said You are our shalom — You have made both one — and the middle wall of partition between us, You broke it down at Calvary; the wall I have been beating on with my fists has been torn since the day You bowed Your head; I do not have to break it down; I have to walk through the door You already opened"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The image of the listener's fists on a wall that is already split top to bottom — and her not noticing
2. The exact line where the veil was torn — top to bottom, in one motion, the day Yeshua bowed His head
3. The sound of cloth ripping in a finished tense — the past-tense rip that is still echoing forward into every wall
4. The doorway that was a wall yesterday — the door frame still warm where the cloth used to hang

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Parokhet (פָּרֹכֶת) — veil. Landed at the revelation moment in Verse 2 — the album's cinematic hinge.

[Verse 1]
I come to the inner veil ready to ask permission, Avinu.
Ready to beg for the door to be opened a crack.
Ready to put both hands on the wall between me and her
and to push until my hands are bloody.

I have been pushing for seventeen years.
I have been pushing since the day she said the thing
and I said the thing back.
And You stand beside me, Avinu, and You smile — softly —

- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
