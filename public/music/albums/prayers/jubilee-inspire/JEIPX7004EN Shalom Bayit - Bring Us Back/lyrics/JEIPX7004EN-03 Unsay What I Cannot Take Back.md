SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener stands at the Bronze Laver, looking down into the water expecting to see her face, and instead sees her mouth — and Yahuah is washing the mouth
- Escalate: The water knows what was said; the water washed Eden once; the water can wash the words she cannot take back
- Elevate: Faith Declaration Bridge declares the past-tense washing already done — the words are unsaid in the heavenlies even when the other person still remembers them; the listener rises in newness with a new mouth

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Proverbs 18:21 — "death and life are in the power of the tongue"; with Ezekiel 36:25 — "I will sprinkle clean water upon you, and you shall be clean — from all your filthiness and from all your idols will I cleanse you"
- Brit Hadashah: James 3:5-6 + Ephesians 5:26 — "the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity — set on fire of Gehenna"; "cleansed by the washing of water by the Word"; with Hebrews 9:14 — "the blood of Mashiach, who through the eternal Ruach offered Himself without spot to Elohim, shall cleanse your conscience from dead works to serve the living Elohim"; and John 19:34 — "one of the soldiers with a spear pierced His side, and forthwith came there out blood and water"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Avinu, You said You would sprinkle clean water on me and I would be clean — and the blood and the water from Yeshua's pierced side at Calvary cleanse the conscience from the dead works of the tongue; the verdict in the heavenlies outranks the memory on earth"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The reflection of a mouth in the laver water — not a face, a mouth
2. The exact words I wish I had not said — the cadence, the tone, the moment
3. The texture of water on the lips, like a Communion of unsaying
4. The image of Eden being washed back — the water remembering what came before the wound

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Mayim Chayim (מַיִם חַיִּים) — living waters. Landed at the chorus. The water that is alive is the only water that can unsay what was already spoken.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, water-like wash on the strings)
Avinu... I am at the laver...
And I see my mouth in the water...

[Verse 1]
I come to the bronze basin expecting to wash my hands.
But You tilt the water toward my mouth, Avinu,
and You say — daughter, that is where the wound came out;
that is where the washing begins.
The water knows.
The water remembers Eden.
Your Ruach hovered over the water
on the first day, before any wound was spoken.
And Your water is going to unsay what I cannot take back.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim Chayim, Mayim Chayim —
the water that washed creation, washing my mouth.
By the blood of the Lamb that bled for the tongue I gave,
by the body of Yeshua on the cross —
Your water is unsaying what I said,
even when she still remembers it.

[Chorus]
Unsay what I cannot take back, Avinu —
wash the cadence, the tone, the moment, the look on my face —
I lay it down, I am made whole,
I lay it down, I am made whole.
Maaa-yim Chaaa-yim — Mayim Chayim over my mouth —
the water remembers Eden,
bring us back, make us echad,
at Your laver, Yahuah-Mekadesh.

[Verse 2]
She still remembers, Avinu. I know it.
The words are still in her ears — they sleep with her at night.
But You say — daughter, the water from My Son's side at Calvary
is older than the moment they were spoken;
the blood and the water from the open spear-wound
cleanse the conscience from dead works of the tongue.
And the version of you that spoke them
is the version I am drowning in this basin right now —
and the verdict in the heavenlies outranks the memory on earth.

[Instrumental]

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
I have an old mouth and I want a new one.
I have a script I have spoken her into for years —
the version of her that lives in my retellings,
the version that fits the wound I gave her —
and I lay the script down at the laver.
You see her as she is, not as I describe her.
Wash the description.
Wash the rehearsal.
Wash the woman I have made her in my own mouth.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, rising)
By Your Word, the water has already washed me.
By Your stripes, I am new from the mouth down.
By Your blood of the Lamb, the words are unsaid in the heavenlies.
In Yeshua's Name, I have received a new tongue and a new ruach within me.

(Niggun Bridge — wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed)

Echad... lai-lai-lai...
Yahuah Echad...

[Refrain]
(Psalm 19:14 + Psalm 141:3 personalized)
Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart
be acceptable in Your sight, O Yahuah, my Rock and my Redeemer.

keep the door of my lips.
And the words I said before You set the watch —
wash them, Avinu, wash them in Mayim Chayim

the water remembers Eden,
bring us back, make us echad,
at Your laver, Yahuah-Mekadesh.

(breath returning to silence)
