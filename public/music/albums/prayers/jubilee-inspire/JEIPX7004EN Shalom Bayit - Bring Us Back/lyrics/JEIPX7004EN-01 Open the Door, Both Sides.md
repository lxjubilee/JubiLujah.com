SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener arrives at the Outer Court carrying a broken relationship — half her fault, half not — fearing the gate will be shut to someone who comes with a fracture instead of a clean offering
- Escalate: The gate opens anyway, because the door has two handles and the Father's hand is on both at the same time — and because Yeshua already ran out toward the prodigal "while he was yet a great way off"
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge — the listener confesses Echad while crossing the threshold; she has come for shalom in the house and the gate already knows her name AND the name of the one she is carrying in

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 100:4 — "Enter His gates with thanksgiving, and His courts with praise — be thankful unto Him, and bless His Name"
- Brit Hadashah: Luke 15:20 — "while he was yet a great way off, his father saw him, and had compassion, and ran, and fell on his neck, and kissed him"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "I come to Your gate carrying the broken thing, Avinu — and You see me a great way off, while I am still rehearsing my excuses — and You run toward me, and the door opens from both sides at the same time"

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shalom — wholeness, peace, completion
- Shalom Bayit — "peace of the house" — household peace, marriage peace, family harmony
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yahuah-Shalom — "Yahuah our peace" (Judges 6:24)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
