---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener has crossed both veils and stands at the Mercy Seat — and there is a second crown in her hand she did not know she was carrying; the crown of the silent one, who is not here in person but whose crown is hers to cast because they were ever echad
- Escalate: Yeshua's High-Priestly Prayer is sung in personalized voice — "that they all may be echad, as You and I are echad"; the listener can no longer remember who hurt whom first because the kavod has fallen on both heads in the same posture
- Elevate: Gethsemane voice ("not my will, but Yours") is permitted ONLY here per master manual §VIII; the listener releases the outcome — she can want reconciliation, but she releases the timing, the form, the answer to Avinu

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Exodus 33:18-19 — "I beseech You, show me Your Kavod — and He said, I will make all My goodness pass before you"; with Genesis 33:4 — "Esau ran to meet him, and embraced him, and fell on his neck, and kissed him; and they wept"
- Brit Hadashah: John 17:21-23 (YESHUA-QUOTED LINE PLACEMENT) — "that they all may be echad — as You, Father, are in Me, and I in You, that they also may be echad in Us — that the world may believe that You have sent Me"; with Matthew 26:39 (Gethsemane) — "not as I will, but as You will"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Father — let us be echad — as You and Yeshua are echad — let the love You loved Him with be in us — let the same Echad heal the two of us across this table — that the world may know You sent Him. And if the reconciliation comes in the seen — let it come. And if it only comes in the unseen — let it come. Not my will, but Yours."

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. Two crowns in one hand — the listener's crown and the silent one's crown — both cast at the same Throne
2. The image of Yaakov and Esav weeping together after twenty years, both ruined men, both held
3. The weight of kavod pressing two heads down to the floor in the same posture
4. The unbearable mercy of releasing the outcome — Gethsemane voice permitted only here

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Kavod (כָּבוֹד) — weighty glory. Landed at the chorus climax. The kavod is heavier than the grudge.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Kavod — weighty glory; the manifest weight of Yahuah's presence
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shalom Bayit — "peace of the house" — household peace, marriage peace, family harmony
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yaakov — Jacob, later called Yisrael
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yochanan — John
