SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A wounded one limps up to the Temple gate with both hands full of years of carried things, expecting to knock - and discovers the hand cannot knock while it is clutching
- Escalate: The relief of being told to put it down at the threshold; joy mixed with awe as the listener realizes the gate was waiting for empty hands all along
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge - the first confession across the threshold is Echad, sung with open palms

- Tanakh: Psalm 100:4 - "Enter His gates with thanksgiving, and His courts with praise"

- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said come heavy-laden and I will give you rest - so I came carrying everything, and at the gate I am learning the rest begins when the hands open"

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of a fist trying to knock and making no sound because the knuckles are full of something else
2. The smell of olive-oil lamps still burning at the threshold after a long walk
3. The texture of the gate-handle worn smooth by every empty-handed one who came before me
4. The taste of breath catching when the gate opens before the knock arrives

[Intro]

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- B'Yadcha — "into Your hand" (Psalm 31:5; Luke 23:46)
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
