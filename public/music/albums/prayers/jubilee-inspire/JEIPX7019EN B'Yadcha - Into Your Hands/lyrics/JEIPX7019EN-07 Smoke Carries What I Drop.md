SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener stands at the golden incense altar; she has been trying to lift her hands in prayer with things still clutched in them - and the smoke will not rise from a closed fist
- Escalate: The lifted hands of Psalm 141:2 are by definition open hands; the smoke carries upward what the hands released downward; casting cares as the opening of the palm so the incense can rise
- Elevate: Faith Declaration Bridge - the cares are cast, the smoke rises, the bowls in heaven fill

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 141:2 - "Let my prayer be set forth before You as incense; and the lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 Peter 5:7 - "Casting all your care upon Him; for He cares for you"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "I lift my hands as the evening sacrifice - but I cannot lift them with anything in them; the casting of all my cares is the opening of my hand, and the prayer rises as incense the moment the hand lets go"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The fragrance of ketoret - the eleven-spice incense blend smoldering on golden coals
2. The visual of smoke that does not rise straight up but spirals, the way an honest prayer spirals
3. The weight of a worry the listener thought she was praying about but was actually clutching
4. The relief of an open palm letting smoke pass through it instead of trying to hold the smoke

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, ney flute breath like smoke rising)
The incense altar is here, Avinu...

[Verse 1]
I have been praying with closed hands, Yahuah -
I confess it now in Your smoke.
Lifting fists, calling it intercession,
asking for things I would not release into Your keeping.
The lifted hand of David's psalm
was open, Avinu - open.
The evening sacrifice does not rise
from a palm still wrapped around the answer.

[Pre-Chorus]
You said cast all my cares -
not whisper them and keep them -

I cast, You receive -
I cast, You receive.

rise, rise, rise to the bowls in heaven -
b'yadcha, b'yadcha,
Your smoke carries what I drop.

is not the prayer I planned to pray -

I would not say out loud before tonight.
