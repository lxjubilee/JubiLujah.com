PRODUCTION METADATA

- Music Styles: Extended Messianic Jewish prayer ballad, cantorial/hazzan tradition, 5-minute slow-burn dwell, resolving into major brightness at the Final Chorus
- Vocal Gender: Female lead with full layered Shekhinah harmonies; the brightest vocal of the album, sung walking back out the gates carrying the answer
- Weirdness Score: 29%
- Style Influence Score: 70%
- Tempo (BPM): 66 (the album's most lifting tempo)
- Key: D minor resolving to D major at Final Chorus (rare modal lift on this album - the Walk-Out moment)
- Song Length: 5:00 (Prayer Song override of 3:30-4:00)
- Hook Delivery Timestamp: waived per Prayer Song spec; "He caught what I released" hook lands at 0:48

---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener releases - and the realization arrives that He was already underneath, already catching, already holding; the relief is not "I let go" but "He had it the whole time"
- Escalate: Walking back out of the Temple gates with shalom because the thing was never out of His hand to begin with - only out of the listener's plan; the Shema is sung in full as the closing confession
- Elevate: Major-key Final Chorus - the album's only resolving lift - confirming He caught everything; the answer is already in the heavenlies, the body is catching up to the verdict

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 116:1-2 - "I love Yahuah, because He has heard my voice and my supplications. Because He has inclined His ear unto me, therefore will I call upon Him as long as I live"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 John 5:14-15 - "And this is the confidence that we have in Him, that, if we ask anything according to His will, He hears us: and if we know that He hears us, whatsoever we ask, we know that we have the petitions that we desired of Him"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You heard me, Yahuah - You inclined Your ear to me - and the petitions I committed b'yadcha, You have caught; I walk back out the gates knowing You have it, because You had it before I released it"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of the listener walking back out the Temple gates with both palms still open - the open-handed walk
2. The smell of the same olive-oil lamps that lit her arrival, now lighting her departure with a different fragrance in her hair
3. The texture of a hand that has been kept open long enough to start to feel that "open" is its true shape
4. The sound of the Shema sung in full as the closing confession over the released life

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Shalom (שָׁלוֹם) - wholeness, peace, completion. Landed at the Final Chorus where the modal resolution lifts.
