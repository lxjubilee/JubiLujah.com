SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The listener stands at the bronze altar with the things she has been clutching and recognizes them as idols-of-outcome - not evil things, but things she had to control
- Escalate: Teshuvah - the turning that puts the outcome on the altar instead of in the hand; the fire does not consume the listener, only her grip
- Elevate: Faith Declaration Bridge in finished-reality voice; the grip has been burned off, the hand is open, the body is offered

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Psalm 51:17 - "The sacrifices of Elohim are a broken spirit: a broken and a contrite heart, O Elohim, You will not despise"
- Brit Hadashah: Romans 12:1 - "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of Elohim, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto Elohim, which is your reasonable service"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "The sacrifice You will not despise is the broken grip - I present my hand, palm up, as a living sacrifice, and the fire takes only what was wrapped around my fingers, never the finger itself"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The dull weight of bronze that does not warm to the touch even with the fire on it
2. The smell of cedar smoke mixed with the iron of the altar railing
3. The texture of a palm that has been clenched so long the calluses know the shape of what was held
4. The sound of something releasing in the fire that the listener did not know she had brought

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Teshuvah (תְּשׁוּבָה) - turning, returning, the deep U-turn of the will. Landed at the Pre-Chorus. The word IS the prayer at that moment.

[Intro]

I come to Your bronze altar, Yahuah,
to lay down the things I confess.
But Your fire has a way of asking
what else is sewn into the lining.
The outcome I made an idol of -
the future I will not release -
the picture in my head of how this story ends -
Your altar is asking for those too.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- B'Yadcha — "into Your hand" (Psalm 31:5; Luke 23:46)
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Calvary — the place of Yeshua's crucifixion — where the veil was torn
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Mashiach — "Anointed One" / Messiah
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
