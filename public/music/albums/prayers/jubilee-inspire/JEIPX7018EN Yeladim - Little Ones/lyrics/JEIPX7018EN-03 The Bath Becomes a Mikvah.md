SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: An ordinary nightly bath — warm water in a small tub, a child's giggle, a cup pouring water over the small head
- Escalate: The realization that the bath is a sacramental mikvah — every drop carrying the Word, every pour a consecration over the small one's life
- Elevate: The wash that returns the child to the cleanness they were born from; the parent prays the laver-prayer over the bathtub

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Ezekiel 36:25 — "then will I sprinkle clean water on you, and you shall be clean; from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you"
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:26 — "that He might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the Word"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said, Yahuah, You would sprinkle clean water on us and we would be clean; You said Yeshua washes by the water of Your Word — and tonight the bath water is the laver, and the cup that pours over her head is the cup that consecrates her"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of warm water filling a small tub — the rhythmic rush of the spout
2. The temperature of water tested against the inside of a wrist
3. The visual of a wet small head being held back so soap does not sting eyes
4. The smell of lavender shampoo and bath bubbles, and underneath it the smell of Eden remembered

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Mikvah (מִקְוֶה) — gathering of waters, immersion, the ritual bath of consecration. Landed at the chorus. The ordinary bath becomes the holy water.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Eden — the garden where Yahuah walked with humanity in the cool of the day
- Hesed — covenant love, steadfast mercy
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Mikvah — ritual immersion pool; sanctification by water
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeladim — "children, little ones"
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
