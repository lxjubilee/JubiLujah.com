SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A parent stands at the Mercy Seat and asks for the kavod of Yahuah to fall over the child's life
- Escalate: Yeshua's High-Priestly Prayer in John 17:24 — "Father, I will that they... behold My glory" — sung as the parent's prayer for the child to be one of the ones the Father gave to Yeshua
- Elevate: The kavod is weight, not just light; the heaviest blanket ever woven settles over the crib; Gethsemane voice ("not my will, but Yours") permitted here only — the parent lays down the script for the child's life and lets Yahuah be the cover

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Exodus 33:18 — "show me Your Kavod"; Exodus 40:34 — "the kavod of Yahuah filled the Mishkan"
- Brit Hadashah: John 17:24 (Yeshua's High-Priestly Prayer — YESHUA-QUOTED LINE) — "Father, I will that they also, whom You have given Me, be with Me where I am; that they may behold My glory, which You have given Me: for You loved Me before the foundation of the world"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yeshua prayed, Father — let the ones You gave Me be with Me and behold Your kavod — and tonight I am asking, Yahuah, let my small one be one of the ones You gave Him, and let her behold Your kavod on this side of the dawn"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of a heavy weighted blanket settling slowly over a sleeping child
2. The sound of a room going still in the way only the kavod makes still
3. The sensation of weight pressing the parent to the floor next to the crib
4. The smell of cloud — the same cloud that filled the Mishkan, suddenly familiar

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Kavod (כָּבוֹד) — weighty glory. Landed at the chorus climax. The kavod is mass; it settles like a blanket; it is the cover the parent stops trying to be.
