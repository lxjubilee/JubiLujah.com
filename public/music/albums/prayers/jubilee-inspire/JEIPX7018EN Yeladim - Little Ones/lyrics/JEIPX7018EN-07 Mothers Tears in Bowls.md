- Brit Hadashah: Revelation 5:8 — "golden bowls full of incense, which are the prayers of the saints"; Romans 8:26 — "the Ruach helps our infirmities; for we know not what we should pray for as we ought, but the Ruach itself makes intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said, Yahuah, You put my tears in Your bottle and they are in Your book — and the bowls in heaven are full of incense which are the prayers of the saints — and the Ruach intercedes with groans I cannot voice; my 3 AM crying tonight is not wasted, it is the incense You are gathering"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The salt taste of a tear that runs into the corner of the mouth at 3 AM
2. The sound of a mother trying to cry silently so the small one does not wake
3. The visual of incense smoke rising in slow curls toward a high ceiling
4. The texture of a wet pillowcase that no one else will know about

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Dim'ah (דִּמְעָה) — tear. Landed at chorus. Every tear has a name in heaven.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, ney flute long breath, subliminal sound of slow steady drip)
Yahuah... I cannot finish the prayer...
The words are not coming...
But the tears are...

[Verse 1]
You wrote it in Tehillim, Avinu,
"put my tears in Your bottle, are they not in Your book" —
and Yochanan saw in Revelation
golden bowls of incense at the throne, the prayers of the saints.
And tonight I understand, Yahuah,
the bottle on Your shelf and the bowl at Your throne
are the same vessel —
and my Dim'ah is Your incense rising while I cry.

by the body of Yeshua who groaned at the grave —
the cry that has no words
is the incense You are smelling tonight.

[Chorus]
Mothers' tears in bowls, Avinu —

You waste not one of my tears,
You waste not one of my tears.

You waste not one of my tears,
in Your smoke, Yahuah-Avinu.

I have wept for the child who is sleeping in the next room, Avinu,
and the child who is not sleeping anywhere I can see,

You know all of them.
You wrote all of them in Your book.

and Your bowl is at Your throne and Your throne hears.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun mournful and curling, ney flute long breath response, oud counterpoint, sparse tof heartbeat — 30 seconds; the niggun is a curl of smoke rising; no resolution, only ascent)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room of my mother's heart laid open and true.
I do not know how to pray for her tonight, Yahuah,
the words have stopped, the throat is closed.
But the tears keep coming,
and You said the tears are prayers,
and Your Ruach is in the room
turning every drop into Your incense before it hits the pillow.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, climbing out of the weeping into receipt)
By Your Word, every Dim'ah is gathered — not one wasted, it is done.
By Your stripes, the cry is heard — the bowl is full at the throne.
By the blood of the Lamb who wept first, I have access — and the cry has reached You.
In Yeshua's Name — the Ruach has carried what I could not say, the answer is on the way, and I have received the prayer that finished itself in heaven.

(Niggun Bridge — Shema melodic fragment hummed within the lai-lai-lai)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
(Shema melody contour hummed, sounding like a controlled cry being smoothed)
Dim'ah... Echad... lai-lai-lai...
Tears as incense... Echad...

[Refrain]
(Romans 8:26 personalized over the mother and the child — Hesed v'Emet at the peak, direct address)
Yahuah, Your Ruach helps my weakness —
when I do not know how to pray as I ought,
Your Ruach makes intercession with groans I cannot voice,
and You who search the hearts know the mind of Your Ruach.
Hesed v'Emet — Your mercy and truth meet over Your bowl at Your throne —
because She intercedes for the saints according to Your will,
and the small one I weep over is a saint You have already heard about.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble soft, weeping strings, layered Shekhinah harmonies curling upward like smoke; Dim'ah lifted to the chorus high note)
Mothers' tears in bowls, Avinu —
Dim'ah — Your bottle is heavy and Your bowls at the throne are full —
You waste not one of my tears,
You waste not one of my tears.
Your Ruach carries what the mouth cannot say,
Dim'ah is the incense and You are inhaling it,
You waste not one of my tears,
in Your smoke, Yahuah-Avinu.

[Outro]

(breath returning to silence, last drip of a tear hitting the pillow audibly)

(Whisper-of-Elijah — almost inaudible)
"They that sow in tears shall reap in joy." — Tehillim 126:5

- Avinu — "our Father"

- Hesed v'Emet — "mercy and truth together"

- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeladim — "children, little ones"
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yochanan — John
