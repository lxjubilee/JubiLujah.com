SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A parent stands at the bronze altar with a small one in arms and realizes the grip they have on the child has become an idol
- Escalate: Hannah at Shiloh — she vowed Shmuel back, and the moment her arms emptied was the moment heaven filled
- Elevate: The Faith Declaration Bridge — every grip, every fear, every plan I made for them is laid down, and the child is given back to the One who gave the child

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: 1 Samuel 1:27-28 — "for this child I prayed; and Yahuah hath given me my petition... therefore also I have lent him to Yahuah; as long as he liveth he shall be lent to Yahuah"
- Brit Hadashah: Romans 12:1 — "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of Elohim, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable, which is your reasonable service"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said, Yahuah, that the one I begged You for was a loan, not a possession — and Hannah lent him back as long as he lived; You said I am to present a living sacrifice, and the first living sacrifice on my altar tonight is the grip I had on this small one"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The metallic smell of the bronze in the morning light, cold against the cheek
2. The exact weight of a child going limp in trusting sleep as the parent finally lets the shoulder relax
3. The sound of Hannah's voice at Shiloh — lips moving without sound (1 Samuel 1:13)
4. The texture of empty arms in the seconds after laying a child down

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Nadar (נָדַר) — to vow, to dedicate, to give back what was given. Landed at the pre-chorus where Hannah's vow is the parent's vow.

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, oud single note, no percussion yet)
Yahuah... I come to Your altar...

is the thing You asked for...

[Verse 1]
Hannah came to Shiloh, Avinu,
moving her lips with no sound,
and Eli thought she was drunk
because heartbroken praying looks like that.
She did not ask for a child to keep —
she asked for a child to give back.
And the day she gave Shmuel to the Temple
was the day the womb of Yisrael opened wide.

[Pre-Chorus]
Teach me her vow, Yahuah,
teach me her Nadar...
By the blood of the Lamb on Your altar,
by the body of Yeshua broken for me —
I come to lay it down,
not to hold it tighter.

the grip and the plan and the fear that he won't survive me —

I lay it down, I give him back.
You gave him to me, and I lend him to You,
as long as he lives he is lent to You,
I lay it down, I give him back,
on the bronze of Yahuah-Avinu.

[Verse 2]
I bring You the future I scripted for him,
the college, the calling, the wife, the road —
the silent expectations I never said out loud
but built around his small life like a fence.

does not consume the child —
it consumes the grip I had,
and the small one is unhurt and the grip is gone.

[Instrumental]

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu — here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room of my parenting laid open and true.
I confess the idolatry of the grip —
I confess I was holding him so tight he could not turn to You.
Burn the grip, Yahuah —
burn the fear that fathered the grip —
burn every plan I made for him that I did not first lay down,
and leave the small one alive on the altar like Yitzhak unbound.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge — finished-reality, present tense, rising soft into receipt)
By Your Word, the Nadar is sealed — it is done.

In Yeshua's Name — he is Yours, and I have received the empty arms as full.

(Romans 12:1 personalized over the parent, direct address to Yahuah)

and the first body I present is the body of this parenting —
my arms a living sacrifice,
my fears a living sacrifice,
the grip I had a living sacrifice,
and the small one on Your altar a child the way Yitzhak was a child —
about to be unbound by Your hand.

(full ensemble soft, weeping strings, layered Shekhinah harmonies, Nadar rises at the peak)

You gave him to me, and I Nadar him to You,
as long as he lives he is lent to You,

on the bronze of Yahuah-Avinu.

(breath returning to silence, the bronze cooling, the altar quiet)

- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah

- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yeladim — "children, little ones"
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
