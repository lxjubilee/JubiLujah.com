SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A parent kneels and says, "I cannot do this — I cannot mother her, I cannot father him, I cannot keep doing this on my own"
- Escalate: The aching womb of the barren one; the grief of the lost child; the parent who is afraid they are getting it wrong; the parent who lost the child whose name they cannot say
- Elevate: Yahuah-Avinu Himself — the Father of every father, the Mother-heart of every mother (Isaiah 49:15) — answers by parenting the parent first so the parent can parent the child; the Personal Insertion Beat operationalizes the petition

- Tanakh: Deuteronomy 6:6-7 — "these words... shall be in your heart, and you shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise up" (ALBUM ANCHOR 1); Isaiah 49:15 — "can a woman forget her sucking child... yea, they may forget, yet will I not forget you"

- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said, Yahuah, the words You commanded would be in my heart and I would teach them diligently to her — and You said You will not forget me the way no nursing mother forgets her infant — and Yeshua said let the small ones come — Hineni, here I am, the parent You gave the child, and the Parent who fathers and mothers through me is You"

1. The exact silence in a house where a child is supposed to be and is not

4. The sound of a parent saying "I am sorry" to a child who is too small to know they were wronged

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeladim — "children, little ones"
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
