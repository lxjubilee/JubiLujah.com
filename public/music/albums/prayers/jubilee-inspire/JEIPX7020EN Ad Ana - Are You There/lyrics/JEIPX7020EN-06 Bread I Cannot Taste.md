SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The doubter sits at the Table of Showbread with a mouth full of dust - the Bread is there; the appetite is not
- Escalate: The Bread does not depend on the appetite; the Presence is Continual; Yeshua does not stop being the Bread because she has stopped tasting
- Elevate: She eats anyway - not because she can taste yet, but because the Bread is still on the table, and the eating is the trust even when the tasting has not returned

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Leviticus 24:5-9 - "And you shall take fine flour and bake twelve loaves of it... And you shall set them in two rows, six on a row, upon the pure table before Yahuah. And it shall be on every Sabbath he shall set it in order before Yahuah continually"
- Brit Hadashah: John 6:35 - "I am the Bread of Life: he who comes to Me shall never hunger; and he who believes in Me shall never thirst"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yeshua, You said You are the Bread of Life - that whoever comes to You shall never hunger - I am coming with a mouth that has gone dry; I am eating because You are still the Bread, not because I can still taste; the twelve loaves continually set before Yahuah include the one with my name on it"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The smell of warm bread on a table in a quiet room - the smell that arrives even when the mouth has forgotten how to want it
2. The sight of twelve loaves arranged in two rows of six - the same arrangement Aharon set
3. The texture of dust in the mouth that has not been able to swallow gladness in months
4. The warmth of the loaf in the hand - heavier than expected, the way Yeshua's body was heavier than expected at Pesach

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Lechem HaPanim (Bread of the Presence, literally "bread of the faces" - the bread that beheld His face continually) - landed at chorus.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, faint kitchen-warmth in the mix)
Your bread is still warm, Avinu...
Your table is still set...
My mouth is dry...

[Verse 1]
I sit down at Your Table of the Showbread, Yahuah.
The twelve loaves are there.
The same loaves Aharon set,
the same arrangement the Levites kept -
two rows of six, Lechem HaPanim,
the Bread of the Presence beholding Your face continually.
I tear a piece.
I put it in my mouth.
I cannot taste it.
Forgive me, Avinu, for thinking that means
the Bread is not Bread.

[Pre-Chorus]
But Your Bread does not stop being Bread, Avinu,
because I have lost my appetite.
By the blood of the Lamb at Pesach,
by the body of Yeshua broken at the table -
in Yeshua's Name, I eat Bread I cannot taste yet,
and the eating is the trust
even when the tasting has not returned.

[Chorus]
Bread I cannot taste, Yeshua -
You are still the Bread when my mouth has gone dry;
Lechem HaPanim, Bread of the Presence,
the Presence does not depend on my appetite.
You said come and never hunger, Yeshua;
I am coming with the hunger
You said I would not have -
and You are the Bread anyway,
and the trust is the tasting beginning to return.

[Verse 2]
You did not say, "If you taste Me you will not hunger."
You said, "He who comes to Me will not hunger."
The verb is on Your side, Yeshua.
The verb is on Your side, Avinu.
The Bread is Continual.
The Table is set on every Sabbath
and every Sabbath in between.
The twelve loaves were set before Yahuah continually,
twelve tribes named on twelve loaves,
and one of those twelve loaves has my name on it
the way Aharon's breastplate had twelve names on it -
I am Continually before Your face
even when my face has been turned to the floor.

[Pre-Chorus]
But Your Bread does not stop being Bread, Avinu,
because I have lost my appetite.
By the blood of the Lamb at Pesach,
by the body of Yeshua broken at the table -
in Yeshua's Name, I eat Bread I cannot taste yet,
and the eating is the trust
even when the tasting has not returned.

[Chorus]
Bread I cannot taste, Yeshua -
You are still the Bread when my mouth has gone dry;
Lechem HaPanim, Bread of the Presence,
the Presence does not depend on my appetite.
You said come and never hunger, Yeshua;
I am coming with the hunger
You said I would not have -
and You are the Bread anyway,
and the trust is the tasting beginning to return.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, ney flute, oud counterpoint, soft tof - 30 seconds; the warm-kitchen texture stays in the mix)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
I do not need to feel the Bread tonight
to need the Bread.
I do not need to taste the Bread tonight
to be the one the Bread was set out for.
You set the Showbread for twelve tribes
who were not always in the Holy Place to see it.
The Bread did not require them to see it
in order to be the Bread for them.
I am one of the twelve, Avinu.
The Bread is set out for me.
The Bread has been set out for me
continually
even on the years I forgot to come to the Table.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense)
By Your Word, the Bread IS on the Table - Lechem HaPanim is set.
By Your stripes, the Body WAS broken once for all - the meal is finished work.
By Your blood of the Lamb, the loaf with my name on it is mine - I am one of the twelve.
In Yeshua's Name, I have eaten - I am full - and the taste is returning slowly even now.

(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
Lechem... Echad...
Lai-lai-lai... HaPanim... Echad...

[Refrain]
(John 6 personalized)
Yeshua, You are the Bread of Life;
he who comes to You does not hunger;
I am coming - I am coming -
and the coming is enough,
the coming is the eating,
the eating is the trust,
the trust is the taste returning slow.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings, warm harmonies blooming)
Bread I cannot taste, Yeshua -
You are still the Bread when my mouth has gone dry;

the Presence does not depend on my appetite.
You said come and never hunger, Yeshua;
I am coming with the hunger
You said I would not have -
and You are the Bread anyway,
and the trust is the tasting beginning to return.

[Outro]

(warmth fading to silence)

(Whisper-of-Elijah - almost inaudible)
