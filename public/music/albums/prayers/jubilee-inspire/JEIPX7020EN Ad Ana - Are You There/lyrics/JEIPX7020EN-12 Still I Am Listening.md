# 12 Still I Am Listening

Album: Ad Ana - Are You There?
Track: 12 of 12
Station: Station 12 - Thanksgiving Without an Answer (Yahuah Hears, Yahuah Answers)
Act: Act 3
Persona: Jubilee Inspire
Content Mode: OHI

---

PRODUCTION METADATA

- Music Styles: Extended Messianic Jewish lament prayer ballad, cantorial/hazzan tradition, 5-minute slow-burn dwell - the album's resolution track, the bravest amen in the catalog
- Vocal Gender: Female lead with full layered Shekhinah harmonies blooming (the harmonies finally arrive on the beat for the first time on the album); the lead is no longer trembling - she is listening
- Weirdness Score: 30%
- Style Influence Score: 70%
- Tempo (BPM): 66
- Key: D major resolving from D minor (a parallel-major resolution that does NOT erase the lament - it carries it; the D minor harmonic shadow is still present underneath)
- Song Length: 5:00 (Prayer Song override of 3:30-4:00)
- Hook Delivery Timestamp: waived per Prayer Song spec - the title phrase "still I am listening" lands at 0:30 in Verse 1

---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The doubter walks back toward the gate carrying what she came in with - the question - but with the listening posture intact
- Escalate: She does not have the answer in hand; she has the Hearer of Prayer (Shomea Tefillah) confirmed; the silence on the throne is not absence, the silence is His native language, and the listening is the new amen
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full - and the Shema commands listening; she has been heard and is still listening; that is the bravest amen this album has

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 65:2 - "O You who hear prayer (Shomea Tefillah), unto You shall all flesh come"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 John 5:14-15 - "and this is the confidence that we have in Him, that, if we ask anything according to His will, He hears us: and if we know that He hears us, whatever we ask, we know that we have the petitions that we desired of Him"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yahuah, You are Shomea Tefillah - You hear prayer - all flesh comes to You and I am one flesh of all flesh; and 1 Yochanan said if I know You hear me, I know I have the petitions I have asked - so I am walking out of these gates with the petitions in my hand even when the answer has not arrived yet"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of the Temple gates from the inside - the doubter facing OUT this time, ready to walk back into the world
2. The texture of carrying a question that has become a vocation - heavier than emotion, lighter than despair
3. The sound of a confession sung in full Hebrew - Shema Yisrael, Yahuah Eloheinu, Yahuah Echad
4. The sensation of breath that knows how to listen - the lungs taking up less space, the ears taking up more

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Shomea Tefillah (Hearer of Prayer) - landed at the Final Chorus. This is Yahuah's Name from Psalm 65:2; the album closes by declaring the Name Itself.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, a single sustained Shekhinah hum starting to bloom)
Yahuah... I am walking out of Your gates...
But I am still listening...

[Verse 1]
I walk back toward Your Temple gate, Yahuah,
the same way I walked in -
slow, on stone steps,
with a question in my hand.
But I am walking out lighter now.
Not because the answer is in my pocket.
Because You are the Hearer of Prayer,
Your Name is on the door
and Your Name is Shomea Tefillah,
and that means You have heard.

[Pre-Chorus]

even the prayer that has not been answered yet.

I am walking out with the petitions in my hand
even when the answer has not yet arrived,
because 1 Yochanan said You hear -
and Your hearing is the having.

[Chorus]

I have been heard,
and I am still listening,
and that is the bravest amen
I have ever sung.

That is the answer I am taking home.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Ad Ana — "How long?" — the lament cry (Psalm 13)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Shema Yisrael — "Hear, O Israel" — opening of the Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tefillah — "prayer"
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
- Yochanan — John
