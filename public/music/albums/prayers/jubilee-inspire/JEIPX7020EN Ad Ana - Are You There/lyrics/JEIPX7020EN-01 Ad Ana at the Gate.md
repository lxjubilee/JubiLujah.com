---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A doubting one walks up the long road to the Temple gate not laughing, not leaping - asking "Ad ana?" the whole way, half-expecting the gate to be shut against her question
- Escalate: The gate is open. The Name on the door has not changed. The doubter is not turned away for bringing a question instead of a song - she is invited in with it
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge - the doubter confesses Echad while crossing the threshold; the answer has not arrived but the gate has opened, and that is enough to keep walking

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 13:1-2 - "Ad ana, Yahuah? Will You forget me forever? Ad ana will You hide Your face from me? Ad ana shall I take counsel in my soul, having sorrow in my heart daily?"
- Brit Hadashah: Hebrews 4:16 - "let us therefore come boldly to the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy and find grace to help in time of need"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Ad ana, Yahuah - I am coming boldly even when bold does not feel like brave - I am coming with the question still in my hand because You said the throne is a throne of grace and grace is what I am out of"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of footsteps that have walked the same road for too many nights
2. The smell of olive-oil lamps still burning at the threshold even at this hour
3. The texture of stone steps worn smooth by every doubter who came before me
4. The weight of a question held in the chest like a stone that has not been laid down yet

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Ad Ana - "How long" - landed on the chorus opening line, sustained on a long minor-third descending note. The Hebrew IS the prayer at that moment. Psalm 13's first words become the album's first words.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Ad Ana — "How long?" — the lament cry (Psalm 13)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
