SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The doubter approaches the Bronze Laver having lost her vocabulary for Yahuah - the words she used to pray no longer fit her mouth
- Escalate: She does not need new words yet; she needs the old words washed - the same prayers she used to pray, returned to her clean, fitting her doubt-shaped mouth
- Elevate: Mayim chayim - living waters that remember Eden - return to her not as new vocabulary but as the old vocabulary re-believable

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Ezekiel 36:25-26 - "I will sprinkle clean water upon you, and you shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you. A new heart also will I give you, and a new ruach will I put within you"
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:26 - "that He might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the Word"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "sprinkle the clean water on the words I lost, Yahuah - wash me with the Word until the words that fell out of my mouth come back with the meaning I forgot they had; the washing of water by the Word is washing my mouth right now"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The cool of bronze under the palm of the hand at the basin rim
2. The sound of water poured from a height - the same water from yesterday, somehow new
3. The taste of vocabulary returning to a mouth that has been dry
4. The reflection in the basin - a face that has been doubting and is being recognized anyway

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Mayim Chayim (living waters) - landed at chorus. The water at the laver was not standing water; it was running water - the same water that remembers Eden. Landed on the chorus high note.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, gentle water sample - drip into basin)
The water is still moving, Avinu...
The basin is still here...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your laver, Yahuah,
with a mouth that does not work anymore.
The words I used to pray
fall out of my mouth like coins out of a torn pocket -
they ring on the floor,
and I cannot pick them back up.
I have not lost the faith;
I have lost the words for the faith.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim chayim, living waters,
remember Eden over my mouth.
By the blood of the Lamb that mingled with water on Calvary,
by the washing of water by the Word -
in Yeshua's Name, wash the words I lost, Avinu -
hand them back to me with the meaning still on them
and the doubt cleansed off the consonants.

[Chorus]
Wash the words I lost, Yahuah -
the prayers I used to say
before the silence ate the vocabulary -
mayim chayim, mayim chayim,
return the words to my mouth.
I do not need a new song tonight, Avinu -
I need the old song
to fit my mouth again.

[Verse 2]
You sprinkled clean water on Yechezkel's exile;
You sprinkled clean water on Yochanan's converts at the Jordan;
You sprinkled clean water on the Body of Mashiach
through the washing of the Word -
sprinkle the same water on me, Avinu, tonight.
The mouth that has forgotten how to say "Father"
remembers what "Avinu" means when the water hits it -
remembers right now -
my own mouth is the one being washed
and the word is coming back as I am singing it.

[Pre-Chorus]
Mayim chayim, living waters,
remember Eden over my mouth.
By the blood of the Lamb that mingled with water on Calvary,
by the washing of water by the Word -
in Yeshua's Name, wash the words I lost, Avinu -
hand them back to me with the meaning still on them
and the doubt cleansed off the consonants.

[Chorus]
Wash the words I lost, Yahuah -
the prayers I used to say
before the silence ate the vocabulary -
mayim chayim, mayim chayim,
return the words to my mouth.
I do not need a new song tonight, Avinu -
I need the old song
to fit my mouth again.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, ney flute responsorial, water-flow texture in the mix, soft tof - 30 seconds)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The cabinet of my old prayers is unlocked.
Take down the prayer I prayed at seventeen
when I first found You.
Take down the prayer I prayed at the bedside
when she was sick and You answered.
Wash them - rinse them - hand them back to me.
I am saying them again right now
with the doubt under them
and the trust over them
and the water of the Word in my mouth.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense)
By Your Word, the vocabulary IS returned - the dry mouth is wet.
By Your stripes, the heart of stone IS flesh - the silence is over.
By Your blood of the Lamb that ran with water, I AM clean - filthiness is gone.
In Yeshua's Name, the words I lost have come home - and the meaning came home with them.

(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
Mayim chayim... Echad...
Lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Ezekiel 36 personalized in finished posture)
Sprinkle clean water on me, Yahuah -
You have sprinkled, and I am clean -
You have taken the heart of stone that the silence left in me
and given me a heart of flesh,
You have put Your Ruach within me
and the words come back wet,
the words come back wet.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings, water-flow in the mix lifting)
Wash the words I lost, Yahuah -
the prayers I used to say
before the silence ate the vocabulary -
mayim chayim, mayim chayim,
return the words to my mouth.
I do not need a new song tonight, Avinu -
I need the old song
to fit my mouth again.

[Outro]

(water-flow fading to silence)

(Whisper-of-Elijah - almost inaudible)
"With joy shall you draw water out of the wells of salvation." - Yeshayahu 12:3
