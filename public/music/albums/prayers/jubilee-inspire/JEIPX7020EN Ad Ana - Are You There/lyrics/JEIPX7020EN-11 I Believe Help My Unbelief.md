---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The doubter brings her own unbelief before Yeshua - not as something to hide, as the thing to hand Him
- Escalate: Mark 9:24 - the truest sentence in the New Testament - sung in second person back to the Lord who first heard it; both the belief AND the unbelief offered
- Elevate: The Personal Insertion Beat - she stops the song and inserts her specific unbelief; the doubt is named, brought, received; the doubt is not the wall, the doubt is what He came for

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 13:1-2 - "Ad ana, Yahuah? Will You forget me forever? Ad ana will You hide Your face from me? Ad ana shall I take counsel in my soul, having sorrow in my heart daily?" (ALBUM ANCHOR 1)
- Brit Hadashah: Mark 9:24 - "and straightway the father of the child cried out, and said with tears, Lord, I believe; help my unbelief" (ALBUM ANCHOR 2)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yeshua, I am the father of the demoniac boy tonight - I am saying his sentence out loud back to You - I believe; help my unbelief - the believing and the unbelief are both mine, and You take both, because You took both from him"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The sound of a sentence being said out loud that the speaker has been afraid to say for years
2. The weight of a hand placed over a heart that has been hiding the unbelief
3. The texture of tears that come from honesty, not from grief
4. The visual of Yeshua's face turned toward the speaker - not flinching when the doubt comes out

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Hineni (here I am) - landed at the Hineni verse, which is the song's emotional core. The same word Avraham said, the same word Moshe said, the same word Yeshayahu said. Tonight the doubter says it.

- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
