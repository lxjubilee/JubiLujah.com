SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The doubter stands at the Bronze Altar with the question itself in her arms - heavier than any other sacrifice she has carried
- Escalate: The fire on the altar will not consume the question; the fire consumes the bitterness wrapped around the question
- Elevate: The question survives the burning - and is now an offering instead of a weight; teshuvah without ever pretending the doubt was gone

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 51:17 - "the sacrifices of Elohim are a broken ruach: a broken and a contrite heart, O Elohim, You will not despise"
- Brit Hadashah: 1 John 1:9 - "if we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "the sacrifice You will not despise, Yahuah, is the one I am bringing - a ruach that has been broken by the silence - and You are faithful and just to forgive even the resentment wrapped around the question, faithful to cleanse what I have not been able to confess out loud until tonight"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The heat of the altar against the face - not enough to burn the skin, enough to warm the question
2. The smell of bronze in the morning fire - cold metal that holds yesterday's smoke
3. The weight of laying something heavier than a body on a flat surface that was made for it
4. The sound of the question hissing in the coals - the bitterness burning off in steam

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Teshuvah (turning, returning) - landed at the Pre-Chorus. Not the teshuvah of moral failure but the teshuvah of bringing the broken ruach back to the altar that was made for broken ruachot.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, faint coal-crackle in the mix)
Your altar is still here, Avinu...
Your fire has not gone out...
I come to lay something down...

[Verse 1]
I have been carrying this question
the way Avraham carried Yitzhak up the mountain -
the wood on my back,
the fire in my hand,
and the question, oh Yahuah, the question
walking up the path with me.
I thought I could carry it forever.
I cannot carry it one more night.

[Pre-Chorus]
Teshuvah, Yahuah - turning, returning -
not because the doubt is sin
but because the bitterness is.
You said if I confess, You are faithful and just to cleanse;
by the blood of the Lamb that ran down the altar,
by the body of Yeshua broken on Calvary -
I am laying down the question
where Avraham almost laid down the boy.

[Chorus]
I lay down the question, I lay it down -
Your altar fire will not consume the question,
Your altar fire will consume the bitterness
wrapped around the question.
The question survives the burning;
the question becomes an offering;
I lay down the question, Avinu,
and walk back down the hill
lighter.

[Verse 2]
I am bringing You every "why" I have hissed
into a pillow, into a kitchen sink,
into the dark above my bed at three in the morning.
I am bringing You every "ad ana" I held
in my chest because I was ashamed to say it out loud.
The altar does not flinch.
The fire does not refuse the offering.
The smoke is already rising
from the part of me I never wanted You to see,
and You are faithful to take it.

[Pre-Chorus]
Teshuvah, Yahuah - turning, returning -
not because the doubt is sin
but because the bitterness is.
You said if I confess, You are faithful and just to cleanse;
by the blood of the Lamb that ran down the altar,
by the body of Yeshua broken on Calvary -
I am laying down the question
where Avraham almost laid down the boy.

[Chorus]
I lay down the question, I lay it down -
Your altar fire will not consume the question,
Your altar fire will consume the bitterness
wrapped around the question.
The question survives the burning;
the question becomes an offering;
I lay down the question, Avinu,
and walk back down the hill
lighter.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, mournful and ornamented, oud counterpoint, soft tof heartbeat - 30 seconds; the coals settle in the mix)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The bitterness has a name and I name it inside the fire.
The resentment has a name and I name it inside the fire.
The years of unanswered prayer have a name
and the name is being burned off
while the question itself is being received.
You are not despising the broken ruach;
You are receiving it as worship.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense, rising from the floor)
By Your Word, the turning is received - teshuvah is complete.
By Your stripes, the broken heart is not despised - it is the sacrifice.
By Your blood of the Lamb, the bitterness is consumed - and only the question remains.
In Yeshua's Name, the offering is accepted - question and all - and I am clean.

(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...
(Shema melody contour hummed - Echad note sustained)
Teshuvah... Echad... lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Psalm 51 personalized in second-person address)
A broken ruach, Yahuah - You will not despise mine -
a broken and a contrite heart - You will not refuse mine -
the sacrifice You take is the one I am bringing tonight;
the sacrifice You take is the one
the bitterness was hiding underneath
and the bitterness is gone now.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings, harmonies still hesitant)
Lay down the question, lay it down -
the altar fire will not consume the question,
the altar fire will consume the bitterness
wrapped around the question.
The question will survive the burning;
the question will become an offering;
lay down the question, Avinu,
and let me walk back down the hill
lighter.

[Outro]

(breath returning to silence, coal-crackle fades)

(Whisper-of-Elijah - almost inaudible)
"For You delight not in sacrifice; else would I give it: You take no pleasure in burnt offering. The sacrifices of Elohim are a broken ruach." - Psalm 51:16-17

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Ad Ana — "How long?" — the lament cry (Psalm 13)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Avraham — Abraham, the father of faith
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Calvary — the place of Yeshua's crucifixion — where the veil was torn
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Teshuvah — "turning, returning" — repentance
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
