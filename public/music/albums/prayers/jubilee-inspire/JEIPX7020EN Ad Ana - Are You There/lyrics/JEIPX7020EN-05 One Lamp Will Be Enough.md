- Music Styles: Extended Messianic Jewish lament prayer ballad, cantorial/hazzan tradition, 5-minute slow-burn dwell
- Vocal Gender: Female lead with layered harmonies that light one at a time across the chorus (like lamps being lit)
- Weirdness Score: 32%
- Style Influence Score: 70%
- Tempo (BPM): 62
- Key: A minor (warmer than Track 4; a single lamp in the room)
- Song Length: 5:00 (Prayer Song override of 3:30-4:00)
- Hook Delivery Timestamp: waived per Prayer Song spec - the title phrase "one lamp will be enough" lands at 0:55 in Pre-Chorus

---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The doubter stands before the menorah expecting seven flames of revelation - and cannot see them
- Escalate: She is not asking for all seven; she is asking for one - one lamp is more light than the dark she has been in
- Elevate: One lamp IS the whole menorah to a soul that has been blind; Yahuah does not refuse the sliver; the one lamp is enough to walk to the next station by

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (TWO-WITNESS CONFIRMATION)

- Tanakh: Psalm 119:105 - "Your word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path"
- Brit Hadashah: 2 Corinthians 4:6 - "for Elohim, who commanded the light to shine out of darkness, has shined in our hearts, to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of Yahuah in the face of Yeshua HaMashiach"
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Your word, Yahuah, is a lamp unto my feet tonight - not a floodlight, a lamp - and one lamp is enough to walk one step at a time; the same Elohim who commanded light out of darkness is commanding light into the room behind my chest right now in the face of Yeshua"

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of a single olive-oil lamp wick catching - the slow yellow that becomes a flame
2. The smell of oil being burned slowly in the Holy Place - sweet, unhurried
3. The shape of the menorah's seven branches in the dark - silhouetted, not yet illuminated
4. The texture of a single step on a path that was invisible until the lamp arrived

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Or (light) - landed at the Faith Declaration Bridge. The same word that opened Genesis 1:3 ("y'hi or" - let there be light) is the word she is sung at her own threshold of light.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, cello drone, faint single-wick crackle in the mix)
Avinu... the lamp is not lit yet...
But there is a wick...

[Verse 1]
I come to Your menorah, Yahuah,
expecting seven flames.
I cannot see them.
Aharon could see them.
Yeshayahu could see them.
Tonight I am asking, Avinu,
just for one.
Just one wick to catch.
Just enough light to find the next station by.

[Pre-Chorus]
And You said, Avinu,
that Your Word is a lamp unto my feet -
a lamp, not a floodlight.
A lamp is for one step.
By the blood of the Lamb who is the Light of the world,
by the same Elohim who said let there be light -
in Yeshua's Name, let one lamp be enough tonight,
and one lamp will be the whole menorah.

[Chorus]
One lamp will be enough, Yahuah -
just one - just one will be enough.
The wick is older than the dark;
the oil is older than the doubt;
the Light is older than the silence.
Let one lamp catch tonight, Avinu -
the next step is the only step I am asking for -
and one lamp will be the whole menorah
to a soul that has been blind.

[Verse 2]
I have been asking for the answer
when I needed the next step.
I have been asking for the floodlight
when I needed the candle.
I have been asking for the whole horizon
when I needed the one foot of path
right in front of me.
Forgive me, Avinu, for asking for too much
when You were ready to give me what is enough.
The one lamp does not require me to deserve it -
the one lamp requires me to walk by it.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, ney flute, oud counterpoint, soft tof - 30 seconds; a single-wick-catching sound bookends the instrumental)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
The first step I cannot see
is the only step You are asking me to take.
The next step will have its own lamp.
The next station will have its own light.
I no longer ask for the whole menorah at once -
I ask for one lamp,
and I walk the one step
the one lamp gives me,
and the walking is the believing.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense)
By Your Word, the lamp IS lit - y'hi or - the light is here.
By Your stripes, the blindness IS healed - my eyes are opening.
By Your blood of the Lamb who is the Light of the world, the Light has come into this room.
In Yeshua's Name, one lamp IS enough - I have received it - and I am walking by it.

(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed, Echad sustained)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...

Or... lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Psalm 119:105 personalized)
Your word, Yahuah, is a lamp unto my feet -
a light unto my path -
one verse, one Hebrew word, one promise
is enough light for one step,
and I take the one step
the one verse gives me right now,
and the one step is the believing.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings, harmonies arriving like lamps catching one by one)
One lamp will be enough, Yahuah -
just one - just one will be enough.

the oil is older than the doubt;
the Light is older than the silence.
Let one lamp catch tonight, Avinu -
the next step is the only step I am asking for -
and one lamp will be the whole menorah
to a soul that has been blind.

(single wick continues to flicker, then fades to silence)

(Whisper-of-Elijah - almost inaudible)
"In Him was life; and the life was the light of men. And the light shines in the darkness; and the darkness has not overcome it." - Yochanan (John) 1:4-5
