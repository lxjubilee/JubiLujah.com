---

SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: The parent slips into their own bed beside the sleeping worried spouse, finally the day is over, and finally there is space to bring the parent's own heart - the spouse, the marriage, the parent's own fear - before Yahuah
- Escalate: The Joshua 24:15 declaration in petition voice - "as for me and my house" sung not as commencement but as desperate vow over the very marriage that has gone quiet
- Elevate: The Personal Insertion Beat - the listener inserts the specific worry, the specific name, the specific marriage trouble; the parent's last petition is for the one beside them

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Joshua 24:15 - "as for me and my house, we will serve Yahuah" (Album anchor 1, reprised in petition voice)
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:25 - "husbands, love your wives, even as Mashiach also loved the assembly, and gave Himself for it"; with 1 Peter 3:7 (heirs together of the grace of life)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said as for me and my house we will serve You, Yahuah - and the worry on the pillow beside me is part of my house; I bring this marriage back to You like Mashiach gave Himself for the assembly, and I am still here"

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The exact weight of a head on the pillow next to one's own
2. The sound of a partner's breathing six inches from one's ear
3. The texture of cool sheets at 1:13am after a long day
4. The shape of moonlight on a ceiling fan blade that is not turning
