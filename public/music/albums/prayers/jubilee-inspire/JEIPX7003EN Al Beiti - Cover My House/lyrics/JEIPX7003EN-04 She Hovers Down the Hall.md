LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, breath foley, fingerpicked guitar)
Ruach HaKodesh... You are walking with me...
I felt You at the threshold of the first room...

[Verse 1]
I start down the hall, Avinu,
checking covers, closing books,
and I thought I was alone.
But there is breath at my shoulder
that is not my own,
and the hush in this hallway
is heavier than silence -
the hush is Your Presence
who hovered once over deep water
and is hovering now over my children.

[Pre-Chorus]
She hovered over chaos once
and the chaos became a world.
By the blood of the Lamb on this house,
by the body of Yeshua on the cross -
She is permitted to hover here,
and She is hovering.

[Chorus]
She hovers down the hall, Avinu -
Ruach HaKodesh over every sleeping face -
cover my house, every breath, every name,
cover my house, every breath, every name.
She hovered over the deep,
She hovers over my deep,
cover my house, every breath, every name,
in the Ruach who fills.

[Verse 2]
I am bringing a hallway full of fears
no parent should have to name out loud -
the diagnosis still on the table,
the conversation we have not yet had,
the room the prodigal one slept in
before they stopped coming home.
But She keeps hovering.
The Comforter does not check the resume.
Your Ruach does not need a competent parent -
Your Ruach needs an open one
who lets Her into the hallway
the way the deep let Her in at the start.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Al Beiti — "over my house"
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Bereshit — Genesis
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
