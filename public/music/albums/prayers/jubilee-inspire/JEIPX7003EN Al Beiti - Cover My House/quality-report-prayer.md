## Album-Level Assessment

This album walks the full Temple journey cleanly. From the swinging front door at Al Beiti (S1) through the kitchen-sink Bronze Altar (S2), the tub-edge Laver (S3), the dim hallway of Ruach (S4), the nightlight Menorah (S5), the crumb-strewn Shewbread (S6), the warm-dryer Ketoret (S7), the cinematic doorpost-walked-through-me revelation at the inner veil (S8), the foot-of-the-bed Mercy Seat with Gethsemane voice (S9), the Choshen-bearing intercession (S10), the marriage-bed Hineni petition (S11), and out into pre-dawn Shalom (S12) — the listener arrives at the Mercy Seat by Track 9, surrenders, intercedes by name with the listener's own names inserted, petitions for the spouse with the listener's own marriage trouble inserted, and walks back out into morning carrying a covered household. The mood holds: candlelit, after-midnight, mother-singing-over-sleeping-house fireside warmth, with brightness bookending at S1 and S12 exactly as the blueprint specifies.

The strongest stretch is the S8-S9-S10-S11 cinematic-to-personal arc. The doorpost-walked-through-me revelation at S8 is the genuine album hinge — the listener stops asking Yahuah to cover the house and recognizes that the lintel of Pesach is already walking inside their ribcage. S9 carries this immediately into the Mercy Seat with Yeshua's Gethsemane voice personalized over the marriage and the household. S10 places the names on Aharon's Choshen and hands the listener six seconds of silence to speak the names aloud. S11 does the same for the spouse and the parent's own fear. This four-song run is unusually load-bearing for a Prayer Album and lands with full weight.

The weakest stretch is mild and structural rather than theological — Tracks 3 and 7 sit slightly below the rest on Earworm scoring (the laver and incense altar stations are inherently quieter), but both carry full SOP compliance, both anchors, the Hebrew word at peak, the Faith Declaration Bridge, and the manifestation grammar intact. They serve the album body well; they are not single candidates and they do not need to be.

## SOP Compliance Findings

No HIGH-severity findings. No MED-severity findings. Two LOW-severity observations recorded for the record, neither blocking and neither requiring revision:

- LOW (informational, T02): The Psalm 51 refrain uses "ruach" twice in lowercase ("steadfast ruach," "willing ruach") as the human-spirit register. This is consistent with the Psalm 51 source text and not a misuse of the Hebrew theological vocabulary — Ruach HaKodesh in feminine pronouns is preserved elsewhere. No action required.
- LOW (informational, T08): The line "the flesh that veiled Him for nine months veils Him in me now" in the personalized Hebrews 10 refrain is a Marian / incarnational pivot. It is scripturally defensible (Hebrews 10:20 calls His flesh the veil; Galatians 2:20 carries the "Mashiach in me" indwelling) but it is unusual phrasing. Reviewer signs off — the line lands at the cinematic-revelation station and is consistent with the album's interior-doorpost theology. No action required.

Compliance verified across all 12 songs:
- Hebrew article rule honored throughout (no "the Ruach HaKodesh"; HaMashiach used correctly without article-doubling)
- No real-artist name-drops in any lyric or any Style Prompt
- No conditional-voice petition closers; Gethsemane voice isolated to Station 9 as specified
- No sentimental drift, no prosperity transactional framing, no Hallmark phrasing
- OHI register strict across the album (Yahuah / Yeshua / Ruach HaKodesh with feminine pronouns load-bearing in T04)
- All Style Prompts under 800 characters
- All 14 sections present in every song
