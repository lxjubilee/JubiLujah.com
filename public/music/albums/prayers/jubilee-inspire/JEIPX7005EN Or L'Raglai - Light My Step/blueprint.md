---

ALBUM VISION STATEMENT

This is the one-hour answer to the prayer every believer has prayed in the dark: Yahuah, light my step. Twelve five-minute Temple stations walking from the Outer Court gates to the Mercy Seat with one oil lamp in the hand and one question on the lips — Yahuah, what is the next step. The album does not promise to show the whole road. The album does not pretend to know the destination. The album insists on the one thing Yahuah has ever promised: a Word for the foot, a lamp for the path immediately ahead. By the time the Sealed Amen of Track 12 has decayed into the Whisper-of-Elijah, the listener has not heard an album about guidance — the listener has prayed one, has named the decision aloud in the Personal Insertion Beats, has laid the counterfeit compasses on the bronze altar, and has been given the only light that ever actually lights a step — Yeshua Himself, the Or HaOlam, walking through the chest of the one who asked.

This is not music about decisions. It is decision, prayed. Tefillah b'shir for the one at the fork in the road.

THREE-ACT MAPPING

ACT 1 — Outer Court (Tracks 1-3): Approach. The listener arrives through the gates with the lamp in one hand and an unanswered question in the other, lays down every counterfeit compass on the bronze altar, is washed clean at the laver of every crooked path ever walked. Tone: relieved on Track 1 (the gate is open), surrendered on Track 2 (the maps burn), cleansed on Track 3 (the feet washed for the path ahead).

ACT 2 — Holy Place to Mercy Seat (Tracks 4-9): The veil. The longest, deepest stretch — Ruach HaKodesh as the patient lamplighter who illuminates one tile at a time; the Menorah's seven flames revealing the Word as Lamp; the Shewbread as one-day's-bread-is-one-day's-guidance; prayer-as-incense carrying the James 1:5 ask up; the inner veil torn and the Lamp walked through the chest; the kavod falling on the kneeling decision-maker. Tone: hushed, candlelit, tear-soaked, climactic on Track 9.

ACT 3 — Intercession, Petition, Thanksgiving (Tracks 10-12): The listener intercedes for others standing at their own forks (Personal Insertion Beat), brings their own decision before Him with the James 1:5 promise on the lips (Personal Insertion Beat), then walks back out into the world by the lamp that has been lit. Tone: priestly compassion (10), childlike specificity (11), released brightness (12).

TESTIMONY ANCHOR DOCUMENTATION

Track Number: 10 — "I Carry Their Forks in the Road"
Testimony Source: The lived intercessory practice of the household. The household stands in the gap for the decisions of others constantly — the prodigal child's road, the team member's career fork, the friend's medical decision, the parent's care plan, the ministry partner's discernment moment. The Aharon-with-twelve-stones priestly archetype IS the lived practice. The listener is invited into the same priestly posture.
Sensory details drawn from the real practice:
1. The physical sensation of laying a hand over the chest where the breastplate would sit, naming a single person's fork in the road
2. The specific weight of intercession for someone else's decision — heavier than asking for your own, because their road affects roads you cannot see
3. The six-second silence where the names and the forks are spoken aloud (Personal Insertion Beat) — the moment the listener stops being audience and becomes priest
Universal bridge: every believer is a priest (1 Peter 2:9). The listener does not need a Temple to carry forks-in-the-road — the listener IS the Temple (1 Cor 6:19), and the carrying happens wherever the listener stands. Anyone with a name and a decision belongs on the breastplate of the praying one.
