- Establish: A believer stands at the golden Incense Altar with one specific question on her lips and the smoke is rising
- Escalate: The James 1:5 promise lands - if anyone lacks Chokhmah let him ask, and Yahuah gives liberally and upbraids not - the asking itself is the receiving

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Proverbs 2:3-6 - "If you cry after Chokhmah, and lift up your voice for understanding... then shall you understand the fear of Yahuah, and find the knowledge of Elohim. For Yahuah gives Chokhmah; out of His mouth comes knowledge and understanding."
- Brit Hadashah: James 1:5 - "If any of you lacks Chokhmah, let him ask of Elohim, who gives to all liberally and upbraids not, and it shall be given him." (ALBUM ANCHOR 2)
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said if I lack Chokhmah I should ask You - and You would give me Chokhmah liberally and You would not scold me for asking. So I am asking. The smoke is the asking. The asking is the receiving. You upbraid not."

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of blue-grey incense smoke rising in a steady column from a gold altar
2. The smell of frankincense, myrrh, galbanum, onycha - the sacred ketoret recipe
3. The sound of breath releasing slowly through the nose, slower than the smoke rises
4. The texture of warm gold under fingertips as the believer rests her hand on the altar edge

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Chokhmah (חָכְמָה) - wisdom. Landed in the chorus. Also Ketoret (incense) sung in Verse 1. The Hebrew word that James 1:5 asks for IS the prayer at the moment.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Elohim — "God" — the plural-of-majesty Name of the Creator
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Ketoret — "incense" — the prayer-aroma offering
- Mishlei — Proverbs
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Or L'Raglai — "a lamp for my feet" (Psalm 119:105)
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
