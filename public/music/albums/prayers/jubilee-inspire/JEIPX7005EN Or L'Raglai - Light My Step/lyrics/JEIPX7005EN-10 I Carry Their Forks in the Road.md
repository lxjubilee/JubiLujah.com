SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A believer walks into the Holy Place carrying twelve forks-in-the-road belonging to others - the prodigal's road, the spouse's job offer, the friend's diagnosis decision
- Escalate: The Aharon priestly metaphor lands - twelve stones over the heart became twelve forks under the foot - and the Personal Insertion Beat opens where the listener speaks aloud the names of those at their own forks
- Elevate: The intercessor receives by faith that Yahuah has heard each name, and lights each of their next steps

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Exodus 28:29 - "Aharon shall bear the names of the sons of Yisrael on the breastplate of judgment upon his heart, when he goes in to the Holy Place, for a memorial before Yahuah continually."
- Brit Hadashah: 1 Timothy 2:1-4 - "I exhort therefore, that, first of all, supplications, prayers, intercessions, and giving of thanks, be made for all men... who will have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth."
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said Aharon was to bear the names on his heart before You continually. I am bearing them tonight, Yahuah. Twelve stones over my heart, twelve forks under my foot - and one of them is theirs - and I am carrying it."

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The physical sensation of laying a hand over the chest where the breastplate would sit
2. The visual of twelve gemstones - sardius, topaz, carbuncle, emerald, sapphire, diamond, jacinth, agate, amethyst, beryl, onyx, jasper - each engraved with a different name
3. The specific weight of intercession for someone else's decision - heavier than asking for your own
4. The six-second silence where the names and the forks are spoken aloud (Personal Insertion Beat)

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Choshen (חֹשֶׁן) - breastplate of judgment, the gemstoned plate worn over Aharon's heart. Landed in Verse 2 (the priestly metaphor of carrying twelve names into the Holy Place).
