SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A believer kneels at the bronze laver, lifts each foot, and watches the dust of every wrong turn she ever took rise off her skin and fall into the basin
- Escalate: The water remembers Eden - the path before the wandering began - and the basin's reflection shows the believer's face the way Yahuah saw it before sickness, before sin, before the first detour
- Elevate: Feet washed clean, rising in newness of life, ready for the path that has not yet been lit

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Ezekiel 36:25-27 - "I will sprinkle clean water on you and you will be clean from all your filthiness and from all your idols... and I will put My Ruach within you, and cause you to walk in My statutes, and you shall keep My judgments, and do them."
- Brit Hadashah: Ephesians 5:26 - "that He might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the Word."
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said You would sprinkle clean water and I would be clean - so I am kneeling at the laver, lifting each foot, and watching every wrong turn rise off my skin. The water remembers Eden. Wash the wandering off."

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The cool weight of bronze water cupped in two hands lifted to the soles of the feet
2. The visual of dust rising off skin in slow swirls, golden in lamplight
3. The sound of water running back into the bronze basin, the same sound for centuries
4. The reflection of a face in the laver that looks younger than the face above it

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Mayim Chayim (מַיִם חַיִּים) - living waters. Landed in the chorus. The water IS the prayer at the moment the dust comes off.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Calvary — the place of Yeshua's crucifixion — where the veil was torn
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Eden — the garden where Yahuah walked with humanity in the cool of the day
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Mayim Chayim — "living water" (cf. John 4:10)
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Or L'Raglai — "a lamp for my feet" (Psalm 119:105)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yechezkel — Ezekiel
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
