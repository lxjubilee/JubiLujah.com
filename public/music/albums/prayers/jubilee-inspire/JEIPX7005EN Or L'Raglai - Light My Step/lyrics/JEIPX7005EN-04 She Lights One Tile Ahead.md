SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A believer crosses the first veil into the Holy Place and feels Ruach HaKodesh hovering behind her with one small oil lamp
- Escalate: She does not light the whole hall - She lights one floor-tile at a time, exactly where the foot is about to land, and the believer realizes the patience of the Spirit is the patience of the next step
- Elevate: Trembling intimacy with the patient One who walks behind the believer with the lamp - never two tiles ahead, always one

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Isaiah 30:21 - "Your ears shall hear a Word behind you saying, This is the way, walk in it, when you turn to the right hand and when you turn to the left."
- Brit Hadashah: John 16:13 - "Howbeit when She, the Ruach of truth, is come, She will guide you into all truth: for She shall not speak of Herself; but whatsoever She shall hear, that shall She speak: and She will show you things to come."
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said I would hear a Word behind me saying, this is the way - so I am walking with my ears toward the back of my head, knowing the Ruach is behind me with one lamp, lighting exactly the tile under the foot about to land."

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of a long dark marble hall with one yellow oil-lamp circle on a single tile ahead of the foot
2. The sound of breath behind the shoulder, just at the back of the ear
3. The sensation of a hand at the small of the back, light, patient, steady
4. The texture of cool marble under bare feet, lit only where the foot is about to land

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Ruach (רוּחַ) - Spirit, breath, wind. Landed in the chorus. The Name of the One walking behind the believer with the lamp IS the prayer at that moment.

[Intro]

I cross the first veil, Yahuah,
into Your Holy Place where it is candlelight, not sun.
I come expecting the whole hall to be lit -
expecting to see the end of the corridor
the way I have always wanted to see the end.
But Your Ruach HaKodesh does not light the hall.
She lights one tile ahead of my foot.
She is behind me, Yahuah,
breathing at the back of my ear,
and the lamp in Her hand is small.

light my step, just my step,
in Your courts, Ruach who guides.

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Or L'Raglai — "a lamp for my feet" (Psalm 119:105)
- Ruach — "spirit, breath, wind"
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Ruach Kodesh — alternative form: "the Spirit, Holy"
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- Tehillim — Psalms
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yeshayahu — Isaiah
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
