SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A believer kneels at the Mercy Seat at the exact point where every map she ever owned ends - the cartography of self-direction stops at the edge of the kavod-cloud
- Escalate: Gethsemane voice surfaces as the Yeshua-quoted line - "not My will but Yours" - and the surrender lands; the only thing left in the believer's hand is His Presence going with the surrendered one
- Elevate: Kavod falls like rain that has mass; the body that has been carrying the decision finally has something heavier than the decision pressing it to the floor

---

ANCHOR SCRIPTURES (Two-Witness Confirmation)

- Tanakh: Exodus 33:13-14 - "Now therefore, I pray You, if I have found grace in Your sight, show me Your way, that I may know You, that I may find grace in Your sight... And He said, My Presence shall go with you, and I will give you rest."
- Brit Hadashah: Matthew 26:39 (Gethsemane - YESHUA-QUOTED LINE PRIMARY PLACEMENT) - "O My Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from Me: nevertheless not as I will, but as You will."
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "Yeshua prayed in Gethsemane, not My will but Yours. So I kneel where my map ends and pray it after Him - not my will, Yahuah, but Yours - not my road, but Yours - and Your Presence going with me is the answer I have been asking for the whole time."

---

SENSORY ANCHOR INVENTORY

1. The visual of a paper map ending at a torn edge, then black ground beyond it
2. The weight of kavod falling like rain with mass - heavier than water, heavier than the diagnosis, heavier than the decision
3. The sound of knees hitting marble before the Mercy Seat - hard, final, exact
4. The taste of olive oil on the lips from the High Priest's anointing horn

---

HEBREW WORD AT PEAK

- Kavod (כָּבוֹד) - weighty glory. Landed on the chorus high note. The Hebrew word for the weight of His Presence IS the prayer at the climax.

LYRICS

[Intro]
(whispered, sparse cello drone, sound of paper tearing at the edge of a map)
Yahuah... the map ends here...
Right at the edge of the kavod-cloud...
And I am kneeling...

[Verse 1]
The map ends here, Avinu.
I have followed the cartography I drew, the cartography You taught me,
the cartography of every wise counselor I trusted -
and they all stop at the edge of Your Kavod-cloud.
Your Sh'khinah does not appear on maps.
Your Mercy Seat is not on any chart.
This is where the road I can read ends -
and the road You give me begins -
and the only way to receive it is to kneel.

[Pre-Chorus]
And I remember what Yeshua prayed, Avinu,
in the garden the night before His back was bared.
By the blood of the Lamb who knelt before I did,
by the body of Yeshua bowed in olive oil and sweat -
He prayed not My will but Yours -
and He meant it -
and the cup did not pass -
and Your will was salvation.

[Chorus]
Kneel where the map ends, Avinu -
Your Kavod falls like rain with mass on the kneeler -
I lay it down, let it fall,
I lay it down, let it fall.
Not my will, but Yours,
not my road, but Yours -
light my step, just my step,
in Your Kavod, Yahuah Tzva'ot.

[Verse 2]
I have been carrying this decision so long, Yahuah,
my shoulders are the shape of it.
And tonight Your Kavod falls on me at the Mercy Seat
and Your Kavod is heavier than the decision
and the decision finally slides off
because there is something heavier holding me to the floor.
And what slides off is not the decision itself -
it is my insistence on directing it.
What slides off is my hand on the wheel of my own life.
And the floor of Your Mercy Seat feels like home.

[Pre-Chorus]
And I remember what Yeshua prayed, Avinu,
in the garden the night before His back was bared.

and He meant it -
and the cup did not pass -
and Your will was salvation.

[Chorus]
Kneel where the map ends, Avinu -
Your Kavod falls like rain with mass on the kneeler -
I lay it down, let it fall,
I lay it down, let it fall.
Not my will, but Yours,
not my road, but Yours -
light my step, just my step,
in Your Kavod, Yahuah Tzva'ot.

[Instrumental]
(violin niggun, mournful and ornamented, ney flute response, oud counterpoint - 30 seconds; the weight of kavod sustained underneath)

[Verse 3]
Hineni, Avinu - here I am, undone before You,
every hidden room laid open and true.
Not my will, Yahuah, but Yours.
Not my road, but Yours.
Not my map, but Yours.
Let this cup of choosing pass if it is Your will -
but not my will, Yours.
Show me Your way, the way Moshe asked,
and Your Presence going with me is enough.
You said it would be enough. It is enough.

[Bridge]
(Faith Declaration Bridge - finished-reality, present tense, surrendered)
By Your Word, it is done.
By Your stripes, I am surrendered.
By the blood of the Lamb who knelt at Gethsemane, I have access to surrender.
In Yeshua's Name, I have received Your will as my will.

(Niggun Bridge - wordless lai-lai-lai with Shema melodic fragment hummed)
Lai-lai-lai... lai-lai-lai...

[Refrain]
(Exodus 33:13-14 personalized as Yeshua's Gethsemane fragment echoes)
Show me Your way, Yahuah, that I may know You -
Your Presence shall go with me,
and You shall give me rest -
not my will, but Yours,
not my road, but Yours.

[Final Chorus]
(full ensemble, weeping strings, stacked Shekhinah harmonies, Kavod weight at maximum)
Kneel where the map ends, Avinu -

I lay it down, let it fall,

Not my will, but Yours,
not my road, but Yours -
light my step, just my step,
in Your Kavod, Yahuah Tzva'ot.

(breath returning to silence, Kavod settling)
