SONG-LEVEL EMOTIONAL ARC

- Establish: A believer with an unanswered decision in one hand and a small clay oil lamp in the other arrives at the Temple gates expecting to be turned away because she does not know which road to take next
- Escalate: Joy mixed with awe - the gates open anyway, because the One on the other side is the Light Himself and a small unlit lamp is the only currency the gate requires
- Elevate: The Shema is sung in full in the bridge - the listener confesses Echad while crossing the threshold; the road is still hidden but the Lamp has been received

- Tanakh: Psalm 100:4 - "Enter His gates with thanksgiving, and His courts with praise. Be thankful unto Him, and bless His Name."
- Brit Hadashah: John 8:12 - "I am the Light of the world; he that follows Me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the Light of life."
- Personalized translation used in lyric: "You said Your Name is Light - and You wrote it on the gate I am walking toward in the dark - and the gate knows me because I am carrying an unlit lamp and You are the only One who lights it"

HEBREW DICTIONARY

- Aaronic Blessing — the priestly blessing of Numbers 6:24-26 (You bless me and keep me, You make Your face to shine upon me...)
- Amen — "so be it" — a verb of faith-sealing
- Avinu — "our Father"
- Brit Hadashah — "the New Covenant" — the New Testament
- Echad — "One" — the unity confessed in the Shema
- Hineni — "here I am" — the surrender word of Avraham, Moshe, and Yeshayahu when Yahuah called
- Mishlei — Proverbs
- Niggun — wordless melodic prayer-song (lai-lai-lai)
- Or — "light"
- Or L'Raglai — "a lamp for my feet" (Psalm 119:105)
- Ruach HaKodesh — "the Holy Spirit" (Ha- already means "the" — never write "the Ruach HaKodesh")
- Shekhinah — the dwelling, manifest Presence of Yahuah
- Shema — "Hear" — opening word of the great Hebrew confession (Deuteronomy 6:4)
- Tanakh — the Hebrew Scriptures — Torah, Prophets, and Writings
- the Lamb — Yeshua as the sacrificial Lamb of Yahuah (Revelation; cf. John 1:29)
- Yahuah — the sacred four-letter Name of the Father (often rendered "the LORD" in English translations)
- Yahuah Echad — "Yahuah is One" (the Shema affirmation)
- Yahuah Eloheinu — "Yahuah our God" (from the Shema)
- Yeshua — the Hebrew name of Jesus, meaning "salvation" or "Yahuah saves"
- Yisrael — Israel; the people of Yahuah
