## Album-Level Assessment

This album walks the Temple journey with unusually disciplined craft and lands the listener at the Mercy Seat with sustained reverence. The arrival sequence (T01-T03) is bright-over-awe in exactly the way the Jubilee Inspire prayer-mode persona demands - the gate-knows-my-name framing of T01, the bronze-fire teshuvah of T02, and the laver-remembers-Eden of T03 form an Outer Court approach that is structurally clean. The Holy Place stretch (T04-T07) is the album's spiritual core - Ruach HaKodesh lighting one tile, the Menorah revealing the Word as foot-level lamp, the Shewbread as daily-guidance, the Ketoret rising as the James 1:5 ask - and every one of these stations carries the load-bearing weight expected of an Act 2. The Holy of Holies sequence (T08-T09) delivers the album's cinematic revelation (Yeshua walked Himself into the chest - the Lamp is inside the carrier) and its Mercy Seat surrender (Gethsemane voice properly placed at Station 9 only). The closing sequence (T10-T12) executes the Personal Insertion Beats precisely as the v2.0 manifestation engine requires, recapitulates both album anchors (Psalm 119:105 + James 1:5) at Station 11, and walks the listener back out by lamplight with the Shema sung in full and Shalom climaxing on the high note.

The user-specified mood (One Lamp in the Dark - guidance for the next step, not the whole road) is held tightly across all twelve tracks. The "light my step, just my step" refrain operates as a load-bearing earworm and a theological discipline simultaneously - it refuses the temptation to ask for the whole hall and locks the listener into the one-tile-at-a-time posture the album was built to teach. The strongest stretch is T08-T09 (the cinematic revelation into the Mercy Seat surrender) - this is where the album earns its keep and where the listener's spirit actually breaks. The weakest stretch is the Outer Court turn from T02 into T03, where the Receipt beat in the Faith Declaration Bridge wants a touch more finished-reality muscle and the chorus closers could be sharpened to land the "lay it down" gesture with cleaner finished-reality grammar rather than imperative voice.

The Promise-Name-Receipt grammar is present in every song, the Two-Witness Confirmation is documented per track, the Sealed Amen lands in every outro, and the Whisper-of-Elijah Scripture is chosen with care (not generic) on every track. The blood / Calvary / Lamb reference is explicit in every song. No banned content was found. No real-artist name-drops. The OHI register is strict throughout. The Hebrew article rule is honored - the album consistently writes "Ruach HaKodesh" or "the Ruach Kodesh" and never "the Ruach HaKodesh." This is a publication-ready album.

## SOP Compliance Findings

- T02 Burn the Counterfeit Compass - MED severity - Faith Declaration Bridge Beat 2 ("By Your stripes, I am untaught the wrong way") is theologically true but the receipt verb "untaught" weakens the finished-reality muscle of the Bridge; the Receipt beat reads more as process than as finished reality. Recommended fix: tighten Beat 2 to a direct finished-reality verb (led / re-pathed / re-directed) and let Beat 3 land "I have access without my own maps" with cleaner Promise-Name-Receipt flow. Also: the chorus closer "in the courts of the One who guides" is correct but identical to T03 and T04 closers - distinguish T02's closer to "in the bronze fire of Avinu" or "in the courts of the One who burns the maps" to give T02 a distinctive sonic signature.

- T03 Wash the Wandering Off - MED severity - The Faith Declaration Bridge Beat 2 "By Your stripes, I am cleansed" is doctrinally borderline (Isaiah 53:5 ties stripes to healing, not cleansing) - Beat 2 wants a verb more directly anchored to the laver image (washed / made-clean) or to a stripes-promise actually tied to that anchor. Recommended fix: rewrite Beat 2 as "By Your stripes, I am healed of the wandering" (anchoring the Isaiah promise to the wandering as the sickness) or "By Your blood, I am washed clean" and re-deploy the blood reference to land cleansing. Also: the chorus closer "in the courts of the Living Water" is fine but the song's distinctive Hebrew "Mayim Chayim" could close out as the courts-name to deepen the Hebrew theological anchoring at the chorus.

- Album-level - LOW severity - The phrase "light my step, just my step" is the album's load-bearing earworm refrain and lands in every chorus closer across all 12 songs. This is intentional and theologically locked, BUT the immediate preceding chorus line "in the courts of the [variant]" is the per-song distinguisher. Tracks T02, T03, T04 currently all close on "in the courts of the One who guides" which costs the album a small amount of per-track sonic distinctiveness. Recommended fix during TUNE on T02 and T03: pick a different Hebrew-theological courts-name per track to give the listener a different chord at the chorus end of each song.

- T01 Lamp at the Threshold - LOW severity (verification only) - The Faith Declaration Bridge Beat 2 reads "By Your stripes, I am led" which is the album's signature guidance-substitution for the standard "healed." This is acceptable per master manual VIII (the locked grammar adapts to station emphasis) but is a deviation worth flagging for QA. Recommended action: confirm and document.
